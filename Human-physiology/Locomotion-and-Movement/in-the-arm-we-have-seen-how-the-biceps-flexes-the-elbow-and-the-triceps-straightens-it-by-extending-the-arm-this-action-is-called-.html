In the arm we have seen how the biceps flexes the elbow and the triceps straightens it by extending the arm. This action is called ____

(A) agonist

(B) antagonist

(C) ** antagonistic

(D) prime mover

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Examples of antagonistic pairs are: ⚫ Biceps and triceps-at the elbow joint. As the biceps bends or flexes the elbow joint by contracting, the triceps relaxes. As the arm straightens, the opposite occurs.

Concept note-2: -For example, the biceps muscle, in the front of the upper arm, is a flexor, and the triceps, at the back of the upper arm, is an extensor. When you bend at your elbow, the biceps contracts. Then the biceps relaxes and the triceps contracts to straighten the elbow.

Concept note-3: -When you do a triceps extension, the roles are reversed-your biceps are the antagonists and your triceps are the agonists.

Concept note-4: -During elbow extension, triceps is agonist muscle and biceps is antagonist muscle.
