Which hormone slows down gastric motility and s timulates the pancreas to release lipase and the gallbladder to release bile?

(A) Gastrin

(B) ** CCK

(C) Secretin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cholecystokinin has several functions at this stage of the digestive process: It stimulates your gallbladder to contract and release bile into your small intestine. (“Cholecystokinin” means to “move the gallbladder.") It stimulates your pancreas to release pancreatic enzymes.

Concept note-2: -Glucagon retards gastric emptying of liquids and inhibits motility throughout the gastrointestinal tract [63].

Concept note-3: -These results demonstrate that CCK-8 decreases gastric motility in the gastric corpus and delays gastric emptying by a capsaicin-sensitive vagal afferent pathway. These same afferent fibers may also play a physiological role in the gastric emptying of liquids.

Concept note-4: -The physiological actions of CCK include stimulation of pancreatic secretion and gallbladder contraction, regulation of gastric emptying, and induction of satiety. Therefore, in a highly coordinated manner, CCK regulates the ingestion, digestion, and absorption of nutrients.

Concept note-5: -Cholecystokinin (CCK) is well-known as a key hormone that inhibits stomach emptying and stimulates midgut motility in gastric species.
