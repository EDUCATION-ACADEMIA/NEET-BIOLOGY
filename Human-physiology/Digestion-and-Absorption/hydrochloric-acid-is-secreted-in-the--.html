Hydrochloric acid is secreted in the ____ .

(A) oesophagus

(B) ** stomach

(C) large intestine

(D) small intestine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is secreted by the oxyntic cells of the gastric gland in the stomach. Gastric juice contains mucous, hydrochloric acid and certain enzymes such as pepsin.

Concept note-2: -Often simplistically described as hydrochloric acid, these species are produced by parietal cells in the gastric glands in the stomach. The secretion is a complex and relatively energetically expensive process.

Concept note-3: -Parietal cells in the mucosa, the inner cell layer of our digestive tract, secrete hydrochloric acid (HCl) into the stomachâ€™s lumen, or cavity.

Concept note-4: -Acid secretion is initiated by food: the thought, smell, or taste of food effects vagal stimulation of the gastrin-secreting G cells located in the distal one third (antrum) of the stomach. The arrival of protein to the stomach further stimulates gastrin output.
