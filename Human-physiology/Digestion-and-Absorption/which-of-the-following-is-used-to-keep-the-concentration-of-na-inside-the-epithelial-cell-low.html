Which of the following is used to keep the concentration of Na+ inside the epithelial cell low?

(A) ** Active transport through the sodium-potassium pump

(B) Secondary active transport through the Na+/glucose co-transporter

(C) Facilitated diffusion through Na+ channels

(D) Simple diffusion through the cell membrane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -[3][4] The Na+K+-ATPase pump helps to maintain osmotic equilibrium and membrane potential in cells. The sodium and potassium move against the concentration gradients. The Na+ K+-ATPase pump maintains the gradient of a higher concentration of sodium extracellularly and a higher level of potassium intracellularly.

Concept note-2: -The sodium-potassium pump system moves sodium and potassium ions against large concentration gradients. It moves two potassium ions into the cell where potassium levels are high, and pumps three sodium ions out of the cell and into the extracellular fluid.

Concept note-3: -The sodium-potassium pump carries out a form of active transport-that is, its pumping of ions against their gradients requires the addition of energy from an outside source.

Concept note-4: -The sodium-potassium pump is an active transport pump that exchanges sodium ions for potassium ions.
