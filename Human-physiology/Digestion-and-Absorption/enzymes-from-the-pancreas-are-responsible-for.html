DIGESTION AND ABSORPTION

[SOURCES]
Enzymes from the pancreas are responsible for

(A) Digesting most macromolecules completely into their monomer units

(B) Digesting starch and triglycerides only

(C) ** Digesting starch, triglycerides, phospholipids, and proteins into smaller units

(D) Passing from the small intestine to the the large intestine to digest food later

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The pancreas produces amylase, which breaks down alpha glycosidic linkages found in starch and glycogen. The pancreas also produces lipase, which breaks down the triglycerides into fatty acids and glycerol.

Concept note-2: -During digestion, your pancreas makes pancreatic juices called enzymes. These enzymes break down sugars, fats, and starches. Your pancreas also helps your digestive system by making hormones.

Concept note-3: -The pancreatic enzyme that is involved in the digestion of protein is trypsin. Trypsin acts on complex protein molecules and converts those into smaller peptides.

Concept note-4: -proteolytic enzyme, also called protease, proteinase, or peptidase, any of a group of enzymes that break the long chainlike molecules of proteins into shorter fragments (peptides) and eventually into their components, amino acids.
