What is involved in controlling the volume and composition of gastric juice?I. nervous systemII. hormonal systemIII. medulla oblangata

(A) I and II only

(B) I and III only

(C) II and III only

(D) ** I, II and III

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gastric acid secretion is under nervous and hormonal control. Gastrin, the major circulating stimulus of acid secretion, probably does not stimulate the parietal cells directly but acts to mobilize histamine from the ECL cells in the oxyntic mucosa. Histamine stimulates the parietal cells to secrete HCl.

Concept note-2: -The medulla oblongata controls gastric secretion both by the parasympathetic branch of the nervous system and the endocrine system.

Concept note-3: -During meal ingestion, the main hormone responsible for stimulating acid secretion is gastrin, which acts primarily by releasing histamine from enterochromaffin-like cells.

Concept note-4: -The process is controlled by a complex system of endocrine cells and neurons. Gastrin is the main hormone regulating gastric acid secretion, and is phylogenetically older than gastric acidity [8].
