HUMAN PHYSIOLOGY

DIGESTION AND ABSORPTION

[SOURCES]
Villi

(A) Stores bile

(B) Produces enzymes that flow into the small intestine

(C) Structure in which waste material is compressed into solid form

(D) ** Tiny finger-shaped structures in the small intestine in which absorption occurs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The lining of the small intestinal mucosa is very highly specialized for maximizing digestion and absorption of nutrients. The lining is highly folded to form microscopic finger-like projections called villi which increase the surface area to help with absorption.

Concept note-2: -Finger-shaped structures called villi line the entire small intestine. They help absorb nutrients.

Concept note-3: -Villi That Line the Walls of the Small Intestine Absorb Nutrients. Villi that line the walls of the small intestine absorb nutrients into capillaries of the circulatory system and lacteals of the lymphatic system.

Concept note-4: -The jejunum is the middle part of the small intestine, between the duodenum and ileum. Most digestion and nutrient absorption takes place in the jejunum.

Concept note-5: -Villi are specialized for absorption in the small intestine as they have a thin wall, one cell thick, which enables a shorter diffusion path. They have a large surface area so there will be more efficient absorption of fatty acids and glycerol into the blood stream.
