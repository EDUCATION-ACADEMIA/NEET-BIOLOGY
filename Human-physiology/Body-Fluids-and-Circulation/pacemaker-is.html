Pacemaker is

(A) Instrument for measuring heart beat

(B) Instrument for measuring pulse rate

(C) Auriculo-ventricular node that provides impulse for heart beat

(D) ** Sinu-aurical node that provides impulse for heart beat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The sinus node continuously generates electrical impulses, thereby setting the normal rhythm and rate in a healthy heart. Hence, the SA node is referred to as the natural pacemaker of the heart.

Concept note-2: -The SA (sinoatrial) node generates an electrical signal that causes the upper heart chambers (atria) to contract. The signal then passes through the AV (atrioventricular) node to the lower heart chambers (ventricles), causing them to contract, or pump. The SA node is considered the pacemaker of the heart.

Concept note-3: -The sinus node, the natural pacemaker of the heart, is located in the heart’s upper chamber, called the atrium. It is responsible for initiating the electrical impulse that travels through the heart, causing it to beat in a regular rhythm and heart rate.

Concept note-4: -The heart is made up of four chambers-two upper (atria) and two lower (ventricles). The rhythm of the heart is typically controlled by the sinus node, an area of specialized cells in the right upper heart chamber (right atrium). This natural pacemaker produces electrical signals that trigger each heartbeat.

Concept note-5: -The sinus node generates an electrical stimulus regularly, 60 to 100 times per minute under normal conditions. The atria are then activated. The electrical stimulus travels down through the conduction pathways and causes the heart’s ventricles to contract and pump out blood.
