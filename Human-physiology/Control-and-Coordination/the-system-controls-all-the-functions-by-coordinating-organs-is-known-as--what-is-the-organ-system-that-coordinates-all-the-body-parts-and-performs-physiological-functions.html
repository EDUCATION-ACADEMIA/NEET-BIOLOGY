HUMAN PHYSIOLOGY

CONTROL AND COORDINATION

[SOURCES]
The system controls all the functions by coordinating organs is known as ____ What is the organ system that coordinates all the body parts and performs physiological functions?

(A) Circulatory system

(B) Digestive system

(C) ** Nervous system.

(D) All the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The brain is like a computer that controls the body’s functions, and the nervous system is like a network that relays messages to parts of the body.

Concept note-2: -The nervous system is a complex network of nerves and nerve cells (neurons) that carry signals or messages to and from the brain and spinal cord to different parts of the body. It is made up of the central nervous system and the peripheral nervous system.

Concept note-3: -The nervous system includes the brain, spinal cord, and a complex network of nerves. This system sends messages back and forth between the brain and the body. The brain is what controls all the body’s functions. The spinal cord runs from the brain down through the back.

Concept note-4: -The brain and the spinal cord make up the central nervous system. The nervous system is the most complex and highly organized body system. It receives information from the sensory organs via nerves, transmits the information through the spinal cord, and processes it in the brain.

Concept note-5: -The brain, spinal cord and the nerves are part of the nervous system. The nervous system controls all the functions of the human body.
