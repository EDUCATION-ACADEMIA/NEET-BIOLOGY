Which nerves arise from the brain and emerges through the skull

(A) Spinal nerves

(B) Thoracic nerves

(C) ** Cranial nerves

(D) Lumbar nerves

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cranial nerves arise directly from the brain in contrast to spinal nerves and exit through its foramina. Most of the cranial nerves originate in the brain stem and pass through the muscles and sense organs of the head and neck.

Concept note-2: -Cranial nerves are the nerves that emerge directly from the brain (including the brainstem), of which there are conventionally considered twelve pairs.

Concept note-3: -Cranial nerves are the nerves that emerge directly from the brain (including the brainstem), in contrast to spinal nerves (which emerge from segments of the spinal cord). Cranial nerves relay information between the brain and parts of the body, primarily to and from regions of the head and neck.

Concept note-4: -Among them, the nerves which arise from brain are called cranial nerves.

Concept note-5: -The Vestibulocochlear nerve (cranial nerve VIII) – Arises from the brainstem directly lateral to the sensory root of the facial nerve. Cranial nerve VIII has two distinct portions, the vestibular, responsible for balance, and the cochlear, which is responsible for hearing.
