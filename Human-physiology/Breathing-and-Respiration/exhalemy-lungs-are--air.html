HUMAN PHYSIOLOGY
BREATHING AND RESPIRATION [SOURCES]

EXHALE!!!My lungs are ____ air.

(A) Taking in

(B) ** Letting out

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Upon inhalation, the diaphragm contracts and flattens and the chest cavity enlarges. This contraction creates a vacuum, which pulls air into the lungs. Upon exhalation, the diaphragm relaxes and returns to its domelike shape, and air is forced out of the lungs.

Concept note-2: -In exhalation, the diaphragm moves upward and the chest wall muscles relax, causing the chest cavity to get smaller and push air out of respiratory system through the nose or mouth. Every few seconds, with each inhalation, air fills a large portion of the millions of alveoli.

Concept note-3: -Expiration (exhalation) is the process of letting air out of the lungs during the breathing cycle. During expiration, the relaxation of the diaphragm and elastic recoil of tissue decreases the thoracic volume and increases the intraalveolar pressure. Expiration pushes air out of the lungs.

Concept note-4: -Weakness, confusion. Sleep disturbances. Numbness and tingling in your arms or around your mouth. Muscle spasms in hands and feet, chest pain and palpitations.

Concept note-5: -When you inhale (breathe in), air enters your lungs, and oxygen from that air moves to your blood. At the same time, carbon dioxide, a waste gas, moves from your blood to the lungs and is exhaled (breathed out).
