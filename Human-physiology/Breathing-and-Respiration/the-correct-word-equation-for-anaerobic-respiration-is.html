The correct word equation for Anaerobic respiration is

(A) glucose → lactic acid + oxygen

(B) ** glucose → lactic acid (+ energy released)

(C) glucose + oxygen → lactic acid (+ energy released)

(D) lactic acid → energy released

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Word equation for anaerobic respiration: Glucose → ethanol + carbon dioxide + energy (2 ATP)

Concept note-2: -Anaerobic respiration takes place in the cell cytoplasm and produces lactic acid. The chemical equation is C6H12O6-> 2C3H6O3 (Glucose-> Lactic acid).

Concept note-3: -So, the correct option is ‘Respiration’.

Concept note-4: -Aerobic respiration is the aerobic catabolism of nutrients to carbon dioxide, water, and energy, and involves an electron transport system in which molecular oxygen is the final electron acceptor. The overall reaction is: C6H12O6 + 6O2 yields 6CO2 + 6H2O + energy (as ATP).

Concept note-5: -C6H12O6 + 6O2 → 6CO2 + 6H2O (glucose + oxygen-> carbon dioxide + water).
