BREATHING AND RESPIRATION

[SOURCES]
Which of the following correctly describe respiration

(A) Chemcially breakdown of food to provide energy for the body cells

(B) Physical process of taking in air and removing it from the body

(C) Physical action of the moving of the lungs cavity

(D) ** Release of energy from glucose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During aerobic cellular respiration, glucose reacts with oxygen, forming ATP that can be used by the cell. Carbon dioxide and water are created as byproducts. The overall equation for aerobic cellular respiration is: In cellular respiration, glucose and oxygen react to form ATP.

Concept note-2: -Glycolysis is a series of reactions that extract energy from glucose by splitting it into two three-carbon molecules called pyruvates.

Concept note-3: -Thus the correct order of the stages in cellular respiration is: Glycolysis-Oxidative decarboxylation-Krebs cycle-Electron transport chain-Oxidative phosphorylation.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The correct option is b. Aerobic respiration requires oxygen and produces carbon dioxide and water and also energy. Cellular respiration occurs in aerobic respiration.
