HUMAN PHYSIOLOGY

BREATHING AND RESPIRATION

[SOURCES]
Breathing is ____

(A) Breaking down food

(B) Making noise

(C) ** Inhaling and Exhaling Air

(D) Producing energy from food

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When you inhale (breathe in), air enters your lungs, and oxygen from that air moves to your blood. At the same time, carbon dioxide, a waste gas, moves from your blood to the lungs and is exhaled (breathed out). This process, called gas exchange, is essential to life.

Concept note-2: -The lungs and respiratory system allow us to breathe. They bring oxygen into our bodies (called inspiration, or inhalation) and send carbon dioxide out (called expiration, or exhalation). This exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide is called respiration.

Concept note-3: -The process of breathing, or respiration, is divided into two distinct phases. The first phase is called inspiration, or inhaling. When the lungs inhale, the diaphragm contracts and pulls downward. At the same time, the muscles between the ribs contract and pull upward.

Concept note-4: -Diaphragmatic breathing requires contraction of the diaphragm and is also called deep breathing. Costal breathing requires contraction of the intercostal muscles and is also called shallow breathing.

Concept note-5: -This model assumes that the respiratory cycle consists of three phases: inspiration, post-inspiration, and expiration.
