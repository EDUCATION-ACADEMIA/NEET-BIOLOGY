Which statement(s) are accurate about pyruvate?

(A) It is the oxidized form of glucose and contains little energy

(B) ** It is the oxidized form of glucose, yet contains high energy

(C) If pyruvate is formed in an anoxic environment, cellular respiration will occur in the mitochondria only

(D) ** If muscles are O2 deficient, they will use pyruvate for fermentation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: The correct statements are Pyruvate can be converted into ethanol and carbon dioxide by yeast and Fermentation is a form of anaerobic respiration . Hence, Option C is correct.

Concept note-2: -When oxygen is not present, pyruvate will undergo a process called fermentation. In the process of fermentation the NADH + H+ from glycolysis will be recycled back to NAD+ so that glycolysis can continue.

Concept note-3: -In anaerobic conditions, pyruvate converts to lactate through anaerobic glycolysis. Anaerobic respiration results in the production of 2 ATP molecules.

Concept note-4: -In the presence of oxygen, pyruvate is converted to acetyl-CoA and then enters the citric acid cycle. More ATP can be formed from the breakdown of glucose when oxygen is present.
