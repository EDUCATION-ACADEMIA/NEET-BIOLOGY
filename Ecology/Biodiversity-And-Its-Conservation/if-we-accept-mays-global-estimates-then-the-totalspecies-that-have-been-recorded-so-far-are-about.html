If we accept May’s global estimates. then the totalspecies that have been recorded so far are about

(A) 70%

(B) ** 22%

(C) 68%

(D) 8.1%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If we accept May’s global estimates, only 22 per cent of the total species have been recorded so far. Applying this proportion to India’s diversity figures, we estimate that there are probably more than 1, 00, 000 plant species and more than 3, 00, 000 animal species yet to be discovered and described.

Concept note-2: -As per R. May, global species diversity is about 7 million. While other scientists believe it would range from 20-50 million. Currently known and described species range between 1.7-1.8 million.

Concept note-3: -Considering May’s discovery proposal there are more than 1, 00, 000 species of flora and 3, 00, 000 species of fauna are yet to be recognized and described. So, the correct answer is option D. There are nearly 1, 00, 000 floral species and 3, 00, 000 fauna species are not yet discovered.

Concept note-4: -Assertion (A): According to Robert May’s estimate, only about 22% of the total number of existing species have been recorded so far. Reason (R): More than 1, 00, 000 plant species are yet to be discovered in India.

Concept note-5: -Remaining 78% of species are yet to be discovered and named. In India, if May’s estimates are applied then, there are probably more than 1, 00, 000 plant species and more than 3, 00, 000 animal species yet to be discovered and described. Q.
