BIODIVERSITY AND ITS CONSERVATION

[SOURCES]
According to the principles of island biogeography, what factors influence species richness? I. Increased proximity to the mainland II.Increased island size III.Increased amount of edge habitat

(A) I only

(B) II only

(C) II and III

(D) ** I and II

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The theory of island biogeography outlines three factors that impact species diversity on an island. These are extinction, immigration, and emigration.

Concept note-2: -Island biogeography is determined by three processes: immigration, evolution, and extinction. These processes are determined by the area and isolation of islands such that smaller and more isolated islands have lower numbers of species than larger and less isolated islands.

Concept note-3: -The availability of resources, such as food, water, and shelter, is an evolutionary factor that influences species richness in a community. Climate, natural disasters, and competition between species can also play a role in determining species richness.

Concept note-4: -Species richness increases in response to increasing tide range, increasing wave energy, decreasing sand particle size and in flatter and wider beaches. This means that macrofaunal species richness increases as beaches become more dissipative (McLachlan and Dorvlo, 2005).
