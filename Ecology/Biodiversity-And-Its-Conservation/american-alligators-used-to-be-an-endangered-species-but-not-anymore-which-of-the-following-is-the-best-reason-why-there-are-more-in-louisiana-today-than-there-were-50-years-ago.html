American alligators used to be an endangered species, but not anymore. Which of the following is the best reason why there are more in Louisiana today than there were 50 years ago?

(A) they have learned how to live in new places

(B) they are bigger now than they were 50 years ago

(C) ** they are now protected from hunting by humans

(D) they have much more habitat now than 50 years ago

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -State and federal protections, habitat preservation efforts, and reduced demand for alligator products have improved the speciesâ€™ wild population to more than one million and growing today.

Concept note-2: -Habitat loss is the primary cause of higher extinction rates. Other causes include habitat changes, over-exploitation of wildlife for commercial purposes, the introduction of harmful nonnative species, pollution, and the spread of diseases.

Concept note-3: -Habitat loss is the leading known cause of population decline.

Concept note-4: -Keystone species have low functional redundancy. This means that if the species were to disappear from the ecosystem, no other species would be able to fill its ecological niche. The ecosystem would be forced to radically change, allowing new and possibly invasive species to populate the habitat.

Concept note-5: -Genes, or Genetic Diversity Genetic diversity is arguably the most important level of biodiversity to conserve, because it is the most fundamental.
