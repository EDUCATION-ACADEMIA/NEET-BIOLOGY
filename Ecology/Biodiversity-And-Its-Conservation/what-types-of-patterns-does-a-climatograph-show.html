What types of patterns does a climatograph show?

(A) rainfall and snowfall

(B) precipitation and humidity

(C) ** precipitation and temperature

(D) temperature and sunlight

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Climographs show monthly average temperatures and precipitation totals on a single graph. Rather than showing measured data for specific time periods-like the graphs you generated in Part A-climographs show long term averages for all 12 months of the year.

Concept note-2: -Climate is defined as an areaâ€™s long-term weather patterns. The simplest way to describe climate is to look at average temperature and precipitation over time.

Concept note-3: -Rainfall Patterns. Precipitation-rain, snow, sleet and hail-is associated with areas of rising air and low pressure. When air rises it cools, and the moisture it contains condenses out as clouds, which eventually produce precipitation.

Concept note-4: -Climate graphs show the average temperature and rainfall, by month, for a location. The temperature is always shown as a red line graph. The rainfall is always shown as a blue bar chart.
