ECOLOGY

ORGANISMS AND THE ENVIRONMENT

[SOURCES]
A North American grassland is usually more productive when it contains diverse plant communities.

(A) ** The biodiversity allows some plant communities to survive when the ecosystem experiences slightly different weather patterns.

(B) The biodiversity allows all plant communities to survive when the ecosystem experiences an ice age.

(C) The biodiversity prevents some plant communities from surviving when the ecosystem experiences consistent weather patterns.

(D) The biodiversity prevents all plant communities from surviving when the ecosystem experiences a heat wave.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Greater biodiversity in ecosystems, species, and individuals leads to greater stability. For example, species with high genetic diversity and many populations that are adapted to a wide variety of conditions are more likely to be able to weather disturbances, disease, and climate change.

Concept note-2: -Biodiversity refers to all the different kinds of living organisms within a given area. Biodiversity includes plants, animals, fungi, and other living things. Biodiversity can include everything from towering redwood trees to tiny, single-cell algae that are impossible to see without a microscope.

Concept note-3: -Coral reefs are believed by many to have the highest biodiversity of any ecosystem on the planet-even more than a tropical rainforest. Occupying less than one percent of the ocean floor, coral reefs are home to more than 25% of all marine life. Why is that important?

Concept note-4: -Natural selection can lead to speciation, where one species gives rise to a new and distinctly different species. It is one of the processes that drives evolution and helps to explain the diversity of life on Earth.
