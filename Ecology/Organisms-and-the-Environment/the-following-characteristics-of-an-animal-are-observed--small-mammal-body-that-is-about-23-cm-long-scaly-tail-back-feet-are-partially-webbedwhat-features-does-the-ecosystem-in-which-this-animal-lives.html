(A) Dry, rocky mountains

(B) Forests of pine trees

(C) Tall, spiny cacti

(D) ** Lakes and rivers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What five characteristics do mammals have in common? Mammals have hair or fur; are warm-blooded; most are born alive; the young are fed milk produced by the mother’s mammary glands; and they have a more complex brain than other animals.

Concept note-2: -Describe the observable features of the animal/plant: – for animals: features such as size, body covering, beak, wings, teeth, ears; – for plants: features such as size; shape; leaf shape, size, smell and texture; roots; flowers; fruit.
