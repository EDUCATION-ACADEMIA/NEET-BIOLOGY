Microhabitats are very small parts of the environment that supports a distinct flora (plant) and fauna (animal). Of the following, which one is a microhabitat?

(A) ** Moss growing on top of a rotting log

(B) Aquatic biomes

(C) Deer, Rabbits, and Fox sharing a field

(D) Fish and seahorses swimming around a reef.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Examples of microhabitats on the short grass prairie of the Llano Estacado include temporary pools of water, a decomposing log or animal, cryptobiotic soils, lichen growth, under rocks, leaf litter, termite tubes, and the interior of an ant bed.

Concept note-2: -Forest microhabitats range from beneath the soil to the tops of the trees. Some examples include rotting logs, leaf litter, under rocks and in wet spots and puddles.

Concept note-3: -Small animals such as insects, mice and frogs often make use of micro-habitats when the habitat they are in makes them vulnerable to predators. A rotting log may become home to a number of different insect species, a clump of moss, a cluster of forest fungus as well as a family of frogs.

Concept note-4: -An overturned decomposing log or the underside of a rock in a stream are excellent examples of microhabitats. Both a habitat and a microhabitat have typical abiotic (e.g. water, temperature, light.
