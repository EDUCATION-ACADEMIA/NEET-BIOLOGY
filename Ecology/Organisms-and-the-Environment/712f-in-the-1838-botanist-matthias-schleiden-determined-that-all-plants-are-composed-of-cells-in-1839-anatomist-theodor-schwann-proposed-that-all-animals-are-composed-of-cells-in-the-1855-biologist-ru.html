[SOURCES]
(7.12F) In the 1838 botanist Matthias Schleiden determined that all plants are composed of cells. In 1839 anatomist Theodor Schwann proposed that all animals are composed of cells. In the 1855 biologist Rudolph Virchow added to Scheiden’s and Schwann’s observations and proposed that all living things are composed of cells. Which statement is also part of Virchow’s cell theory?

(A) All cells have a cell wall.

(B) ** All cells arise from pre-existing cells.

(C) All cells are capable of photosynthesis.

(D) All cells can develop into any other type of cell.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1839 anatomist Theodor Schwann proposed that all animals are composed of cells. In 1855 biologist Rudolph Vichow added to Schleiden’s and Schwann’s observations and proposed that all living things are composed of cells. Which statement is also part of Virchow’s cell theory ? All cell have a cell wall.

Concept note-2: -In 1838 Schleiden defined the cell as the basic unit of plant structure, and a year later Schwann defined the cell as the basic unit of animal structure. Schleiden and Schwann articulated their observations as a unified theory-the cell theory-in 1839.

Concept note-3: -TEK 7.12F: Recognize that according to cell theory all organisms are composed of cells and cells carry on similar functions such as extracting energy from food to sustain life. A cell is the basic unit of function and structure in all living organisms.

Concept note-4: -In 1838, Matthias Schleiden, a German botanist, concluded that all plant tissues are composed of cells and that an embryonic plant arose from a single cell. He declared that the cell is the basic building block of all plant matter.

Concept note-5: -In his short article, Schleiden did state that the cell is the fundamental unit of plant structure; i.e., all plants are made of cells.
