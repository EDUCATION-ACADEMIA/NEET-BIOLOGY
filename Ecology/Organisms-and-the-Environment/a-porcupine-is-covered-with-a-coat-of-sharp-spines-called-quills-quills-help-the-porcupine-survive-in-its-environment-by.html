A porcupine is covered with a coat of sharp spines called quills. Quills help the porcupine survive in its environment by:

(A) helping it locate sources of food.

(B) keeping it warm in cold weather.

(C) ** defending the porcupine from enemies.

(D) making it easier for the porcupine to climb trees.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Although porcupines cannot shoot their quills, they can aim their body movements for vital places on the predatorâ€™s body. If the predator is hit, the quills can embed themselves in the animal because the body heat of the predators body causes the barbs to expand.

Concept note-2: -Porcupines have soft hair, but on their back, sides, and tail it is usually mixed with sharp quills. These quills typically lie flat until a porcupine is threatened, then leap to attention as a persuasive deterrent. Porcupines cannot shoot them at predators as once thought, but the quills do detach easily when touched.

Concept note-3: -Functionally, most spines serve as defense from predators, but spines are also important tools for communication, shock absorption, and rain protection.

Concept note-4: -Porcupines are large rodents with coats of sharp spines, or quills, that protect them against predation.
