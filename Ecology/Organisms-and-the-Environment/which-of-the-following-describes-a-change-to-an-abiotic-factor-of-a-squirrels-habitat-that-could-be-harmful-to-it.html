Which of the following describes a change to an abiotic factor of a squirrelâ€™s habitat that could be harmful to it?

(A) increase in the population of food plants

(B) ** drought that depletes available water

(C) deforstation leading to reduced nesting sites

(D) increase in the population of local predators

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. This is because a drought that depleted available water is a change to an abiotic factor. water is non living unlike the population or trees in the other answers. The fact that water is not there can be harmful to the squirrel as well!

Concept note-2: -An abiotic factor is a non-living part of an ecosystem that shapes its environment. In a terrestrial ecosystem, examples might include temperature, light, and water. In a marine ecosystem, abiotic factors would include salinity and ocean currents.

Concept note-3: -Abiotic factors are the nonliving parts of the environment that can often have a major influence on living organisms. Abiotic factors include water, sunlight, oxygen, soil and temperature.

Concept note-4: -Dissolved oxygen is critical for the survival of animals and plants that live in the water. As the water temperature increases, the amount of oxygen that can dissolve in the water decreases.
