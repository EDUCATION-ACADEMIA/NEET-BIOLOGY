You are in an environment that did not receive any rain and you saw the large ears of a jackrabbit hiding behind a rock In which biome are you in?

(A) Tundra

(B) Tropical Rainforest

(C) ** Desert

(D) Taiga

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These jackrabbits live in open grasslands and sagebrush plains, and are sometimes found in coniferous forests and subalpine meadows. They are particularly associated with bunchgrass grasslands. As herbivores, white-tailed jackrabbits help to maintain a healthy ecosystem by preventing the overgrowth of vegetation.

Concept note-2: -Jackrabbits live in desert habitats with hot daytime temperatures. Their ears are an adaptation to deal with heat in the desert. The large thin ears have a network of blood vessels that control blood flow depending on the temperature.

Concept note-3: -Jackrabbits have a set of adaptations that suit them to arid and semiarid environments: a spartan diet with little water, camouflage, eyes on the sides of their heads, furry feet, agile flight and enormous ears. Jackrabbits are herbivores able to thrive on some of the most defended and unappealing plants around them.

Concept note-4: -The antelope jackrabbit inhabits the drier areas of the desert, including creosote bush flats, mesquite grassland, and cactus plains into and beyond southern Sonora. It prefers open places with sparse grasses where it can see predators and flee if need be.
