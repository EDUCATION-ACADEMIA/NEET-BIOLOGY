Check all the examples of secondary succession.

(A) ** A forest is completely destroyed in a fire

(B) A new island is created by a hotspot in the ocean

(C) ** A tornado completely destroyed a crop of corn

(D) a glacier retreats exposing the bed rock.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Secondary succession is the series of changes that take place when there is soil present. For example, after a fire, hurricane, flood, destruction by man, or tornado.

Concept note-2: -Secondary succession happens when a climax community or intermediate community is impacted by a disturbance. This restarts the cycle of succession, but not back to the beginning-soil and nutrients are still present.
