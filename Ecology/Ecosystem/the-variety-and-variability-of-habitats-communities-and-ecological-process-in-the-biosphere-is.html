The variety and variability of habitats, communities, and ecological process in the biosphere is

(A) Ecosystem Adaptation

(B) ** Ecosystem diversity

(C) Ecosystem differential

(D) Ecosystem standard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Biodiversity may be defined as the variety and variability of living organisms and the ecological complexes in which they exist.

Concept note-2: -Ecosystem diversity is all the different habitats, biological communities, and ecological processes, as well as variation within individual ecosystems.

Concept note-3: -Ecological biodiversity is the diversity of ecosystems, natural communities, and habitats. In essence, itâ€™s the variety of ways that species interact with each other and their environment.

Concept note-4: -Biodiversity is defined as the variety and variability amongst life forms at all levels of biological organisation (ranging from macromolecules within cells to biomes). Edward Wilson, a sociobiologist, popularised the term biodiversity to describe the combined diversity at all the levels of biological organisation.

Concept note-5: -Biodiversity comes from two words Bio meaning life and diversity meaning variability. Biodiversity is the variety of all living things; the different plants, animals and micro organisms, the genetic information they contain and the ecosystems they form.
