An owl and a hawk both eat mice. Which of these describes the relationship between a hawk and an owl?

(A) parasitism

(B) ** competition

(C) predation

(D) mutualism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An owl and a hawk both eat mice. Which of the following describes the relationship between a hawk and an owl? mutualism.

Concept note-2: -The best-known examples of predation involve carnivorous interactions, in which one animal consumes another. Think of wolves hunting moose, owls hunting mice, or shrews hunting worms and insects.

Concept note-3: -Camouflage of Prey and Predators One of the most common predator adaptations is camouflage. Camouflage is the process of using body shape and coloration to blend in with the environment.

Concept note-4: -Mutualism. In a mutualism, two species have a long-term interaction that is beneficial to both of them (+/+ interaction).

Concept note-5: -Which of the following is an example of predation? Sea anemones coating the shallow-water coral reefs in Australia feed on fish and shrimp that wander too close to their tentacles. predation.
