ECOSYSTEM

[SOURCES]
The transfer of energy from one organism to the next in an ecosystem begins with a producer. A producer is an organism that produces its own food. A consumer is an organism in a food chain that obtains energy from producers or other consumers; consumers may be herbivores or carnivores.Which food chain correctly describes the flow of energy in an ecosystem?

(A) ** grass → grasshopper → fish → human

(B) grasshopper → fish → human

(C) human → fish → grasshopper

(D) human → grasshopper → fish → grass

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The transfer of energy from one organism to the next in an ecosystem begins with a producer. A producer is an organism that produces its own food. A consumer is an organism in a food chain that obtains energy from producers or other consumers; consumers may be herbivores or carnivores.

Concept note-2: -A food chain shows the flow of energy and materials from one organism to the next in a habitat . It begins with a producer .

Concept note-3: -Each time something eats something else, food energy is transferred from one organism to another. The transfer of energy between organisms is called a food chain. Ecosystem All the organisms in an area and the environment in which they live.

Concept note-4: -A food chain is a linear sequence of organisms through which nutrients and energy pass as one organism eats another.

Concept note-5: -Transfer of food energy from the producers through a series of organisms with repeated eating and being eaten is known as food chain. The food chain consists of producers, primary consumers, secondary consumers, tertiary consumers or top carnivores and decomposers.
