The symbiotic relationship between a flower and the insect that feeds on its nectar is an example of

(A) ** mutualism because the flower provides the insect with food and the insect pollinates the flower.

(B) parasitism because the insect lives off the nectar from the flower.

(C) commensalism because the insect doesn’t harm the flower, and the flower doesn’t benefit from the relationship.

(D) predation because the insect feeds on the flower.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mutualism because the flower provides the insects with food and the insect pollinates the flower. Correct! This is an example of mutualism.

Concept note-2: -mutualism because the flower provides the insect with food and the insect pollinates the flower.

Concept note-3: -A preeminent association between flowering plants and insects is pollination. Pollination is a mutualism in which two interactors reciprocally benefit: a host plant receives the service of insect pollination in return for a reward provided for its insect pollinator.

Concept note-4: -Aphids and ants Many aphid species are known to engage in a mutualistic relationship with ants that feed on the honeydew by ‘milking’ the aphids with their antennae. In return, some species of ants will protect the aphids from predators and parasites.
