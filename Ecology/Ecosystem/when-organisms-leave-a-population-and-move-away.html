When organisms leave a population and move away

(A) ** Emigration

(B) Immigration

(C) Birth Rate

(D) Death Rate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The movement of organisms out of a range is called emigration. It is the migration or movement of individuals out of a population and leads to a decrease in the size of a given population. Immigration is the arrival of new individuals into a population.

Concept note-2: -Emigration is the movement of individuals out of a population. This decreases the population size and growth rate.

Concept note-3: -emigration. People moving out of a population can be named as emigration. Its one of the factors that affect the population density.

Concept note-4: -Emigration is the relocation or process of people leaving one country to reside in another. People emigrate for many reasons, including increasing oneâ€™s chance of employment or improving quality of life. Emigration has an economic impact on the countries involved, including the workforce and consumer spending.

Concept note-5: -Immigration describes individuals moving into the population while emigration describes individuals exiting the population. If immigration rates are higher than emigration rates, the population increases, whereas if emigration rates are higher than immigration rates, the population size decreases.
