In a grassland, ecosystem lions prey on wildebeest. The population of lions has declined steadily because of illegal hunting. What will most likely happen to the population of wildebeest?

(A) The wildebeest population will decrease.

(B) ** The wildebeest population will increase.

(C) The wildebeest population will remain the same.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which ecosystem would be more likely to survive if a disease killed the grasses? The forest ecosystem, because most of the animals can eat other organisms.

Concept note-2: -Answer. Answer: Biodiversity prevents habitats from being destroyed by people. Maintaining diversity allows species to use up the resources in an ecosystem.

Concept note-3: -One way a population can increase its carrying capacity is by expanding into new niches. Limiting factors are biotic or abiotic environmental conditions that limit population growth in an ecosystem.

Concept note-4: -How are a grassland ecosystem and a pond ecosystem connected? Grassland animals, such as buffalo and rabbits, may drink freshwater from a pond. Pond animals, such as buffalo and rabbits, may drink freshwater from the grassland. Grassland animals, such as buffalo and rabbits, may drink salt water from a pond.
