Concept note-1: -Canada’s response. Canada created a federal-provincial team to devise a common solution ― the 1985 Eastern Canada Acid Rain Program. It established: An eastern Canada cap of 2.3 million tonnes of Sulphur Dioxide (SO2)in the seven easternmost provinces, to be met by 1994 and maintained until 2000.

Concept note-2: -Emissions Over Time Fuel for electricity and heating, as well as non-ferrous smelters (producing such metals as nickel and copper are the largest sources of SO2 emissions in Canada, followed closely by emissions produced by the oil and gas sector.
