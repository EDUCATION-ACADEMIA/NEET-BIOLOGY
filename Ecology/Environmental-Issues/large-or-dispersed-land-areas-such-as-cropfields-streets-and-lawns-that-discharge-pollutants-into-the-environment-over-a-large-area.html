ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES

[SOURCES]
Large or dispersed land areas such as cropfields, streets, and lawns that discharge pollutants into the environment over a large area.

(A) ** nonpoint source

(B) point source

(C) pollution

(D) recycle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nonpoint source pollution comes from oil, pet waste, pesticide, herbicide, fertilizer, road salt, bacteria, sediment, and any other contaminant that ends up on the ground naturally or from human activity.

Concept note-2: -Point-source pollution is easy to identify. As the name suggests, it comes from a single place. Nonpoint-source pollution is harder to identify and harder to address. It is pollution that comes from many places, all at once.

Concept note-3: -Nonpoint Source Pollution is transported by the storm water system or other surface water flows to local streams and bays. In contrast, a particular ongoing discharge from a wastewater plant or industrial facility is considered a “point source” of water pollution.

Concept note-4: -Nonpoint source pollution occurs when runoff from rain and snowmelt carries pollutants into waterways such as rivers, streams, lakes, wetlands, and even groundwater.
