Which country in Southwest Asia is landlocked?

(A) ** Afghanistan

(B) Turkey

(C) Israel

(D) Saudi Arabia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Afghanistan, landlocked multiethnic country located in the heart of south-central Asia.

Concept note-2: -These landlocked countries are Afghanistan, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bhutan, Turkmenistan, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Laos, Mongolia, Nepal, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan.

Concept note-3: -Afghanistan is most commonly referred to as being a part of the region of Central Asia; a group of countries that occupies the area between China, India, Russia and Iran.

Concept note-4: -The Islamic Republic of Afghanistan is a landlocked country at the juncture of Central and South Asia. Afghanistan is bordered by Pakistan in the east and south and from Iran in the west with Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan in the north and with China in the northeast.

Concept note-5: -Due to their geographical realities, South Asian landlocked states that include Afghanistan, Bhutan and Nepal, depend on their neighbours for trade with the outside world.
