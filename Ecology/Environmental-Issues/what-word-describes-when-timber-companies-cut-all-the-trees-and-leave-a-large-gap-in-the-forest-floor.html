what word describes when timber companies cut all the trees and leave a large gap in the forest floor

(A) acid rain

(B) ** clear-cutting

(C) tree extraction

(D) mineral extraction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deforestation is the purposeful clearing of forested land. Throughout history and into modern times, forests have been razed to make space for agriculture and animal grazing, and to obtain wood for fuel, manufacturing, and construction.

Concept note-2: -wood·cut·ting ˈwu̇d-ˌkə-tiŋ : the action or occupation of cutting wood or timber. : the producing of woodcuts.

Concept note-3: -Final answer: Cutting down trees on a large scale is called Deforestation.

Concept note-4: -Felling involves cutting a standing tree and dropping it in the place you want it. Limbing is the removal of the branches from either standing or downed trees. Bucking is the process of cutting a downed tree into appropriate lengths.

Concept note-5: -What is Clearcutting? Clearcutting is a method of timber harvest and regeneration. It involves cutting all or most of the trees in an area at one time.
