ECOLOGY

ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES

[SOURCES]
All external conditions and factors, living and nonliving (chemicals and energy), that affect an organism or other specified system during its lifetime.

(A) pollution

(B) ** environment

(C) ecology

(D) resource

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An organism’s environment is the sum of all external factors to which it is exposed, including biotic (living) and abiotic (nonliving) factors. Biotic factors include influences by members of the same and other species on the development and survival of the individual.

Concept note-2: -So, the correct answer is ‘Environment’.

Concept note-3: -environment, the complex of physical, chemical, and biotic factors that act upon an organism or an ecological community and ultimately determine its form and survival.

Concept note-4: -An abiotic factor is a non-living part of an ecosystem that shapes its environment. In a terrestrial ecosystem, examples might include temperature, light, and water. In a marine ecosystem, abiotic factors would include salinity and ocean currents.
