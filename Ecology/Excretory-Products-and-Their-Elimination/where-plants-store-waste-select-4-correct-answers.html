Where plants store waste? (Select 4 correct answers)

(A) ** Leaves documents

(B) ** bark

(C) ** Fruits

(D) ** Roots

(E) Flowers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In plants the waste products are stored in cellular vacuoles. In a plant waste materials are often stored in old xylem in the form of gums and resins. It is also stored in falling leaves. Excretion of gaseous waste in plants takes place through stomatal pores on leaves.

Concept note-2: -The gums, oils, latex, resins, etc. are some waste products stored in plant parts like barks, stems, leaves, etc. Eventually, plants shed off these parts. The oil produced from orange, eucalyptus, jasmine, latex from the rubber tree, papaya tree, and gums from acacia, are different forms of stored waste products.

Concept note-3: -Solution : (i) Resin and gums are the two wastes which are stored in old xylem in plants.

Concept note-4: -Waste that is derived from plant sources is called plant waste. Commonly they include food waste, fruit and vegetable peels, flower trimmings. Dead matter or fallen leaf material which is called litter is also included in this type of waste.
