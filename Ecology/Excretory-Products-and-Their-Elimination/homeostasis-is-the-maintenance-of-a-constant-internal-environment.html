Homeostasis is the maintenance of a constant internal environment.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Homeostasis is the maintenance of a stable internal environment. Homeostasis is a term coined to describe the physical and chemical parameters that an organism must maintain to allow proper functioning of its component cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems.

Concept note-2: -Homeostasis can be defined as ‘preserving constancy in the internal environment’.

Concept note-3: -Homeostasis is not constant. It is a dynamic process that changes internal conditions to promote survival. Regulation via homeostasis is not a singular feedback cycle. It reflects the complex interaction of many feedback systems controlled by nerves and hormones.

Concept note-4: -Homeostasis is maintained by a series of control mechanisms functioning at the organ, tissue or cellular level. These control mechanisms include substrate supply, activation or inhibition of individual enzymes and receptors, synthesis and degradation of enzymes, and compartmentalization.

Concept note-5: -Homeostasis maintains optimal conditions for enzyme action throughout the body, as well as all cell functions. It is the maintenance of a constant internal environment despite changes in internal and external conditions. In the human body, these include the control of: blood glucose concentration.
