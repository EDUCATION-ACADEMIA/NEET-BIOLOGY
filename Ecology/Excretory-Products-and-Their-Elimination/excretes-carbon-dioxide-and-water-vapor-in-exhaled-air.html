EXCRETORY PRODUCTS AND THEIR ELIMINATION

[SOURCES]
Excretes carbon dioxide and water vapor in exhaled air

(A) ** Lungs

(B) Kidneys

(C) Skin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The lungs are responsible for the excretion of gaseous wastes, primarily carbon dioxide from cellular respiration in cells throughout the body. Exhaled air also contains water vapor and trace levels of some other waste gases. The paired kidneys are often considered the main organs of excretion.

Concept note-2: -The lungs and respiratory system allow us to breathe. They bring oxygen into our bodies (called inspiration, or inhalation) and send carbon dioxide out (called expiration, or exhalation). This exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide is called respiration.

Concept note-3: -Carbon dioxide is the waste product of the body that needs to be expelled out through exhalation. It is saturated with water vapours, that’s why water vapours also come out with it. So the correct answer is ‘Exhaled air’.

Concept note-4: -Gas exchange takes place in the millions of alveoli in the lungs and the capillaries that envelop them. As shown below, inhaled oxygen moves from the alveoli to the blood in the capillaries, and carbon dioxide moves from the blood in the capillaries to the air in the alveoli.

Concept note-5: -The carbon dioxide breathed out is a by-product of the process of cell respiration, as is water. In this process, energy is produced in the mitochondria of cells.
