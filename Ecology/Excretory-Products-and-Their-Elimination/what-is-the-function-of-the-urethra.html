What is the function of the urethra?

(A) It filters urea out of the bloodstream

(B) ** It delivers urine from the bladder to the outside world

(C) It transfers urine from the kidneys to the bladder

(D) It prevents urine from leaking out of the bladder

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The urethra is the tube that lets urine leave your bladder and your body. If you were assigned male at birth, your urethra passes through your prostate and into your penis. If you were assigned female at birth, your urethra is much shorter. It runs from your bladder to open in front of your vagina.

Concept note-2: -(yoo-REE-thruh) The tube through which urine leaves the body. It empties urine from the bladder.

Concept note-3: -The urine is stored in the bladder until it leaves the body through the urethra.

Concept note-4: -Functions. In males the urethra allows for the passage of urine from the bladder to the outside of the body (urination) and is also responsible for the expulsion of sperm during ejaculation.

Concept note-5: -When a person urinates, the pee exits the bladder and goes out of the body through the urethra (pronounced: yoo-REE-thruh), another tube-like structure. The male urethra ends at the tip of the penis; the female urethra ends just above the vaginal opening.
