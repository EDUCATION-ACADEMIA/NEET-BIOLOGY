Physical process called peristalsis occurs in

(A) trachae

(B) intestines

(C) ** esophagus

(D) mouth only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overview. Peristalsis is a series of wave-like muscle contractions that move food through the digestive tract. It starts in the esophagus where strong wave-like motions of the smooth muscle move balls of swallowed food to the stomach.

Concept note-2: -Peristalsis squeezes your esophageal muscles from top to bottom. This pushes food and liquid along. If you could see peristalsis, it would look like a wave passing down your esophagus. To keep food and liquids moving in the right direction, your digestive tract has special muscles along its course called sphincters.

Concept note-3: -Peristalsis, waves of involuntary muscle contractions, push food toward the stomach. There, more physical and chemical changes occur.

Concept note-4: -Peristalsis is a series of muscle contractions. These contractions occur in your digestive tract. Peristalsis is also seen in the tubes that connect the kidneys to the bladder. Peristalsis is an automatic and important process.
