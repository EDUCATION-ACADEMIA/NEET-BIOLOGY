For the following situation, determine if there is a correlation. If there is a correlation, is it likely a causal relationship? The amount of time you study for a test and the score you receive.

(A) Negative correlation and likely a causal relationship

(B) Negative correlation but not a causal relationship

(C) Positive correlation but not a causal relationship

(D) ** Positive correlation and likely a causal relationship

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Causation means one thing causes another-in other words, action A causes outcome B. On the other hand, correlation is simply a relationship where action A relates to action B-but one event doesn’t necessarily cause the other event to happen.

Concept note-2: -The formula for the test statistic is t=r√n−2√1−r2. The value of the test statistic, t, is shown in the computer or calculator output along with the p-value. The test statistic t has the same sign as the correlation coefficient r. The p-value is the combined area in both tails.

Concept note-3: -Correlation tests for a relationship between two variables. However, seeing two variables moving together does not necessarily mean we know whether one variable causes the other to occur. This is why we commonly say “correlation does not imply causation.”

Concept note-4: -Correlation implies association, but not causation. Conversely, causation implies association, but not correlation.
