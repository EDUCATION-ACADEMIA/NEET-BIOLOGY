What is the first stage in the life cycle of a frog

(A) larva/tadpole

(B) ** egg

(C) adult frog

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Eggs are laid in a group in the water by the frog. These then hatch into tadpoles. A female frog can lay up to 4, 000 eggs at any one time! These eggs float on the water in ponds, lakes, and rivers, and look like a large mass of jelly.

Concept note-2: -The life cycle of a frog consists of three stages: egg, larva, and adult. As the frog grows, it moves through these stages in a process known as metamorphosis.

Concept note-3: -The third stage is the froglet stage. It grows lungs and legs. Its gills disappear. Its tail gets shorter until it disappears too.
