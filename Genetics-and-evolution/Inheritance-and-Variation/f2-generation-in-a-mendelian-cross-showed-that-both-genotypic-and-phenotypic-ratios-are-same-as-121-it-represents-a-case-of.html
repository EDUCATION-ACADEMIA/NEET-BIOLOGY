F2 generation in a Mendelian cross showed that both genotypic and phenotypic ratios are same as 1:2:1. It represents a case of:

(A) Co-dominance

(B) Dihybrid cross

(C) Monohybrid cross with complete dominance

(D) ** Monohybrid cross with incomplete dominance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Final answer: F2 generation in a Mendelian cross with both genotypic and phenotypic ratios 1 : 2 : 1 is a case of incomplete dominance.

Concept note-2: -The normal phenotypic ratio in F2 generation is 3:1 and the genotypic ratio is 1:2:1.

Concept note-3: -Thus, the correct answer is ‘Incomplete dominance’.

Concept note-4: -The F2 progeny of Mendel’s dihybrid cross exhibit four different phenotypes: round yellow, round green, wrinkled yellow and wrinkled green. There are 9 different genotypes: RRYY, RRYy, RrYY, RrYy-4 genotypes of the round yellow phenotype. RRyy, Rryy-2 genotypes of the round green phenotype.

Concept note-5: -The F1 plants produce two types of gametes. Assuming that these gametes combine randomly, we could say that in the F2 generation, homozygous dominant, heterozygous dominant and homozygous recessive individuals are produced in a 1:2:1 ratio.
