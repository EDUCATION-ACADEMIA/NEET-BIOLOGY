What map unit (Centimorgan) is adopted in the construction of genetic maps?

(A) A unit of distance between two expressed genes representing 10% cross over.

(B) A unit of distance between two expressed genes representing 100% cross over.

(C) ** A unit of distance between genes on chromosomes, representing 1% cross over.

(D) A unit of distance between genes on chromosomes, representing 50% cross over.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is often used to guess the distance along a chromosome but this map unit is not a true physical distance. Hence, the correct answer is option (C). Note: A centimorgan or map unit is a unit which is used for the measurement of genetic distance.

Concept note-2: -In genetics, a centimorgan (abbreviated cM) or map unit (m.u.) is a unit for measuring genetic linkage. It is defined as the distance between chromosome positions (also termed loci or markers) for which the expected average number of intervening chromosomal crossovers in a single generation is 0.01.

Concept note-3: -A centimorgan (abbreviated cM) is a unit of measure for the frequency of genetic recombination. One centimorgan is equal to a 1% chance that two markers on a chromosome will become separated from one another due to a recombination event during meiosis (which occurs during the formation of egg and sperm cells).

Concept note-4: -The recombination percentage depends on the distance between genes on chromosomes. The unit of a genetic map is called a centiMorgan, and one centiMorgan corresponds to one percentage recombination frequency.

Concept note-5: -CentiMorgan (cM) is a unit to measure the distance between genes on the chromosome. It is denoted by map unit. One centiMorgan is equal to one map unit and same will be the recombination frequency. The recombination rate varies not only among the individuals but also the chromosomes.
