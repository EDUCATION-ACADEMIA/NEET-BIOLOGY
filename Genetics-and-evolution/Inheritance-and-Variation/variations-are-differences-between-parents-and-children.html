INHERITANCE AND VARIATION

Variations are differences between parents and children.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Variation is the degree by which progeny or offspring differ from their parents. Thse variations could either be genotypic variations (difference in genetic makeup) or phenotypic variations (different traits). Q. The difference that exists between progeny and parents is known as variation.

Concept note-2: -The answer has to do with the fact that each parent actually has two different sets of genes. And that each parent passes only half of their genes to their child. And that the half that gets passed down is random. All of this together ensures that each child ends up with a different, unique set of genes.

Concept note-3: -Variation is defined as the visible differences represented by the individuals within the progeny and species.

Concept note-4: -The difference that exists between progeny and parents is known as variation.
