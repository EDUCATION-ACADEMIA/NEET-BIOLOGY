What was the name of the ship Darwin traveled the world on?

(A) ** HMS Beagle

(B) SS Beagle

(C) HMS Bugle

(D) SS Bugle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Charles Darwin set sail on the ship HMS Beagle on December 27, 1831, from Plymouth, England. Darwin was 22 years old when he was hired to be the ship’s naturalist. Most of the trip was spent sailing around South America.

Concept note-2: -A voyage of discovery The Beagle reached the Galapagos Islands on 15 September 1835, nearly four years after setting off from Plymouth, England. The visit to the Galapagos would prove the starting point from which Darwin would develop his theories on evolution and secure his enduring fame.

Concept note-3: -Beagle was a Royal Navy ship, famed for taking English naturalist Charles Darwin on his first expedition around the world in 1831–36. Beagle was launched at Woolwich Dockyard, London, in 1820.

Concept note-4: -Eight vessels of the Royal Navy have been named HMS Beagle, after a dog breed. The most notable of these ships is the second HMS Beagle, 1820–1870, which transported Charles Darwin around the world in the voyage of Beagle.

Concept note-5: -The ship’s first stop was meant to be Tenerife in the Canary Islands, the same place that Darwin had hoped to visit with Henslow.
