process by which different types of cells arise from less specialized cells

(A) morphogenesis

(B) ** differentiation

(C) determination

(D) genomic equivalence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cell differentiation is an important process whereby a single cell gradually develops various tissues and organs. It helps in the development of an organism from a single cell zygote.

Concept note-2: -Differentiation is the process by which unspecialized cells become specialized to carry out distinct functions.

Concept note-3: -The process of hematopoiesis involves the differentiation of multipotent cells into blood and immune cells. The multipotent hematopoietic stem cells give rise to many different cell types, including the cells of the immune system and red blood cells.

Concept note-4: -The process during which young, immature (unspecialized) cells take on individual characteristics and reach their mature (specialized) form and function.
