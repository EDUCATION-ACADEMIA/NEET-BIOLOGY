A tool that helps to show all possible combinations of sex cells in a cross between parents to predict the probability of their offspring possessing certain physical characteristics.

(A) ** Punnett Square

(B) Rubrics Cube

(C) Probability Circle

(D) Reproduction Table

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Punnett square is a square diagram that is used to predict the genotypes of a particular cross or breeding experiment. It is named after Reginald C. Punnett, who devised the approach in 1905. The diagram is used by biologists to determine the probability of an offspring having a particular genotype.

Concept note-2: -A Punnett square is a chart that allows you to determine the expected percentages of different genotypes in the offspring of two parents. A Punnett square allows the prediction of the percentages of phenotypes in the offspring of a cross from known genotypes.

Concept note-3: -The Punnett square is a table in which all of the possible outcomes for a genetic cross between two individuals with known genotypes are given. In its simplest form, the Punnett square consists of a square divided into four quadrants.

Concept note-4: -Punnet squares are used to predict the genotypes and phenotypes of offspring using known dominant and recessive genes for single gene traits. The genotype for one parent is written along the side of the square with dominant alleles written as capital letters and recessive alleles written as lowercase letters.

Concept note-5: -The possible genotypes and phenotypes of the offspring can be determined using a Punnett square, a grid that shows the possible combinations of alleles that can result at fertilisation.
