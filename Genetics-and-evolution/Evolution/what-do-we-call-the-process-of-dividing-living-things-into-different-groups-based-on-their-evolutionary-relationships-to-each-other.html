What do we call the process of dividing living things into different groups based on their evolutionary relationships to each other?

(A) Lamarkism

(B) natural selection

(C) Linnaeus

(D) ** classification

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Taxonomy is the study of relationships between living things and the formal classification of organisms into groups based upon those hypothesized relationships. Organisms are classified based upon their similarities and differences.

Concept note-2: -Answer: Biological classification is the process of dividing living organisms into groups and subgroups.

Concept note-3: -Phylogeny is the evolutionary relationship between organisms through the course of history. The branch of science that deals with study of phylogeny is called phylogenetics.

Concept note-4: -Classification based on evolutionary relationships of organisms is called phylogenetic system of classification.

Concept note-5: -Cladistics is the classification of organisms based on the evolutionary and phylogenetic relationships and it does not take morphological similarities or dissimilarities into account.
