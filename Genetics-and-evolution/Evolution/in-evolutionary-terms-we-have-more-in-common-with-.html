In evolutionary terms, we have more in common with ____

(A) a Chimpanzee

(B) a spider

(C) a bacterium

(D) ** an African school boy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In evolutionary terms humans and chimpanzees are more similar as they belong to the same order Primate and family Hominidae. They are both mammals. Humans are more similar to Pan troglodytes (chimpanzees). The scientific name of humans is Homo sapiens.

Concept note-2: -In terms of evolution, organisms belonging to the same species have more in common than organisms belonging to different species of the same genus or different genus. We have more in common with a Chinese school-boy because both belongs to the same species of Homo sapiens, and thus, share a common ancestor.

Concept note-3: -The American snake is a close relative, but a Chinese horse is a mammal and therefore, the closest evolutionary relative of the Indian school boy.

Concept note-4: -In terms of evolution, humans have more in common with a Chimpanzee as we share the same family and primates have identical order. So, the current answer is option B.
