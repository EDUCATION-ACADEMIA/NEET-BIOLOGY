Which would indicate that a series of fossils represent the evolution of the horse?

(A) All of the fossils were found in the same layers of rocks as horse fossils.

(B) All of the fossils are completely identical to horse bones.

(C) ** All of the fossils show similar structures to that of the modern horse.

(D) All of the fossils were located in the same place on Earth as horses.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If all of the fossils show similar structures to that of the modern horse, it indicates that a series of fossils represent the evolution of the horse.

Concept note-2: -Q. Which would indicate that a series of fossils represent the evolution of the horse? All of the fossils were found in the same layers of rocks as horse fossils.

Concept note-3: -Evidence from Fossils The fossil record reveals how horses evolved. The lineage that led to modern horses (Equus) grew taller over time (from the 0.4 m Hyracotherium in early Eocene to the 1.6 m Equus). This lineage also developed longer molar teeth and the degeneration of the outer phalanges on the feet.

Concept note-4: -Fossils help in establishing the time period in which organisms lived. Fossils provide good evidence of the structure and lifestyle of animals (and even plants) from the past. They provide a record of the remains of long-extinct species.
