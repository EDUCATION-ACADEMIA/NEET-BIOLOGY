A single segment of DNA that controls protein production and the cell cycle.

(A) ** Gene

(B) Allele

(C) Chromosome

(D) Chromatid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A gene is the section of DNA required to produce one protein. Genes are typically hundreds or thousands of base pairs in length because they code for proteins made of hundreds or thousands of amino acids. Most genes contain the information needed to make functional molecules called proteins.

Concept note-2: -A nucleotide is a subunit of DNA or RNA that consists of a nitrogenous base (A, G, T, or C in DNA; A, G, U, or C in RNA), a phosphate molecule, and a sugar molecule (deoxyribose in DNA, and ribose in RNA).

Concept note-3: -A gene is the basic physical and functional unit of heredity. Genes are made up of DNA. Some genes act as instructions to make molecules called proteins.

Concept note-4: -A gene is a segment of DNA that provides the code to construct a protein.

Concept note-5: -Summary. Two classes of genes, oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes, link cell cycle control to tumor formation and development. Oncogenes in their proto-oncogene state drive the cell cycle forward, allowing cells to proceed from one cell cycle stage to the next.
