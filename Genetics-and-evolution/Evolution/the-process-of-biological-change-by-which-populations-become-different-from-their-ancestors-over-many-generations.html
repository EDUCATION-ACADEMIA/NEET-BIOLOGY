EVOLUTION

[SOURCES]
The process of biological change by which populations become different from their ancestors over many generations

(A) Heredity

(B) Transitioning

(C) Adaptation

(D) ** Evolution

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Evolution is the process by which populations of organisms change over generations. Genetic variations underlie these changes.

Concept note-2: -Biological evolution is the change in inherited traits over successive generations in populations of organisms. Adaptation is a key evolutionary process in which variation in the fitness of traits and species are adjusted by natural selection to become better suited for survival in specific ecological habitats.

Concept note-3: -Evolution is the biological change process by which descendants come to differ from their ancestors.

Concept note-4: -Genetic variation can be caused by mutation (which can create entirely new alleles in a population), random mating, random fertilization, and recombination between homologous chromosomes during meiosis (which reshuffles alleles within an organismâ€™s offspring).

Concept note-5: -Evolution is a process that results in changes in the genetic material of a population over time. Evolution reflects the adaptations of organisms to their changing environments and can result in altered genes, novel traits, and new species.
