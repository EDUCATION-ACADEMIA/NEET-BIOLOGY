[SOURCES]
The struggle for existence against other members of an organisms species is

(A) ** Competition

(B) Natural Selection

(C) gene flow

(D) genetic drift

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The concept of the struggle for existence concerns the competition or battle for resources needed to live. It can refer to human society, or to organisms in nature. The concept is ancient, and the term struggle for existence was in use by the end of the 18th century.

Concept note-2: -So, the correct answer is ‘Intraspecific struggle’.

Concept note-3: -In Darwin’s theory on the mechanism of evolution, competition among living things is viewed as a major part of the “struggle for existence” and therefore as a basis for natural selection (Darwin 1872).

Concept note-4: -noun phrase : the automatic competition of members of a natural population for limited vital resources (such as food, space, or light) that results in natural selection.

Concept note-5: -Solution : Intraspecific struggle, interspecific struggle and environmental struggle are the three types of struggle for existence.
