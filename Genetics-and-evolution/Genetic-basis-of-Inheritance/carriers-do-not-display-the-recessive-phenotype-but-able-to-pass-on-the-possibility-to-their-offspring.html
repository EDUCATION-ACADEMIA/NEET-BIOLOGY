GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

Carriers do not display the recessive phenotype but able to pass on the possibility to their offspring.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A recessive gene means that it is rarely found in the gene pool Recessive genes never are expressed in the phenotype If both a dominant and a recessive gene are present; the dominant one will be expressed in the phenotype: A dominant gene is the one most frequently found in the gene pool.

Concept note-2: -Autosomal recessive inheritance is a way a genetic trait or condition can be passed down from parent to child. A genetic condition can occur when the child inherits one copy of a mutated (changed) gene from each parent. The parents of a child with an autosomal recessive condition usually do not have the condition.

Concept note-3: -If you are born to parents who both carry the same autosomal recessive gene, you have a 25% (1 in 4) chance of inheriting the abnormal gene from both parents and developing the disease. You have a 50% (1 in 2) chance of inheriting one abnormal gene.

Concept note-4: -Recessive alleles only show their effect if the individual has two copies of the allele (also known as being homozygous?). For example, the allele for blue eyes is recessive, therefore to have blue eyes you need to have two copies of the ‘blue eye’ allele.
