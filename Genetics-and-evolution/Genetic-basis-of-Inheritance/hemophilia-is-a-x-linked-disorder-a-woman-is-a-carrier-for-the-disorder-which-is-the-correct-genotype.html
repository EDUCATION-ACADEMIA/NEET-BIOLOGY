Hemophilia is a x-linked disorder. A woman is a carrier for the disorder. Which is the correct genotype?

(A) Hh

(B) ** XHXh

(C) hh

(D) XhXh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A female with a hemophilia allele on one X chromosome usually has a normal allele on her other X chromosome that can produce normal clotting factor, so she has some protection against having hemophilia. A female with one hemophilia allele and one normal allele is called heterozygous or a carrier.

Concept note-2: -Hemophilia is a sex-linked recessive disorder. The abnormal gene responsible for hemophilia is carried on the X chromosome.

Concept note-3: -Hemophilia is an X-linked recessive hereditary disorder that classically affects males due to the presence of only one X chromosome in males. Females are usually carriers due to the presence of counterpart X chromosome, but many times manifestations of hemophilia are seen in heterozygous carrier females.
