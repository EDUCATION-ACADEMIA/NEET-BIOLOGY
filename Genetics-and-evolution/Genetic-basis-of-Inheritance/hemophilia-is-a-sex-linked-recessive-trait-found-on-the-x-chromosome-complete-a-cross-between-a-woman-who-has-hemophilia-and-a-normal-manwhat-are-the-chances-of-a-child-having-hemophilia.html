[SOURCES]
Hemophilia is a sex-linked recessive trait found on the x chromosome. Complete a cross between a woman who has hemophilia and a normal man.What are the chances of a child having hemophilia?

(A) 100%

(B) 75%

(C) ** 50%

(D) 25%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Males are affected more often than females, because the gene is located on the X chromosome. Hemophilia A. Hemophilia A is a disorder where the blood cannot clot properly due to a deficiency of a clotting factor called Factor VIII.

Concept note-2: -However, all daughters of a man with hemophilia are considered obligate carriers of hemophilia because they must receive the affected X chromosome from their fathers to be female, and they can pass the gene to their sons. A female with the hemophilia gene is called a hemophilia carrier.

Concept note-3: -A man who has hemophilia can only pass his altered hemophilia gene on to his daughters, as only his daughters will inherit his altered X chromosome (his sons will inherit his unaffected Y chromosome).

Concept note-4: -In the United States Hemophilia A affects 1 in 5, 000 male births. About 400 babies are born with hemophilia A each year.
