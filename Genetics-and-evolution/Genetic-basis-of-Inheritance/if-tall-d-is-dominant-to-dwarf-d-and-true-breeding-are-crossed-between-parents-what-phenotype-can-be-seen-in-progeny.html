If tall (D) is dominant to dwarf (d) and true breeding are crossed between parents, what phenotype can be seen in progeny?

(A) ** all tall

(B) all dwarf

(C) all intermediate forms

(D) 1 tall:1 dwarf

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The genotype of dwarf offspring: t/t. The genotype of tall offspring: T/t whose parental cross always produce offspring.

Concept note-2: -A cross between pure breeding tall (TT) and dwarf (tt) plants produce all tall (Tt) plants in F1 progeny. These heterozygous tall plants produce 50% gametes with “T” allele and 50% with “t” allele. Random fusion of these gametes from two heterozygous tall plants gives tall and dwarf plants in 3:1 ratio.

Concept note-3: -Therefore, option b) 1 : 2: 1 :: Tall homozygous : Tall heterozygous : Dwarf is the correct option.

Concept note-4: -The phenotypic ratio between tall and dwarf plants in F2 generation is 3:1. Example of Mendel’s pea cross: If parent is heterozygous dominant then the genotype will be Tt and Tt. Now, if you will do a cross then the result you will get is: Parents: Tt x Tt.
