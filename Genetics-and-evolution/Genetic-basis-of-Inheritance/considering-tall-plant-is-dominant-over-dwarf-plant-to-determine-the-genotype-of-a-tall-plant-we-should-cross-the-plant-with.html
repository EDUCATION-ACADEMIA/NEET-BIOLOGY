Considering tall plant is dominant over dwarf plant. To determine the genotype of a tall plant, we should cross the plant with:

(A) a tall plant of different species

(B) a tall plant of the same species

(C) a dwarf plant of different species

(D) ** a dwarf plant of the same species

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(C) (i)Genotype of dwarf plants will be ‘tt’ because dwarf-ness is a recessive trait that is expressed only when both the genes are recessive. (ii)Genotype of tall plant will be ‘TT’ because the parental cross is producing tall offspring always.

Concept note-2: -As given in the question, the ratio between tall and dwarf plants in the offspring is 1 : 1, hence, the genotype of the tall plant is Tt. Q.

Concept note-3: -The phenotypic ratio of 3:1 is observed when heterozygous plants are self-pollinated. Hence, genotype of parents is Tt and Tt.

Concept note-4: -In a cross between heterozygous tall plants (Tt) and a homozygous dwarf plant (tt), the ratio in the offspring shall be 1 tall : 1 dwarf. This is because the heterozygous plant will make two types of gametes, one type having T allele and the other having t allele. Hence, the correct answer is ‘1 tall : 1 dwarf’
