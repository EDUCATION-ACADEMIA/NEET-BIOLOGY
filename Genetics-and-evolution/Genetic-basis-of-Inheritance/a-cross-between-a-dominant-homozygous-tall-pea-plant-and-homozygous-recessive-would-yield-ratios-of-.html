GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
A cross between a dominant homozygous tall pea plant and homozygous recessive would yield ratios of ____

(A) 1 homozygous tall:3 heterozygous tall

(B) 1 homozygous tall:1 heterozygous tall

(C) 3 homozygous tall:1 heterozygous tall

(D) ** All heterozygous tall

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The selfing of heterozygous tall plants yields F2 generation with the genotypic ratio of 1 TT (homozygous tall): 2 Tt (heterozygous tall): 1 tt (homozygous dwarf). Thus, the correct answer is option B.

Concept note-2: -In a cross between heterozygous tall plants (Tt) and a homozygous dwarf plant (tt), the ratio in the offspring shall be 1 tall : 1 dwarf. This is because the heterozygous plant will make two types of gametes, one type having T allele and the other having t allele.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct option is ‘50%’.

Concept note-4: -A homozygous short pea plant will have the genotype tt. A cross between a homozygous tall tree plant and a homozygous short tree plant will give the following genotypes of the offspring: 100% Tt. Since the offspring has at least one allele that is dominant for tall trees, all the offspring will be tall.
