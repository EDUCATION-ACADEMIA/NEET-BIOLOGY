GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
A genetic cross of inbred snapdragons with red flowers with inbred snapdragons with white flowers resulted in F1 hybrid offspring that all had pink flowers. When the F1 plants were self-pollinated, the resulting F2 generation plants had a phenotypic ratio of 1 red:2 pink:1 white. The most likely explanation is:

(A) pink flowers are the result of a blending of the red and white genotypes.

(B) flower colour is due to 2 or more complementary genes.

(C) ** heterozygous plants have a different phenotype than either inbred parent because of incomplete dominance of the dominant allele.

(D) flower color inheritance in snapdragons does not behave as a Mendelian trait.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, the correct option is ‘pink’

Concept note-2: -A red snapdragon flower is crossed with a white snapdragon flower, and all the resulting flowers from the cross are pink.

Concept note-3: -As mentioned earlier, incomplete dominance is a partial dominance, meaning the phenotype is in between the genotype dominant and recessive alleles. In the above example, the resulting offspring has a pink color trait despite the dominant red color and white color trait due to incomplete dominance.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct answer is ‘Incomplete dominance’.

Concept note-5: -Incomplete dominance The F1-hybrid plants have a different phenotype (pink flowers) than either of the true-breeding parents. This is an example of incomplete dominance.
