Somatic cells (all cells except gametes) have 46 chromosomes. A boy’s somatic cells would have ____

(A) 46 identical chromosomes of identical length.

(B) 46 chromosomes of varying length.

(C) ** 22 pairs of different chromosomes, but each pair is of the same length; one X and one Y chromosome.

(D) 22 pairs of different chromosomes, but each pair is of the same length; and two X chromosomes.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Human somatic cells have 46 chromosomes consisting of two sets of 22 homologous chromosomes and a pair of nonhomologous sex chromosomes. This is the 2n, or diploid, state. Human gametes have 23 chromosomes or one complete set of chromosomes. This is the n, or haploid, state.

Concept note-2: -There are 23 pairs of chromosomes in somatic cells; one member of each pair is paternal (from the father) and one maternal (from the mother). There are 22 matched pairs of autosomal chromosomes, plus one pair of sex chromosomes.

Concept note-3: -Egg and sperm cells have just 23 chromosomes each. That’s half as many chromosomes as regular cells. Through the process of fertilization, egg and sperm join to make a cell with 46 chromosomes (23 pairs), called a zygote.

Concept note-4: -Human body cells (somatic cells) have 46 chromosomes. A somatic cell contains two matched sets of chromosomes, a configuration known as diploid.
