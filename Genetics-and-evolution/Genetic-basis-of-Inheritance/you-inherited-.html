GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
You inherited ____

(A) most of your chromosomes from your mother

(B) most of your chromosomes from your father

(C) a random number of chromosomes from each parent

(D) ** half your chromosomes from your mother and half from your father.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To form a fetus, an egg from the mother and sperm from the father come together. The egg and sperm each have one half of a set of chromosomes. The egg and sperm together give the baby the full set of chromosomes. So, half the baby’s DNA comes from the mother and half comes from the father.

Concept note-2: -We inherit a set of 23 chromosomes from our mothers and another set of 23 from our fathers. One of those pairs are the chromosomes that determine the biological sex of a child – girls have an XX pair and boys have an XY pair, with very rare exceptions in certain disorders.

Concept note-3: -Normally, each cell in the human body has 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total chromosomes). Half come from the mother; the other half come from the father. Two of the chromosomes (the X and the Y chromosome) determine your sex as male or female when you are born.

Concept note-4: -After all, children inherit half of their DNA from each parent: 50 percent from mom (through an egg), and 50 percent from dad (through sperm).

Concept note-5: -While women do inherit 50% of their DNA from each parent, men inherit about 51% from their mother and only 49% from their father.
