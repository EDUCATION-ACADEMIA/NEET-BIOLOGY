GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
The trait that will always be expressed if at least one copy of an allele is present.

(A) Recessive

(B) ** Dominant

(C) DNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A recessive allele does not produce a trait at all when only one copy is present. This contrasts to a dominant trait, which requires that only one of the two alleles be present to express the trait.

Concept note-2: -Dominant alleles show their effect even if the individual only has one copy of the allele (also known as being heterozygous?). For example, the allele for brown eyes is dominant, therefore you only need one copy of the ‘brown eye’ allele to have brown eyes (although, with two copies you will still have brown eyes).

Concept note-3: -Dominant-An allele whose trait always shows up in the organism when it is present.

Concept note-4: -Genetic disorders determined by a single gene (Mendelian disorders) are easiest to analyze and the most well understood. If expression of a trait requires only one copy of a gene (one allele), that trait is considered dominant.

Concept note-5: -A dominant trait will always be expressed in the offspring if the dominant allele is present, even if there is only one copy of it (heterozygous or dominant homozygous, Aa or AA).
