What is a genetic test cross?

(A) Testing a suspected homozygote by crossing it with a known heterozygote

(B) Testing a suspected heterozygote by crossing it with a known heterozygote

(C) Testing a suspected homozygote by crossing it with a known homozygous dominant

(D) ** Testing a suspected heterozygote by crossing it with a known homozygous recessive

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Test cross refers to a genetic cross between a homozygous recessive individual and a corresponding suspected heterozygote to determine the genotype of the latter. Consider the following example: Suppose you have a purple and white flower and purple color (P) is dominant to white (p).

Concept note-2: -Heterozygous dominant when crossed with homozygous recessive, it is called as test cross. Parent: Tt (Tall) X tt (Dwarf) Gamete: T t and t. F1 generation: Tt, tt. The ratio is 1 : 1.

Concept note-3: -Testcross is a cross of any genotype showing dominant phenotype with a recessive homozygote to determine the genotype of a dominant parent if it is a heterozygous-or homozygous-dominant. Being a hybrid Tt shows dominant phenotype and tt is the recessive parent; it is a test cross. Option A is correct.

Concept note-4: -The test cross is another fundamental tool devised by Gregor Mendel. In its simplest form, a test cross is an experimental cross of an individual organism of dominant phenotype but unknown genotype and an organism with a homozygous recessive genotype (and phenotype).

Concept note-5: -Assertion :Cross of F1 individual with a recessive homozygous parent is test cross.
