Which of the following would be an example of codominant inheritance in humans

(A) Type A

(B) ** Type AB

(C) Type O

(D) Type B

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In people, one codominant trait that you canâ€™t really observe by looking at a person, but many people know about themselves, is blood type. People with the AB blood type have one A allele and one B allele. Because both alleles are expressed at the same time, their blood type is AB.

Concept note-2: -In the ABO blood type system in humans, blood type AB is an example of codominance. It implies that both dominant alleles are present and expressed together.

Concept note-3: -AB blood type is codominant because the red blood cells have the products of both the A and the B alleles of the ABO gene.

Concept note-4: -When two alleles of the same gene are expressed simultaneously, it is called codominance. Yes, codominance occurs in humans. This can be seen in human blood groups. In ABO type of blood group system, allele A and allele B are dominant over allele O and are expressed as AB when present together.

Concept note-5: -Human blood type is determined by codominant alleles. There are three different alleles, known as IA, IB, and i. The IA and IB alleles are codominant, and the i allele is recessive. The possible human phenotypes for blood group are type A, type B, type AB, and type O.
