GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
Each species has a specific identifying number of chromosomes. For example, a cat, Felis catus, has 38 chromosomes, while corn, Zea mays, has 20 chromosomes. Each chromosome carries

(A) the same genes as all of the other chromosomes in the set

(B) ** . specific genes that are unique to that chromosome.

(C) an enzyme helicase that binds the chromosome when it breaks.

(D) identical DNA and RNA that was received from the parents.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Domestic cats have 38 chromosomes, including 18 autosomal pairs and the sex chromosomes, X and Y. This karyotype depicts a female cat and therefore has two X chromosomes.

Concept note-2: -In humans, each cell normally contains 23 pairs of chromosomes, for a total of 46. Twenty-two of these pairs, called autosomes, look the same in both males and females. The 23rd pair, the sex chromosomes, differ between males and females.

Concept note-3: -Law of Segregation. Observing that true-breeding pea plants with contrasting traits gave rise to F1 generations that all expressed the dominant trait and F2 generations that expressed the dominant and recessive traits in a 3:1 ratio, Mendel proposed the law of segregation.

Concept note-4: -A genome is all the hereditary information-all the genes-of an organism.
