A couple has had 1 child, who is a boy. Which of the following statements is true?

(A) Their second child will be a girl because the ratio of boy:girl is 1:1.

(B) Their second child will be definitely be a girl because the chance of her being a girl is 50%.

(C) ** Their second child has a 50% chance of being either a boy or a girl as gender of the first child has no effect on the subsequent children.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If you have two children, you’ll have a 50% chance of having a second boy, based on the ratios above. Then if you have three children, you’ll have a 25% chance of having all three boys, and a 75% chance of two girls and a boy or two boys and a girl. Yes, genetics can play a part, but it all comes down to chance.

Concept note-2: -If we assume that boys and girls are born with equal probability, then the probability to have two girls (and one boy) should be the same as the probability to have two boys and one girl. So you would have two cases with probability 2/3, giving an impossible 4/3 probability for both cases.

Concept note-3: -Solution : The production of male or the female child is determined by the type of chromosomes present in the sperm X or Y). There is no criteria to determine the chances of male or female children. Hence, a couple will always have a 1/2 chance to have a daughter and 1/2 chance to have a son each time.
