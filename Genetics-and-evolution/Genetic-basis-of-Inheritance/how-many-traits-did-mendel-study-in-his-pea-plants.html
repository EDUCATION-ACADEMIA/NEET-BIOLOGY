How many traits did Mendel study in his pea plants?

(A) 1

(B) 3

(C) 5

(D) ** 7

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During this time, Mendel observed seven different characteristics in the pea plants, and each of these characteristics had two forms (Figure 3). The characteristics included height (tall or short), pod shape (inflated or constricted), seed shape (smooth or winkled), pea color (green or yellow), and so on.

Concept note-2: -The characteristics included plant height, seed texture, seed color, flower color, pea-pod size, pea-pod color, and flower position.

Concept note-3: -Mendel studied the inheritance of seven different features in peas, including height, flower color, seed color, and seed shape. To do so, he first established pea lines with two different forms of a feature, such as tall vs. short height.

Concept note-4: -Gregor Mendel choose pea plants to perform his experiments because they grow quickly, are easy to breed, and have a variety of traits.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is 7. Mendel selected 7 contrasting characters for his experiment.
