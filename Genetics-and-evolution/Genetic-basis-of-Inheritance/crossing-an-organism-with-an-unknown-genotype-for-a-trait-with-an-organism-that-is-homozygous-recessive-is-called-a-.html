Crossing an organism with an unknown genotype for a trait with an organism that is homozygous recessive is called a ____

(A) Punnet Square

(B) ** Testcross

(C) Mendelian cross

(D) heterozygote

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The test cross is another fundamental tool devised by Gregor Mendel . In its simplest form, a test cross is an experimental cross of an individual organism of dominant phenotype but unknown genotype and an organism with a homozygous recessive genotype (and phenotype).

Concept note-2: -A test cross is when an unknown genotype is crossed with a homozygous recessive genotype in order to determine the unknown genotype. This genotype can be deduced according to the phenotypes of the offspring.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The answer is 50%.

Concept note-4: -Explanation: If all offspring from the test cross display the dominant phenotype, the individual in question is homozygous dominant; if half the offspring display dominant phenotypes and half display recessive phenotypes, then the individual is heterozygous.

Concept note-5: -Solution : A cross between an individual with unknown genotype for a particular trait with a recessive individual of the same trait is called test cross.
