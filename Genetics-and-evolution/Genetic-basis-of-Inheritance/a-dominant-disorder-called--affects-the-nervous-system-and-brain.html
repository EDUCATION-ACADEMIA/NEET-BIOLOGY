[SOURCES]
A dominant disorder called ____ affects the nervous system and brain.

(A) sickle cell anemia

(B) ** huntingtons disease

(C) galactosemia

(D) Tay Sachs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Huntington’s disease is a rare, inherited disease that causes the progressive breakdown (degeneration) of nerve cells in the brain. Huntington’s disease has a wide impact on a person’s functional abilities and usually results in movement, thinking (cognitive) and psychiatric disorders.

Concept note-2: -Huntington’s disease (HD) is an inherited disorder that causes nerve cells (neurons) in parts of the brain to gradually break down and die. The disease attacks areas of the brain that help to control voluntary (intentional) movement, as well as other areas.

Concept note-3: -Huntington’s disease is autosomal dominant, meaning inheritance of just a single copy of an abnormal chromosome from a biological parent is enough to cause it. If one parent carries the abnormal gene, each of their biological children has a 50 percent chance of Huntington’s disease inheritance.

Concept note-4: -Huntington’s disease is caused by a faulty gene that results in parts of the brain becoming gradually damaged over time. You’re usually only at risk of developing it if one of your parents has or had it. Both men and women can get it.

Concept note-5: -The parts of the brain affected include the areas called the basal ganglia and cerebral cortex. These inter-connected areas are associated with different types of activity including movement, learning, thinking, planning, motivation and emotion.
