GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
Each human somatic cell contains how many alleles for a given trait?

(A) 1

(B) ** 2

(C) 23

(D) it depends on their genotype

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Somatic cells contain two alleles for every gene, with one allele provided by each parent of an organism.

Concept note-2: -An individual inherits two alleles, one from each parent, for any given genomic location where such variation exists. If the two alleles are the same, the individual is homozygous for that allele. If the alleles are different, the individual is heterozygous.

Concept note-3: -In sexually reproducing organisms, the number of chromosomes in the body (somatic) cells typically is diploid (2n; a pair of each chromosome), twice the haploid (1n) number found in the sex cells, or gametes. The haploid number is produced during meiosis.

Concept note-4: -Number of genotypes for a given number of alleles Given n alleles at a locus, the number genotypes possible is the sum of the integers between 1 and n: With 2 alleles, the number of genotypes is 1 + 2 = 3.

Concept note-5: -Every individual has two copies, or alleles, or a single gene. When the alleles are the same, they are known as homozygotes. When they are different, they are called heterozygotes. Homozygotes code for the same trait, for instance, blue eyes.
