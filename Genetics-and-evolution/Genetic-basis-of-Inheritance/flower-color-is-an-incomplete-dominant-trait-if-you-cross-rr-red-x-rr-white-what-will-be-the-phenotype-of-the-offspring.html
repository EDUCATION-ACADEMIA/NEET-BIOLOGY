GENETIC BASIS OF INHERITANCE

[SOURCES]
Flower color is an INCOMPLETE dominant trait. If you cross RR (red) x rr (white), what will be the phenotype of the offspring?

(A) Rr

(B) RR

(C) Red

(D) ** Pink

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What phenotype(s) would be seen in the first generation if flower color exhibited incomplete dominance? Explanation: The genotypes of the offspring can be determined by crossing the red flowers, RR, with the white flowers, rr. Offspring: all offspring are Rr.

Concept note-2: -The allele for red flowers is incompletely dominant over the allele for white flowers. Red + white = pink. The results of a cross where the alleles are incompletely dominant can still be predicted, just as with complete dominant and recessive crosses.

Concept note-3: -Explanation: The genotype for the pink flower is Rr and the genotype for the white flower is rr. This would lead to a 50% chance of the offspring having a phenotype of pink.

Concept note-4: -The crossing of a red flowered plant and a white flowered plant produces all the offspring with pink flowers. This cross illustrates red and white exhibit incomplete dominance. This situation occurs due to the blending of both the alleles.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: Answer is 50%. 50% of the progenies will have pink flowers.
