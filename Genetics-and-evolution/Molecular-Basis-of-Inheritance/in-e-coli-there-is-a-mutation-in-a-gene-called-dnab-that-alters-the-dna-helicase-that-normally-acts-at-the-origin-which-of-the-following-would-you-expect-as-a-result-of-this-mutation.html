In E. coli, there is a mutation in a gene called dnaB that alters the DNA helicase that normally acts at the origin. Which of the following would you expect as a result of this mutation?

(A) Replication will occur via RNA polymerase alone.

(B) The DNA will supercoil.

(C) ** No replication fork will be formed.

(D) No proofreading will occur.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -coli, there is a mutation in a gene called dnaB that alters the helicase that normally acts at the origin of replication. Which of the following events would you expect to occur as a result of this mutation? No replication fork will be formed.

Concept note-2: -In Escherichia coli, the DnaB helicase forms the basis for the assembly of the DNA replication complex. The stability of DnaB at the replication fork is likely important for successful replication initiation and progression.

Concept note-3: -Genetic and biochemical analyses indicate that the Escherichia coli dnaB replication protein functions in the propagation of replication forks in the bacterial chromosome.

Concept note-4: -The E. coli DnaB helicase is a hexameric protein comprised of six 52 kDa monomers (3–6). DnaB is a multifunctional enzyme that controls the formation and translocation of the replisome in E. coli and phage DNA replication.

Concept note-5: -Initiating new rounds of Escherichia coli chromosome replication requires DnaA-ATP to unwind the replication origin, oriC, and load DNA helicase.
