If the sequence of the 5’-3’ strand is AATGCTAC, then the complementary sequence has the following sequence:

(A) 3’-AATGCTAC-5’

(B) 3’-CATCGTAA-5’

(C) ** 3’-TTACGATG-5’

(D) 3’-GTAGCATT-5’

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Because of the complimentary base pairing rule, if the strand sequence is AATGCTAC, the complementary sequence will be TTACGATG. As a result, option (c) is right.

Concept note-2: -The sequence of the mentioned DNA strand is 5’-ATCAGC-3’. So, the sequence of the complementary strand will be 3’-TAGTCG-5’.

Concept note-3: -Adenine makes the double bond with thymine while guanine makes the triple bond with cytosine which means adenine and thymine are complementary base pairs and cytosine and guanine are complementary base pairs. The complementary sequence for the DNA strand 5’ ATCGAACGT 3’ is 3’ TAGCTTGCA 5’.

Concept note-4: -DNA is always synthesized in the 5’-to-3’ direction, meaning that nucleotides are added only to the 3’ end of the growing strand.

Concept note-5: -So, the sequence of the complimentary strand in 5’ to 3’ direction is 5’-GCATGCATGCATGCATGCATGCATGCAT− 3’. Was this answer helpful?
