Which nitrogen base does RNA have that DNA does not?

(A) Thymine

(B) Adenine

(C) ** Uracil

(D) Cytosine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Uracil is the nitrogenous base present only in RNA, but not in DNA. Thymine is in DNA. DNA have thymine, guanine, adenine and cytosine. Thymine is replaced by uracil in RNA.

Concept note-2: -Uracil (U) is one of the four nucleotide bases in RNA, with the other three being adenine (A), cytosine (C) and guanine (G). In RNA, uracil pairs with adenine. In a DNA molecule, the nucleotide thymine (T) is used in place of uracil.

Concept note-3: -Nitrogen base not found in DNA is uracil. It is found only in RNA.

Concept note-4: -In RNA, however, a base called uracil (U) replaces thymine (T) as the complementary nucleotide to adenine (Figure 3).

Concept note-5: -RNA consists of four nitrogenous bases: adenine, cytosine, uracil, and guanine.
