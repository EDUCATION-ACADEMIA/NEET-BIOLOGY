A nucleoside differs from a nucleotide. It lacks the:

(A) base

(B) sugar

(C) ** phosphate group

(D) hydroxyl group

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A nucleoside differs from a nucleotide in that it lacks the phosphate group.

Concept note-2: â€“A nucleoside comprises the sugar bound to the nitrogenous base. A nucleotide, however, is a nucleoside bound to a phosphate group. Therefore, the correct answer is (A) Phosphate.

Concept note-3: -The main difference lies in their molecular composition as Nucleosides contain only sugar and a base whereas Nucleotides contain sugar, base and a phosphate group as well. A nucleotide is what occurs before RNA and DNA, while the nucleoside occurs before the nucleotide itself.

Concept note-4: -A nucleoside consists of a nitrogenous base covalently attached to a sugar (ribose or deoxyribose) but without the phosphate group. A nucleotide consists of a nitrogenous base, a sugar (ribose or deoxyribose) and one to three phosphate groups.

Concept note-5: -Nucleosides are glycosylamines that can be thought of as nucleotides without a phosphate group.
