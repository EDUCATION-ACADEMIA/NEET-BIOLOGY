In DNA, the two purines are ____, and the two pyrimidines are ____

(A) cytosine and thymine ____ adenine and guanine

(B) adenine and thymine ____ cytosine and guanine

(C) adenine and cytosine ____ guanine and thymine

(D) ** adenine and guanine ____ cytosine and thymine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -While they are similar in many aspects, there are so many key differences between them. So, the correct answer is option B. Note: Adenine and guanine are purines while cytosine, thymine and uracil are pyrimidines.

Concept note-2: -The purines in DNA are adenine and guanine, the same as in RNA. The pyrimidines in DNA are cytosine and thymine; in RNA, they are cytosine and uracil.

Concept note-3: -Purines. Adenine and guanine are found in both DNA and RNA.

Concept note-4: -Thus, the pyrimidine bases present in DNA are thymine and cytosine.

Concept note-5: -Purine and pyrimidine are fundamental components of nucleotides in DNA. There are three pyrimidines cytosine, thymine, and uracil and two purines adenine and guanine.
