Why does the DNA double helix have a uniform diameter?

(A) ** Purines pair with pyrimidines.

(B) C nucleotides pair with A nucleotides.

(C) Deoxyribose sugars bind with ribose sugars.

(D) Nucleotides bind with nucleoside triphosphates.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The diameter of the DNA double helix is uniform throughout because a purine (two rings) always pairs with a pyrimidine (one ring) and their combined lengths are always equal.

Concept note-2: -The diameter of the DNA double helix is 2 nm and is uniform throughout. Only the pairing between a purine and pyrimidine can explain the uniform diameter.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: Purine always pairs with pyrimidines due to its structural properties. The structure of purines allows them to make hydrogen bonds with pyrimidines. Adenine bonds with thymine because both have two binding sites, so they make double hydrogen bonds.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: Purines pair with pyrimidines because they both contain nitrogenous bases which means that both molecules have complementary structures that make up DNA. Purines have a double-ringed structure with a six-membered ring combined with a five-membered ring.

Concept note-5: -Solution : the diameter of the strand is always constant due to a pairing of purine (adenine and guanine) and pyrimidine (cytosine and thymine ). This specific bonding gives uniform width to the DNA.
