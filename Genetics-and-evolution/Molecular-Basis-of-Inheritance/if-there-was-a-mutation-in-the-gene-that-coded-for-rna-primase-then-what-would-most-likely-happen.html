If there was a mutation in the gene that coded for RNA primase, then what would most likely happen?

(A) RNA bases would start to be found in the DNA

(B) DNA wouldn’t unwind

(C) The lagging strand would elongate, but the leading strand wouldn’t

(D) ** DNA polymerase would be unable to initiate replication

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mutation of DNA primase causes extensive apoptosis of retinal neurons through the activation of DNA damage checkpoint and tumor suppressor p53. Development.

Concept note-2: -In the case of a modified or mutated primase, DNA polymerase III would be incapable of producing a complementary strand; thus, RNA primers can bind to any incorrect DNA, which is why DNA replication becomes inefficient.

Concept note-3: -Defective DNA Polymerase -Primase Leads to X-Linked Intellectual Disability Associated with Severe Growth Retardation, Microcephaly, and Hypogonadism.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer: The consequences to DNA synthesis if primase (the enzyme that lays down the RNA primer) were nonfunctional C. Lagging strand synthesis would be incomplete; leading strand synthesis would be unaffected.
