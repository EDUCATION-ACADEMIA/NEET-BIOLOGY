A bacterial cell was transformed with a recombinant DNA that was generated using a human gene. However, the transformed cells did not produce the desired protein. Reasons could be

(A) ** human gene may have intron which bacteria cannot process

(B) amino acid codons for humans and bacteria are different

(C) human protein is formed but degraded by bacteria

(D) all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, the correct answer is ‘Human gene may have intron which bacteria cannot process’

Concept note-2: -Recombinant DNA is a technology scientists developed that made it possible to insert a human gene into the genetic material of a common bacterium. This “recombinant” micro-organism could now produce the protein encoded by the human gene.

Concept note-3: -Transformation is a key step in DNA cloning. It occurs after restriction digest and ligation and transfers newly made plasmids to bacteria. After transformation, bacteria are selected on antibiotic plates. Bacteria with a plasmid are antibiotic-resistant, and each one will form a colony.

Concept note-4: -Transduction occurs when foreign DNA or RNA is introduced into bacterial or eukaryotic cells via a virus or viral vector. One example are bacteriophages that attach to bacterial membranes and inject their genetic material into the cell.

Concept note-5: -If transformation is successful, the recombinant DNA is integrated into one of the chromosomes of the cell. Genetic engineering has spurred the growth of biotechnology, which is a new industry that is changing the way we interact with the living world.
