What type of science allows us to alter the DNA of a bacterial cell to produce human insulin?

(A) Geology

(B) Chemistry

(C) Biology

(D) ** Biotechnology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Recombinant DNA is a technology scientists developed that made it possible to insert a human gene into the genetic material of a common bacterium. This “recombinant” micro-organism could now produce the protein encoded by the human gene. Scientists build the human insulin gene in the laboratory.

Concept note-2: -Insulin produced by recombinant DNA technology. The gene of interest (ie. Insulin coding gene) is isolated from -cell and inserted in opened plasmid. This recombined plasmid is inserted into suitable host cell (ie E. coli) and now this recombined host cell starts producing insulin hormone.

Concept note-3: -Recombinant human insulin was one of the first products of biotechnology. It was developed in response to the need for a consistent and sufficient worldwide supply. Recombinant human insulin replaced the animal insulins and semisynthetic insulins obtained by modification of animal insulins.

Concept note-4: -The genetic engineering process The gene for human insulin is inserted into the gap in the plasmid. This plasmid is now genetically modified. The genetically modified plasmid is introduced into a new bacteria or yeast cell. This cell then divides rapidly and starts making insulin.
