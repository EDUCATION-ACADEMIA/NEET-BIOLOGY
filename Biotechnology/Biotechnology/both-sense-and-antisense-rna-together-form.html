Both sense and antisense RNA together form

(A) ** dsRNA

(B) ssRNA

(C) cRNA

(D) siRNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When the vector is present in a cell with both T7 and T3 RNA polymerase, both an antisense and a sense transcript are transcribed for each of the clones. The two strands spontaneously anneal to form dsRNA, which then activates RNAi. Each clone in the library must have two different promoters flanking the coding region.

Concept note-2: -The production of double-stranded RNA (dsRNA) in eukaryotic cells, generally as the result of viral replication or the transcription of transposable elements and repetitive DNA sequences, is known to elicit two types of cellular defense responses.

Concept note-3: -During protein productions, the two DNA strands temporarily separate. The antisense strand then becomes a template for making messenger RNA, and the resulting messenger RNA is then a sense messenger RNA that, essentially, matches the sense DNA strand.

Concept note-4: -Abstract. Double-stranded RNA (dsRNA) is associated with most viral infections-it either constitutes the viral genome (in the case of dsRNA viruses) or is generated in host cells during viral replication.

Concept note-5: -There is ample evidence that cells of higher eukaryotes express double-stranded RNA molecules (dsRNAs) either naturally or as the result of viral infection or aberrant, bidirectional transcriptional readthrough. These duplex molecules can exist in either the cytoplasmic or nuclear compartments.
