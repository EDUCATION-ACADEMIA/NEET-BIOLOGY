Which of these has to do with genetic modification?

(A) Crossbreeding

(B) Kidney surgery

(C) ** Gene splicing

(D) use of herbicides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A genetically modified organism (GMO) is an organism whose genetic structure has been altered by adding a gene that will express a desirable trait. This is often referred to as ‘gene splicing’.

Concept note-2: -The process by which introns, the noncoding regions of genes, are excised out of the primary messenger RNA transcript, and the exons (i.e., coding regions) are joined together to generate mature messenger RNA. The latter serves as the template for synthesis of a specific protein.

Concept note-3: -The key steps involved in genetic engineering are identifying a trait of interest, isolating that trait, inserting that trait into a desired organism, and then propagating that organism.
