With which kingdoms do bacteria share the same genetic code?

(A) ** animal, plant, fungus and protoctist

(B) animal, plant and fungus only

(C) animal and plant only

(D) animal only

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A new study reveals that viruses share genes across the three superkingdoms of life, from the single-celled microbes known as bacteria and archaea, to eukarya, a group that includes animals, plants, fungi and all other living things. Most of this unusual sharing occurs between eukarya and bacteria and their viruses.

Concept note-2: -Exceptions to the Code Bacteria and some related microorganisms called the Archeans have exceptions to the standard genetic code, but the bulk of their genetic code is the same one as humans.

Concept note-3: -True. All cells use the same genetic code. They transcribe their DNA to RNA, translate RNA to proteins using ribosomes.

Concept note-4: -Monera kingdom This is the kingdom of microscopic living things and groups together the prokaryotes (archaea and bacteria). This group is present in all habitats and is made up of single-cell things with no defined nucleus.
