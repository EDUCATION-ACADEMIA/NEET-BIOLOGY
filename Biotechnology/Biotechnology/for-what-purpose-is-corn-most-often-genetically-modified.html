For what purpose is corn most often genetically modified?

(A) color change

(B) ** disease resistance

(C) slower growth

(D) shorter growing season

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most GMO corn is created to resist insect pests or tolerate herbicides.

Concept note-2: -Another benefit of Bt maize is reduced occurrence of ear molds (Figure 1b). Because insect damage provides a site for infection by molds, Bt-protected maize can have lower levels of toxins produced by molds (i.e., mycotoxins), especially fumonisin and deoxynivalenol (Dowd 2000, Munkvold et al.

Concept note-3: -A donor organism may be a bacterium, fungus or even another plant. In the case of Bt corn, the donor organism is a naturally occurring soil bacterium, Bacillus thuringiensis, and the gene of interest produces a protein that kills Lepidoptera larvae, in particular, European corn borer.

Concept note-4: -Some 90 percent of the American field corn crop is genetically engineered to resist herbicides or to produce a protein derived from bacillus thringiensis bacteria that can kill certain insect pests such as the southwestern corn borer (USDA 2013).

Concept note-5: -Tastier food. Disease-and drought-resistant plants that require fewer environmental resources (such as water and fertilizer) Less use of pesticides. Increased supply of food with reduced cost and longer shelf life.
