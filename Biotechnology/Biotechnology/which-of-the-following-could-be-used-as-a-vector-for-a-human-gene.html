Which of the following could be used as a vector for a human gene?

(A) ** Bacterial Plasmid

(B) Bacteria

(C) A mitochondria

(D) mRNA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, the correct answer is BAC and YAC.

Concept note-2: -Certain viruses are used as vectors because they can deliver the material by infecting the cell. The viruses are modified so they canâ€™t cause disease when used in people. Some types of virus, such as retroviruses, integrate their genetic material (including the new gene) into a chromosome in the human cell.

Concept note-3: -Usually a retrovirus is used as a vector for introducing a DNA fragment in human cells like lymphocytes.

Concept note-4: -Plasmids that are used experimentally for these purposes are called vectors. Researchers can insert DNA fragments or genes into a plasmid vector, creating a so-called recombinant plasmid. This plasmid can be introduced into a bacterium by way of the process called transformation.

Concept note-5: -Artificial plasmids are widely used as vectors in molecular cloning, serving to drive the replication of recombinant DNA sequences within host organisms. In the laboratory, plasmids may be introduced into a cell via transformation. Synthetic plasmids are available for procurement over the internet.
