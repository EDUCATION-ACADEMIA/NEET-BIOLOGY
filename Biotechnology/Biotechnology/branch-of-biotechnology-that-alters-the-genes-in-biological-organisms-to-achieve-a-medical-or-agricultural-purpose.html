BIOTECHNOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Branch of biotechnology that alters the genes in biological organisms to achieve a medical or agricultural purpose.

(A) ** genetic modification

(B) biotechnology

(C) engineering

(D) bioremediation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gene editing, based on a technology known as CRISPR-Cas9, allows researchers to customize a living organism’s genetic sequence by making very specific changes to its DNA.

Concept note-2: -Agricultural biotechnology is a range of tools, including traditional breeding techniques, that alter living organisms, or parts of organisms, to make or modify products; improve plants or animals; or develop microorganisms for specific agricultural uses.

Concept note-3: -The main subfields of biotechnology are medical (red) biotechnology, agricultural (green) biotechnology, industrial (white) biotechnology, marine (blue) biotechnology, food biotechnology, and environmental biotechnology (Fig. 1.1.).

Concept note-4: -A genetically modified organism (GMO) is an organism in which the genetic material has been changed through biotechnology in a way that does not occur naturally by multiplication and/or natural recombination; cloned animals are included within this definition.
