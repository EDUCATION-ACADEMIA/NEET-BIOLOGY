How can gel electrophoresis be used for identification of individuals when over 99 percent of human DNA is the same?

(A) Each person’s DNA will band in different colors in the gel tray

(B) The 99 percent of the DNA that is the same will reflect UV light differently

(C) ** It focuses only on the small regions of the DNA which are known to vary among individuals.

(D) Experts can determine the different genes by reading the genetic alphabet displayed in the gel.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How can gel electrophoresis be used for identification of individuals when over 99 percent of human DNA is the same? It focuses only on the small regions of the DNA which are known to vary among individuals. Experts can determine the different genes by reading the genetic alphabet displayed in the gel.

Concept note-2: -Gel electrophoresis of DNA fragments plays a central role in the diagnosis of hereditary disease. Electrophoretic separation of differently sized fragments enables the characterization or typing of normal variants which are known to be genetically linked to disease genes.

Concept note-3: -Gel electrophoresis is used to isolate, identify, and characterize properties of DNA fragments in many different situations and at many different points during the cloning process. A small amount of DNA can be loaded into a well at one end of a gel in an apparatus that allows a current to be run through the gel.

Concept note-4: -A transgenic, or genetically modified, organism is one that has been altered through recombinant DNA technology, which involves either the combining of DNA from different genomes or the insertion of foreign DNA into a genome.

Concept note-5: -With genetic engineering, however, any gene from any organism can be transferred to a different organism. Thus, that allows a snippet of DNA that codes for an insecticidal protein from a bacterium, such as Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt), to be transferred into a corn or cotton plant.
