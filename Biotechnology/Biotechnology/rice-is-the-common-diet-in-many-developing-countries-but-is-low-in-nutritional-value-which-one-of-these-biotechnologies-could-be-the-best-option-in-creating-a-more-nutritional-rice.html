BIOTECHNOLOGY

Rice is the common diet in many developing countries, but is low in nutritional value. Which one of these biotechnologies could be the best option in creating a more nutritional rice?

(A) selective breeding of current rice plants

(B) cloning of current rice plants

(C) using a nitrogen rich fertilizer

(D) ** genetic modification of current rice plants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Golden rice is genetically modified to produce beta-carotene, which is not normally present in rice. Beta-carotene is converted into vitamin A when metabolized by the human body. We need vitamin A for healthier skin, immune systems, and vision.

Concept note-2: -Rice is a source of protein and contains various vitamins, such as thiamin and niacin, and minerals, such as zinc and phosphorus.

Concept note-3: -Rice provides more than 15 essential vitamins and minerals including, folic acid, B vitamins, potassium, magnesium, selenium, fiber, iron, and zinc.

Concept note-4: -Conclusion: -Carotene derived from Golden Rice is effectively converted to vitamin A in humans.
