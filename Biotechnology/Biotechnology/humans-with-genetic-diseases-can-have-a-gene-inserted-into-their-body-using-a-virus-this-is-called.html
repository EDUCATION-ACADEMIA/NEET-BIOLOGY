BIOTECHNOLOGY

[SOURCES]
Humans with genetic diseases can have a gene inserted into their body using a virus. This is called:

(A) ** Gene therapy

(B) Cloning

(C) DNA fingerprinting

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gene therapy is a technique that modifies a person’s genes to treat or cure disease. Gene therapies can work by several mechanisms: Replacing a disease-causing gene with a healthy copy of the gene. Inactivating a disease-causing gene that is not functioning properly.

Concept note-2: -There are basically three types of gene therapy: ex vivo, in vivo, and in situ.

Concept note-3: -Viruses are currently the most commonly used vectors in gene therapies because they have a natural ability to deliver genetic material into cells. Before a virus can be used to carry therapeutic genes into human cells, it is modified to remove its ability to cause infectious disease.

Concept note-4: -Gene therapy is when DNA is introduced into a patient to treat a genetic disease. The new DNA usually contains a functioning gene to correct the effects of a disease-causing mutation.

Concept note-5: -Gene therapy is of two types. They are somatic gene therapy and germline gene therapy. In somatic gene therapy, we transfer a gene into an ordinary cell of the body. But in germline gene therapy we introduce a gene to a germ cell.
