Which chemical reaction is catalysed by maltase?

(A) glycogen → glucose + maltose

(B) ** maltose → glucose + glucose

(C) maltose → starch

(D) starch → glucose + maltose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Maltase is an economically valuable enzyme that is used to catalyze the hydrolytic process of maltose and yields d-glucose as a product.

Concept note-2: -Therefore, from the above explanations we can see that the reaction catalysed by enzyme ‘Maltase’ is Maltose → ⁡ Glucose . So, the correct answer is “Option B”.

Concept note-3: -Maltose is converted to two molecules of glucose by the enzyme maltase, which hydrolyzes the glycosidic bond.

Concept note-4: -The enzyme maltase converts maltose into glucose. As we know the basic pH of the duodenum is basic, so enzyme maltase will convert the maltose into glucose at a Ph of more than 7.
