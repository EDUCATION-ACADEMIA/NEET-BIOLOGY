RNA interference helps in

(A) Cell proliferation

(B) Micropropagation

(C) ** Cell defence

(D) Cell differentiation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -RNA interference (RNAi) is an important defence against viruses and transposable elements (TEs). RNAi not only protects against viruses by degrading viral RNA, but hosts and viruses can also use RNAi to manipulate each otherâ€™s gene expression, and hosts can encode microRNAs that target viral sequences.

Concept note-2: -RNAi takes place in all eukaryotic organisms as a method of cellular defence. This method involves silencing of a specific mRNA due to a complementary dsRNA (infection by viruses or mobile genetic elements called transposons) molecule that binds to and prevents translation of the mRNA (silencing).

Concept note-3: -RNA interference (RNAi) or Post-Transcriptional Gene Silencing (PTGS) is a conserved biological response to double-stranded RNA that mediates resistance to both endogenous parasitic and exogenous pathogenic nucleic acids, and regulates the expression of protein-coding genes.

Concept note-4: -RNAi is a natural process by which cells use short, double-stranded RNAs (dsRNAs) to recognize messenger RNAs (mRNAs) with exquisite specificity, leading to their enzymatic destruction and preventing their translation into a protein. Therefore, they inhibit gene function.
