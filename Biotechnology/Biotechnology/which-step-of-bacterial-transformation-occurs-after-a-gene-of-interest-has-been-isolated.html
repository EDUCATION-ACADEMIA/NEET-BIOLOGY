Which step of bacterial transformation occurs after a gene of interest has been isolated?

(A) removing the plasmid from the bacterium

(B) inserting the plasmid into a bacterium

(C) inserting the gene of the parent generation into the plasmid

(D) ** cutting the plasmid with restriction enzymes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a typical cloning experiment, researchers first insert a piece of DNA, such as a gene, into a circular piece of DNA called a plasmid. This step uses restriction enzymes and DNA ligase and is called a ligation. After a ligation, the next step is to transfer the DNA into bacteria in a process called transformation.

Concept note-2: -A small section is then cut out of the circular plasmid by restriction enzymes, ‘molecular scissors’. The gene for human insulin is inserted into the gap in the plasmid. This plasmid is now genetically modified. The genetically modified plasmid is introduced into a new bacteria or yeast cell.

Concept note-3: -Key steps in the process of bacterial transformation: (1) competent cell preparation, (2) transformation of cells, (3) cell recovery, and (4) cell plating.

Concept note-4: -Describe the role of restriction enzymes in the process of transformation. Restriction enzymes are used to cut the DNA of both the organism with the desired gene and the plasmid. This allows the fusion of the nitrogen base pairs of the two DNA segments.
