Which of these is a problem without having the pasteurization of milk?

(A) Diabetes

(B) Smallpox

(C) ** Food-borne illness

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Raw milk is milk that has not been pasteurized to kill harmful bacteria. Raw milk can carry harmful germs, such as Campylobacter, Cryptosporidium, E. coli, Listeria, Brucella, and Salmonella. These germs can pose serious health risks to you and your family.

Concept note-2: -Raw milk is milk from cows, sheep, and goats-or any other animal-that has not been pasteurized to kill harmful bacteria. Raw milk can carry dangerous bacteria such as Salmonella, E. coli, Listeria, Campylobacter, and others that cause foodborne illness, often called “food poisoning.”

Concept note-3: -Most foodborne illness is caused by bacteria in your food. Irradiation and pasteurization kill the bacteria before you buy the food.

Concept note-4: -Pasteurization Destroys Beneficial Bacteria and Enzymes. All the living food in raw milk–delicate enzymes, probiotic bacteria, and various other nutrients-is bombed with extreme high heat and left for dead. Left in its wake is a trail of lost vitamins and minerals, altered flavor and texture and denatured proteins.

Concept note-5: -The health of dairy animals is a very important parameter because a number of diseases including brucellosis, Q-fever, salmonellosis, staphylococcal and streptococcal infections and foot and mouth disease virus may be transmitted to man through milk.
