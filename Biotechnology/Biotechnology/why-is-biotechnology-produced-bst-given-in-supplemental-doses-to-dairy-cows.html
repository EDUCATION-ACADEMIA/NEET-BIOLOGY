Why is biotechnology-produced BST given in supplemental doses to dairy cows?

(A) increase muscle for meat

(B) produce only female offspring

(C) produce large calves

(D) ** increase milk production

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is known that cows receiving supplemental BST consume more feed. Additional feed consumption and milk production are related to the amount of BST administered. Therefore, BST stimulates the cow to produce more milk, which results in increased intake to provide the extra energy required.

Concept note-2: -Why is biotechnologically produced BST given in supplemental doses to dairy cows? To increase milk production.

Concept note-3: -rBST is carried to the liver where it stimulates that organ to produce Insulin-like Growth Factor (IGF-1), a protein hormone that aids in turning nutrients to milk. IGF-1 is carried to a cowâ€™s mammary gland where it stimulates milk production. Studies have found that rBST can increase milk production 10% to 20%.

Concept note-4: -Bovine somatotropin (bST), also known as bovine growth hormone, is an animal drug approved by FDA to increase milk production in dairy cows.

Concept note-5: -In addition to increasing milk yield, bST increases the efficiency of lactation. Cows treated with bST increase feed intake over the first few weeks to match increased nutrient demands for milk synthesis and thus cows remain in neutral or positive energy balance during the majority of lactation.
