Which is a hypothesis a scientist could test using principles of biotechnology?

(A) How long can a wheat plant live without water?

(B) What are the effects of extreme cold on wheat plants?

(C) ** Can wheat be genetically modified to be resistant to mold?

(D) Which variety of wheat tastes best when made into pasta?

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is a concern of scientists when genetically modifying plants? There will be a decrease in biodiversity.

Concept note-2: -GM is a technology that involves inserting DNA into the genome of an organism. To produce a GM plant, new DNA is transferred into plant cells. Usually, the cells are then grown in tissue culture where they develop into plants.

Concept note-3: -A small piece of circular DNA called a plasmid? is extracted from the bacteria or yeast cell. A small section is then cut out of the circular plasmid by restriction enzymes, ‘molecular scissors’. The gene for human insulin is inserted into the gap in the plasmid. This plasmid is now genetically modified.
