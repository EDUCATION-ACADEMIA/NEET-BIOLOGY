EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gel electrophoresis is a technique used to separate DNA fragments according to their size. DNA samples are loaded into wells (indentations) at one end of a gel, and an electric current is applied to pull them through the gel. DNA fragments are negatively charged, so they move towards the positive electrode.

Concept note-2: -Electrophoresis Electrophoresis is a laboratory technique used to separate DNA, RNA or protein molecules based on their size and electrical charge.

Concept note-3: -• The separated DNA fragments can be seen only after staining DNA with a compound ethidium bromide (EtBr), as bright orange coloured bands, on exposure to UV radiations. • The separated bands of DNA are cut out from the agarose gel and extracted from the gel piece. This step is called as elution.

Concept note-4: -To separate DNA using agarose gel electrophoresis, the DNA is loaded into pre-cast wells in the gel and a current applied. The phosphate backbone of the DNA (and RNA) molecule is negatively charged, therefore when placed in an electric field, DNA fragments will migrate to the positively charged anode.
