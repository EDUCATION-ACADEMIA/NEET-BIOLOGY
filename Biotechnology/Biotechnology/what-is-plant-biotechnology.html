What is plant biotechnology?

(A) Adding chemicals to foods to preserve them.

(B) Making synthetic foods using artificial, manmade proteins.

(C) ** Inserting a gene with a beneficial trait into a plant cell.

(D) Taking immature plants and adding steroids to make them grow bigger and stronger.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Transgenic plants are plants that have had their genomes modified through genetic engineering techniques either by the addition of a foreign gene or removal of a certain detrimental gene [1]. A foreign gene inserted into a plant can be of a different species or even kingdom.

Concept note-2: -GM is a technology that involves inserting DNA into the genome of an organism. To produce a GM plant, new DNA is transferred into plant cells. Usually, the cells are then grown in tissue culture where they develop into plants. The seeds produced by these plants will inherit the new DNA.

Concept note-3: -Gene Transfer: The introduction of new DNA into an existing organismâ€™s cell, usually by vectors such as plasmids and modified viruses. Cells may be modified ex vivo for subsequent administration to humans, or may be altered in vivo by gene therapy given directly to the subject.

Concept note-4: -Plant biotechnology can be defined as the use of tissue culture and genetic engineering techniques to produce genetically modified plants that exhibit new or improved desirable characteristics.

Concept note-5: -They can also control the way in which these genes express themselves in the new variety of plant or animal. In short, by homing in on desired traits, molecular gene transfer can shorten the breeding time for new varieties and, in addition, lead to improvements not possible by traditional breeding.
