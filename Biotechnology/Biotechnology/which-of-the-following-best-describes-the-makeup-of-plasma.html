Which of the following best describes the makeup of plasma?

(A) ** Mostly water with different amounts of various proteins and platelets.

(B) Equal amounts of platelets, proteins and water.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plasma contains 91% to 92% of water and 8% to 9% of solids. It mainly comprises of: Coagulants, mainly fibrinogen, aid in blood clotting. Plasma proteins, such as albumin and globulin, that help maintain the colloidal osmotic pressure at about 25 mmHg.

Concept note-2: -Plasma. The watery fluid portion of blood (90 percent water) in which the corpuscular elements are suspended. It transports nutrients as well as wastes throughout the body.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: Blood plasma is composed of approximately 91.5% water, 7.0% plasma proteins, and 1.5% solutes. The plasma proteins exert osmotic pressure, which helps maintain fluid balance with the tissues and regulates blood volume. Important plasma proteins include albumin, globulins, and fibrinogen.

Concept note-4: -serum albumin, protein found in blood plasma that helps maintain the osmotic pressure between the blood vessels and tissues. Serum albumin accounts for 55 percent of the total protein in blood plasma.
