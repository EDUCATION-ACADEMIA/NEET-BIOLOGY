bt toxin requires which medium to become active

(A) acidic

(B) ** alkaline

(C) neutral

(D) none

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bt toxins are initially inactive protoxins but after ingestion by the insect these inactive toxins become active due to the alkaline pH of the gut which solubilizes the crystals. Hence, high pH value is required to make Bt toxins active.

Concept note-2: -Once ingested by insects, these crystals are solubilized in the midgut, the toxins are then proteolytically activated by midgut proteases and bind to specific receptors located in the insect cell membrane [5, 7], leading to cell disruption and insect death.

Concept note-3: -Final Answer: Hence, alkaline pH of gut triggers activation of protoxin to active Bt toxin of Bacillus thuringiensis in bollworm.

Concept note-4: -The alkaline pH (8.6 approximately) of the gut in insects triggers activation of protoxin ( inactive crystallized form) to active Bt toxin after ingestion.

Concept note-5: -How is inactive Bt-toxin converted into active form ? When Bt Toxin is ingested by the insect the inactive toxin is converted into active form due to alkaline pH of gut which solubilizes the crystal.
