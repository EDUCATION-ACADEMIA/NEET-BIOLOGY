A DNA fingerprint is

(A) ** unique sequence of repeating units in a section of DNA

(B) a gene inserted into a bcteril chromosome

(C) the complete sequence of an organism’s DNA

(D) a copy of a chromosome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Correct steps or sequence of DNA fingerprinting are: Digestion of DNA by restriction endonuclesase → Separation or electrophoresis of DNA fragments → Blotting of DNA fragments to nitrocellulose membrane → Hybridisation with DNA probes → Detection of hybridised DNA fragments by autoradiography.

Concept note-2: -DNA fingerprinting uses repetitive sequences that are highly variable, called variable number tandem repeats (VNTRs). Modern law enforcement in particular uses short tandem repeats (STRs).

Concept note-3: -Genetic fingerprints They are the same in every cell and retain their distinctiveness throughout a person’s life. Human cells contain 23 chromosomes (packets of DNA) from the father and 23 from the mother. Each DNA strand contains a unique sequence or code of genetic information.

Concept note-4: -Gene Sequencing vs DNA Fingerprinting Gene sequencing is the process that determines the nucleotide sequence of a particular gene or the whole genome. DNA fingerprinting involves a technique where DNA samples of two or more subjects are fragmented and analyzed to determine the identity of a person.
