Which of these is used as a vector to insert new DNA into an organism?

(A) Enzymes

(B) Yeast

(C) ** Viruses

(D) Plants

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -coli bacteriophage is one of the most widely used vectors used to carry recombinant DNA into bacterial cells. This virus makes an excellent vector because about one-third of its genome is considered nonessential, meaning that it can be removed and replaced by foreign DNA (i.e., the DNA being inserted).

Concept note-2: -Researchers can insert DNA fragments or genes into a plasmid vector, creating a so-called recombinant plasmid. This plasmid can be introduced into a bacterium by way of the process called transformation.

Concept note-3: -The most commonly used vectors are plasmids (circular DNA molecules that originated from bacteria), viruses, and yeast cells. Plasmids are not a part of the main cellular genome, but they can carry genes that provide the host cell with useful properties, such as drug resistance, mating ability, and toxin production.

Concept note-4: -In Molecular biology, an insert is a piece of DNA that is inserted into a larger DNA vector by a recombinant DNA technique, such as ligation or recombination. This allows it to be multiplied, selected, further manipulated or expressed in a host organism.

Concept note-5: -Vectors can be a plasmid from the bacterium, a cell from the higher organism or DNA from a virus. The target DNA is inserted into the specific sites of the vector and ligated by DNA ligase. The vector is then transformed into the host cell for replication.
