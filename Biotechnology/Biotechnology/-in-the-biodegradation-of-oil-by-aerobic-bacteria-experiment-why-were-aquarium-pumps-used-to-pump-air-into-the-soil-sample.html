* In the biodegradation of oil by aerobic bacteria experiment, why were aquarium pumps used to pump air into the soil sample?

(A) Because the air keeps the soil from turning to mud.

(B) Because the air keeps the soil dry.

(C) ** Because the air provides the oxygen bacteria need to survive.

(D) To minimize the foul odor created by the soil.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -* In the biodegradation of oil by aerobic bacteria experiment, why were aquarium pumps used to pump air into the soil sample? Because the air keeps the soil from turning to mud.

Concept note-2: -Microorganisms are utilized in bioremediation because of their ability to degrade environmental pollutants due to their metabolism via biochemical pathways related to the organisms activity and growth.

Concept note-3: -Bioremediators are biological agents used for bioremediation in order to clean up contaminated sites. Bacteria, archaea and fungi are typical prime bioremediators [2].

Concept note-4: -Bioremediation is an eco-friendly process of the degradation of toxic pollutants into nontoxic or less hazardous substances mediated by microbial enzymes. Biodegradation of various hazardous pollutants through the application of different enzymes from bacteria and fungi have been widely studied.
