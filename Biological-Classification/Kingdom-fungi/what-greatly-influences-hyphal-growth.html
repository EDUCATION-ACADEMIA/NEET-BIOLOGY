What greatly influences hyphal growth?

(A) ** a food source, presence of water, reproductive units of other fungi

(B) A food source, phototropism and the presence of water

(C) only the presence of water

(D) only phototropism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyphal growth is greatly influenced by stimuli and will grow toward a food source, water, or even toward reproductive units of other fungi. Collectively, a mass of hyphae are referred to as a mycelium.

Concept note-2: -Hyphal tip growth is initiated by establishment of a growth site and the subsequent maintenance of the growth axis, with transport of growth supplies, including membranes and proteins, delivered by motors along the cytoskeleton to the hyphal apex.

Concept note-3: -The basic unit of a filamentous fungus is the hypha, which usually consists of a chain of elongated cells that expand at the apex of the tip cell (10, 41, 115). Hyphal tip growth is characterized by the initial establishment of one growth site, which is followed by its continuous maintenance.

Concept note-4: -The environmental conditions required by the fungi are: warm temperatures, moisture, poor ventilation and the presence of oxygen. Wood fungi will not grow if temperatures are too low (optimum temperature is between 70ยบ and 90ยบ F), the moisture content is less Page 2 72 than 20%, and if oxygen is absent.
