What is true for cyanobacteria?

(A) ** Oxygenic with nitrogenase

(B) Oxygenic without nitorgenase

(C) Non-Oxygenic with nitrognase

(D) Non-Oxygenic without nitrogenase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cyanobacteria are oxygenic photoautotrophs and nitrogen fixers. Thus, they release oxygen during photosynthesis and also contain nitrogenase enzymes.

Concept note-2: -Note: Cyanobacteria are microscopic and rich in chemical diversity and they use photosynthesis to make their own food in the presence of oxygen so they are oxygenic. They are also nitrogenase as they convert inert atmospheric nitrogen into nitrate or ammonia.

Concept note-3: -Thus, the correct answer is ‘Oxygenic with nitrogenase. ‘

Concept note-4: -It is found in fresh water only. It may be unicellular, colonial or filamentous. They often form bloom in non-polluted fresh water bodies. Colonies are not surrounded by gelatinous sheath.

Concept note-5: -Other cyanobacteria use different strategies to protect their nitrogenase from oxygen. Certain filamentous strains form bundles of filaments. In these bundles, only certain filaments will fix dinitrogen, the other filaments somehow shielding the N2-fixing filaments from oxygen.
