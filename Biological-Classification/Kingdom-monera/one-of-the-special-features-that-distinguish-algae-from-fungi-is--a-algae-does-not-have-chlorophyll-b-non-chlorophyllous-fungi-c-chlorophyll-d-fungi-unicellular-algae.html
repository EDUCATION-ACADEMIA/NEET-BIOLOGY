KINGDOM MONERA

[SOURCES]
One of the special features that distinguish algae from fungi is ____ A. algae does not have chlorophyll B. non-chlorophyllous fungi C. chlorophyll D fungi. unicellular algae

(A) non-chlorophyllous algae

(B) ** fungi do not have chlorophyll

(C) chlorophyll fungi

(D) single-celled algae

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Algae contain a pigment called chlorophyll, which induces green color to the plants. Fungi do not contain chlorophyll and hence are not green in color.

Concept note-2: -Algae and fungi differ in their mode of nutrition. Algae are autotrophic. They have chlorophyll and perform photosynthesis. Fungi are heterotrophic, they are dependent on others for their nutrients requirement.

Concept note-3: -Characteristics of Fungi Fungi are eukaryotic, non-vascular, non-motile and heterotrophic organisms. They may be unicellular or filamentous. They reproduce by means of spores. Fungi exhibit the phenomenon of alternation of generation.

Concept note-4: -Characteristics of Algae Algal cells are eukaryotic. For instance, algae can photosynthesize like plants, and they possess specialized structures and cell-organelles, like centrioles and flagella, found only in animals. The algal cell walls consist of mannans, cellulose and Galatians.

Concept note-5: -Algae are plant-like organisms that can be either unicellular or multicellular, and derive energy via photosynthesis. Protozoa are unicellular organisms with complex cell structures; most are motile. Microscopic fungi include molds and yeasts. Helminths are multicellular parasitic worms.
