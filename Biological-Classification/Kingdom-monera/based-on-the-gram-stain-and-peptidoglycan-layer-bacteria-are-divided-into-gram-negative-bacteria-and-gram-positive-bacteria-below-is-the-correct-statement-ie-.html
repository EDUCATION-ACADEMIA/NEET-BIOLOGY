KINGDOM MONERA

Based on the Gram stain and peptidoglycan layer, bacteria are divided into Gram negative bacteria and Gram positive bacteria. Below is the correct statement, i.e. ____

(A) The peptidoglycan of gram-negative bacteria is composed of lipids (fats), while gram-positive bacteria are composed of carbohydrates and proteins

(B) The peptidoglycan of gram negative bacteria is thicker than that of gram positive bacteria

(C) if it is dripped with gram stain, the peptidoglycan of gram positive bacteria will be red

(D) ** peptidoglycan of gram positive bacteria is thicker than gram negative bacteria

(E) if it is dripped with gram stain, the peptidoglycan of gram negative bacteria will turn purple

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gram-negative bacteria are surrounded by a thin peptidoglycan cell wall, which itself is surrounded by an outer membrane containing lipopolysaccharide. Gram-positive bacteria lack an outer membrane but are surrounded by layers of peptidoglycan many times thicker than is found in the Gram-negatives.

Concept note-2: -A Gram stain is colored purple. When the stain combines with bacteria in a sample, the bacteria will either stay purple or turn pink or red. If the bacteria stays purple, they are Gram-positive. If the bacteria turns pink or red, they are Gram-negative.

Concept note-3: -Gram staining is a technique that uses violet dye to distinguish between gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria. If the bacteria are gram-positive, the thick, peptidoglycan layer in their cell walls will retain the dye and they will stain violet.

Concept note-4: -Gram positive bacteria have a thick peptidoglycan cell wall with a single internal plasma membrane. They do not contain LPS but they do contain teichoic and lipoteichoic acid. Gram negative bacteria have a thinner peptidoglycan cell wall with an outer membrane and an inner plasma membrane (dual membrane).
