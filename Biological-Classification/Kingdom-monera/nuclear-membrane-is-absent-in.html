BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM MONERA

[SOURCES]
Nuclear membrane is absent in

(A) penicillium

(B) Agaricus

(C) Volvox

(D) ** Nostoc

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nostoc is a cyanobacterium, i.e., prokaryote, so it lacks true nucleus, thus nuclear membrane is absent.

Concept note-2: -Similar to other forms of bacteria, the single-celled Nostoc does not have a nucleus or other internal membrane systems and the cytoplasm is comprised of between seventy and eighty-five percent water.

Concept note-3: -Complete Answer: Nuclear membrane is absent in kingdom Monera because it lacks well-defined nucleus and other membrane bound cell organelles like mitochondria, Golgi apparatus and chloroplast. Monera are the unicellular, prokaryotic organisms whose cell wall is not made up of cellulose.

Concept note-4: -This organism has no internal membrane bound structures, or organelles, and the genetic material is not found within a nucleus.

Concept note-5: -Volvox is a green alga which is a eukaryotic organism. Penicillium and Agaricus are fungi and are also eukaryotic. Eukaryotic organisms have nuclear membranes. Q.
