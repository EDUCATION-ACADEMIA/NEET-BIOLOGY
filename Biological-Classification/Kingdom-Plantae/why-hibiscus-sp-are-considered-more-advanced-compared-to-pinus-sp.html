Why Hibiscus sp. are considered more advanced compared to Pinus sp.?

(A) ** Xylem consists of vessel and tracheid

(B) Reproduction by producing seeds

(C) Phloem consists of sieve tube

(D) Has true roots, stem and leaves

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How does the cuticle represent and adaptive advantage for early land plants? The cuticle, a waterproof covering, reduces water loss. It allowed early land plants to survive in drier environments.

Concept note-2: -The sporophyte possess a complex of vascular tissue: vascular tissue permits larger size because water can be translocated from the soil to the plant. Vascular tissue.

Concept note-3: -Vascular plants are able to grow taller than nonvascular plants because they have more lignin in their cell walls. 2. They can live in drier areas than nonvascular plants because the cell walls of vascular plants are less permeable to water.

Concept note-4: -Pteridophytes are also called first vascular cryptogam or spore-bearing vascular plants. They are the first terrestrial plants to possess vascular tissues. Xylem transport water and minerals to different parts of the plant body and phloem transports organic food to the plant body.
