KINGDOM PLANTAE

[SOURCES]
The vascular tissue of plants is MOST similar in function to what structures in the human body?

(A) ** The veins, arteries, and capillaries

(B) The bone cells of the skeletal system

(C) The cardiac muscle of the heart

(D) The outer layer of skin tissue

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your heart, your blood, and your blood vessels form your cardiovascular system (cardio = heart; vascular = vessels). Of course plants don’t have hearts, but they do have vessels which transport water, minerals, and nutrients through the plant. These vessels are the vascular tissue.

Concept note-2: -Well, the human circulatory system acts as a transport system to distribute water gasses nutrients to all the cells of the body, and the plant vascular system transport water and nutrients to the plant cells. So this circulatory system is similar to the plant’s vascular tissue system.

Concept note-3: -The circulatory systems in animals and plants are alike because they both serve as transport systems that circulate substances throughout the body of the organism. Both types of circulatory systems move important molecules to the various parts and structures of the body.

Concept note-4: -Which plant tissue system is similar to the human circulatory system? Explanation: A plant’s vascular tissues transport nutrients throughout the plant, just as the circulatory system transports nutrients throughout human bodies.

Concept note-5: -The plant’s vascular tissues are similar to the veins and arteries in anima bodies in that they are both systems of tubes that transport blood and other substances throughout the plant.
