KINGDOM PLANTAE

[SOURCES]
Spore-producing sori are located on the underside of the leaves of a

(A) haploid gametophyte

(B) haploid sporophyte

(C) diploid gametophyte

(D) ** diploid sporophyte

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sori are formed on sporophyte generation, sporangia present in the sori produce haploid meiospores.

Concept note-2: -Sori (singular: sorus) are groups of sporangia (singular: sporangium), which contain spores. Sori are usually found on the underside of the blade. Young sori are commonly covered by flaps of protective tissue called indusia (singular: indusium).

Concept note-3: -Each of the sori contains diploid cells called spore mother cells. Spore mother cells undergo meiosis to produce haploid spores, which are stored in a structure called a sporangium. Remember, a haploid cell, which is often represented as n, contains only one set of chromosomes, half the number of most other body cells.

Concept note-4: -Sori form on the fronds. Each contains many sporangia mounted on stalks. Within each sporangium, the spore mother cells undergo meiosis producing four haploid spores each.

Concept note-5: -The fern sori fern sori fern sori placed under a frond are protected against rain, strong wind, and ultraviolet (UV) rays from the sun that might damage the spores and sporangia. Sporangia spread along all of veins of the lower surface of the fertile frond.
