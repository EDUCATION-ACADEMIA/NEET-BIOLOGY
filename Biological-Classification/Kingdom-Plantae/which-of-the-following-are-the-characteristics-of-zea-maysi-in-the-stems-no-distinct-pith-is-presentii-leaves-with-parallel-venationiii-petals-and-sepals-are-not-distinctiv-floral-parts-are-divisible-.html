Which of the following are the characteristics of Zea mays?I. In the stems, no distinct pith is presentII. Leaves with parallel venationIII. Petals and sepals are not distinctIV. Floral parts are divisible by three

(A) I, II and III

(B) I, II and IV

(C) II, III and IV

(D) ** I, II, III and IV

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant Characteristics Maize (Zea mays) is a tall, deep-rooted, warm weather annual grass. A single long stalk will develop from seed. Long smooth leaves are attached at the stem nodes. Seed producing shoots originate from the base of the main stem.

Concept note-2: -Maize is a monoecious plant with staminate flowers borne in a tassel and pistillate flowers in ears. (A) A pair of staminate flowers each showing a lemma, a palea, and three stamens (the two lodicules are not seen here). (B) A row of pistillate flowers showing one silk (style) each.

Concept note-3: -The seven characteristics of plants are nutrition, respiration, movement, excretion, growth, reproduction, and Sensitivity.

Concept note-4: -It has an erect growth form with usually 1 main stem and 1-2 lateral branches. Large leaves (0.3-1.5 m long, 5-15 cm wide) are linear (straight with parallel sides) to lanceolate (lance-shaped) with a prominent midrib. Each stem produces 8-21 leaves which occur singly on alternating sides of the stem.
