KINGDOM PLANTAE

[SOURCES]
The challenges that faced early land plants included

(A) conserving water.

(B) reproducing on land.

(C) absorbing minerals from the rocky surface.

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are four major challenges to plants living on land: obtaining resources, staying upright, maintaining moisture, and reproducing. Obtaining Resources From Two Places at Once Algae and other aquatic organisms acquire the resources they need from the surrounding water.

Concept note-2: -The life on land presents significant challenges for plants, including the potential for desiccation, mutagenic radiation from the sun, and a lack of buoyancy from the water.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: Some challenges that land plants face compared to life in the water is obtaining nutrients, resisting gravity, and preventing dehydration.

Concept note-4: -The challenges that the first land plants had to overcome going limp on land from gravity, the availability of water meaning that water is not always available on land, and having to receive their nutrients from the ground and light and carbon dioxide from the air.
