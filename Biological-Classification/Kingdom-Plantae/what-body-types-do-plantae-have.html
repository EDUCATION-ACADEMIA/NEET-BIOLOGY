What body types do Plantae have?

(A) all are unicellular

(B) ** all are multicellular

(C) most are unicellular but some are multicellular

(D) most are multicellular but some are unicellular

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plant Kingdom – Plantae Kingdom Plantae includes all the plants. They are eukaryotic, multicellular and autotrophic organisms.

Concept note-2: -The plant body is sporophyte and differentiated into root, stem, and leaves. The plant possesses a well-developed tap root system. In some cases, the roots are symbiotically associated with algae (e.g., Coralloid roots of Cycas) or with fungi (e.g., Mycorrhizal roots of Pinus).

Concept note-3: -Kingdom Plantae is broadly composed of four evolutionarily related groups: bryophytes (mosses), (seedless vascular plants), gymnosperms (cone bearing seed plants), and angiosperms (flowering seed plants).

Concept note-4: -Plantae is the kingdom of plants. Important characteristics of plants are: 1. Plants are multicellular.
