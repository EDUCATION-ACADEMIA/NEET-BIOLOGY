What happens during lysis?

(A) Virus injects DNA

(B) Cell kills virus

(C) Cell makes more viruses

(D) ** Cell bursts and viruses are released

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cell bursting, or lysis, releases hundreds of new phages, which can find and infect other host cells nearby. In this way, a few cycles of lytic infection can let the phage spread like wildfire through a bacterial population.

Concept note-2: -The new viruses burst out of the host cell during a process called lysis, which kills the host cell. Some viruses take a portion of the hostâ€™s membrane during the lysis process to form an envelope around the capsid. Following viral replication, the new viruses may go on to infect new hosts.

Concept note-3: -Viruses can be released from the host cell by lysis, a process that kills the cell by bursting its membrane and cell wall if present. This is a feature of many bacterial and some animal viruses.

Concept note-4: -In the lytic cycle, the phage replicates and lyses the host cell. The third stage of infection is biosynthesis of new viral components. After entering the host cell, the virus synthesizes virus-encoded endonucleases to degrade the bacterial chromosome.

Concept note-5: -Endotoxin is a lipopolysaccharide component of the Gram-negative bacteria cell and is released during active cellular growth and after cell lysis.
