BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

VIRUSES

[SOURCES]
Are 200-300 nm in size

(A) ** Viruses

(B) Bacteria

(C) Both viruses and bacteria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Virus and cell size comparison. Human viruses can vary in size but are generally in the range of 20–200 nm in diameter. In comparison, bacteria are generally 2–3 M in length, and an average human cell is 10–30 M.

Concept note-2: -Most viruses vary in diameter from 20 nanometres (nm; 0.0000008 inch) to 250–400 nm; the largest, however, measure about 500 nm in diameter and are about 700–1, 000 nm in length. Only the largest and most complex viruses can be seen under the light microscope at the highest resolution.

Concept note-3: -Mimivirus is the largest and most complex virus known. Is it an evolutionary bridge between nonliving viruses and living organisms, or is it just an anomaly? Viruses are small and fairly simple.

Concept note-4: -AAV is the smallest DNA virus with an average size of 20 nm. AAV was discovered in 1965 as a defective contaminating virus in an adenovirus stock (Atchison et al., 1965).
