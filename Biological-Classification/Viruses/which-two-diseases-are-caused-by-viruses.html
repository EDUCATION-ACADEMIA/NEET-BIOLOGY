VIRUSES

[SOURCES]
Which TWO diseases are caused by Viruses

(A) ** Measles

(B) Shigella

(C) Salmonella

(D) ** Rubella

(E) Ringworm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rubella (German Measles, Three-Day Measles) Rubella is a contagious disease caused by a virus. Most people who get rubella usually have a mild illness, with symptoms that can include a low-grade fever, sore throat, and a rash that starts on the face and spreads to the rest of the body.

Concept note-2: -Rubella is caused by a virus that’s passed from person to person. It can spread when an infected person coughs or sneezes. It can also spread by direct contact with infected mucus from the nose and throat. It can also be passed on from pregnant women to their unborn children through the bloodstream.

Concept note-3: -Rubella is a contagious disease caused by a virus. It is also called German measles, but it is caused by a different virus than measles.

Concept note-4: -Measles and rubella (also known as “German measles”) are diseases that can lead to serious health complications, or even death. In unvaccinated pregnant women, rubella can lead to miscarriage or multiple birth defects that together are called congenital rubella syndrome (CRS).

Concept note-5: -Rubella, also known as German measles or three-day measles, is an infection caused by the rubella virus. This disease is often mild, with half of people not realizing that they are infected. A rash may start around two weeks after exposure and last for three days.
