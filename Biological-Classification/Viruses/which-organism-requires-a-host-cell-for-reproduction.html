VIRUSES

[SOURCES]
Which organism requires a host cell for reproduction?

(A) bacteria

(B) plant cells

(C) ** viruses

(D) animal cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Viruses, in contrast, use the basic host cell machinery for most aspects of their reproduction: they all depend on host cell ribosomes to produce their proteins, and some also use host cell DNA and RNA polymerases for replication and transcription, respectively.

Concept note-2: -Viruses cannot replicate on their own, but rather depend on their host cellâ€™s protein synthesis pathways to reproduce. This typically occurs by the virus inserting its genetic material in host cells, co-opting the proteins to create viral replicates, until the cell bursts from the high volume of new viral particles.

Concept note-3: -Indeed, viruses have the remarkable ability to spread from one host to another host, hosts that belong to the three cellular domains of life-Archaea, Bacteria, and Eukarya. This constitutes their host range.

Concept note-4: -A virus cannot replicate alone; instead, it must infect cells and use components of the host cell to make copies of itself. Often, a virus ends up killing the host cell in the process, causing damage to the host organism. Well-known examples of viruses causing human disease include AIDS, COVID-19, measles and smallpox.

Concept note-5: -virus. Viruses are microscopic biological agents that invade living hosts and infect their bodies by reproducing within their cell tissue. Viruses are tiny infectious agents that rely on living cells to multiply. They may use an animal, plant, or bacteria host to survive and reproduce.
