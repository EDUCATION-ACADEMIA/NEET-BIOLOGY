BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM ANIMALS

[SOURCES]
Group of cells are loosely arranged and does not form true tissues are known as

(A) ** Parazoa

(B) Eumetazoa

(C) Unicellular

(D) Multicellular

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Parazoa includes only Phylum Porifera. They have a cellular level of organization in which cells are not organized into tissues. Parazoa are animals whose body consists of loosely aggregated cells.

Concept note-2: -In sponges, cells are arranged as loose cell aggregates.

Concept note-3: -Loose aggregates are the phenomenon, in which, cells are dissociated and tend to ungroup themselves with cells of their own type. Loose cells can migrate easily, in contrast to epithelial cells. Example: Tumour cells of loose aggregation can move to other parts and reaggregate themselves and spreads cancer.

Concept note-4: -Animals included in phylum Porifera are Parazoans because they do not show the formation of true tissues (except in class Hexactinellida). These organisms show very simple organization, with a rudimentary endoskeleton. Sponges have multiple cell types that are geared toward executing various metabolic functions.

Concept note-5: -Although sponge is a multicellular animal but the cells do not function in coordination with each other. Most of the cells work independently. Thus, there is no division of labour; as seen in the tissue-level organization.
