Tissue level organization is first seen in:

(A) ** Coelenterates

(B) Platyhelminthes.

(C) Nematahelminthes.

(D) Arthropods.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: â€“First time in the animal kingdom the tissue level of the organization is seen in coelenterates.-Some organ systems are also present in Platyhelminthes.-Arthropods shed a chitinous exoskeleton for allowing the growth and development of the body to give room/space for the growing organs.

Concept note-2: -In Coelenterata and Ctenophora, the arrangement of cells is complex. Here the cells performing similar function are arranged into tissues, hence tissue level of organisation is seen in them.

Concept note-3: -The phylum in which we first see tissue-level organization is Cnidaria, which includes roughly 10, 000 species of animals such as jellyfish and coral. Although very primitive in many ways, cnidarians have cells organized into tissues.
