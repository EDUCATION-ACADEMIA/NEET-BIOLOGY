BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM ANIMALS

[SOURCES]
Have 8 legs, no antennae and no wings, includes:spiders, scorpions, mites, ticks

(A) ** Arachnida (arachnids)

(B) Insecta (insects)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Arachnids are a group of animals that comprises spiders, scorpions, mites, and ticks. Four pairs of legs and also no antennae are just what these all have in general, which separates them from insects.

Concept note-2: -The easiest way to recognise an arachnid is to count the legs. They have eight legs and no wings. Not all arachnids are spiders. Other examples are scorpions, mites, ticks, false scorpions, and harvestmen.

Concept note-3: -Arachnids are spiders, harvestmen, mites and ticks, and their relatives like scorpions that don’t live in Michigan. All arachnids have eight legs, and unlike insects, they don’t have antennae. The bodies of arachnids are divided into two sections, the cephalothorax in front and the abdomen behind.

Concept note-4: -Arachnids include spiders, scorpions, mites and ticks. There are about 60, 000 species of arachnids. Arachnids have eight legs, not six like insects.

Concept note-5: -Arachnida (/əˈræknɪdə/) is a class of joint-legged invertebrate animals (arthropods), in the subphylum Chelicerata. Arachnida includes, among others, spiders, scorpions, ticks, mites, pseudoscorpions, harvestmen, camel spiders, whip spiders and vinegaroons.
