This term refers to the ability of an organism to control their body temperature from within (which means they are warm blooded).

(A) ** Endothermic

(B) Exothermic

(C) Vertebrates

(D) Invertebrates

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -endotherm, so-called warm-blooded animals; that is, those that maintain a constant body temperature independent of the environment. The endotherms primarily include the birds and mammals; however, some fish are also endothermic.

Concept note-2: -Ectotherms are animals that depend on their external environment for body heat, while endotherms are animals that use thermoregulation to maintain a somewhat consistent internal body temperature even when their external environment changes.

Concept note-3: -An ectotherm (reptile/amphibian) relies primarily on its external environment to regulate the temperature of its body.
