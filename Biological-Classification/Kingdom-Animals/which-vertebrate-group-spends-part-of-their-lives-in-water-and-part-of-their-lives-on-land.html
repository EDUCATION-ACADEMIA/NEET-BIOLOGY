Which vertebrate group spends part of their lives in water and part of their lives on land?

(A) Fish

(B) Birds

(C) ** Amphibians

(D) Reptiles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amphibians are a group of animals that spend some part of their life in water as well as on land.

Concept note-2: -Amphibians are vertebrates (animals with backbones) which are able, when adult, to live both in water and on land. Unlike fish, they can breathe atmospheric oxygen through lungs, and they differ from reptiles in that they have soft, moist, usually scale-less skin, and have to breed in water.

Concept note-3: -An amphibian is a cold-blooded vertebrate animal that is born in water and breathes with gills. As the larva grows into its adult form, the animal’s lungs develop the ability to breathe air, and the animal can live on land. Frogs, toads, and salamanders are all amphibians.

Concept note-4: -Amphibians! Amphibians are a group of vertebrates that has adapted to live in both water and on land. Amphibian larvae are born and live in water, and they breathe using gills.

Concept note-5: -Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates (vertebrates have backbones) that don’t have scales. They live part of their lives in water and part on land.
