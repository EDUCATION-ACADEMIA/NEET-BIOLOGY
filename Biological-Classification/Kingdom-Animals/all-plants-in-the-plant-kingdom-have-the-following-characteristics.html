BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM ANIMALS

[SOURCES]
All plants in the plant kingdom have the following characteristics

(A) ** Have chloroplast to make its own food (producers) and multicellular

(B) Consumers and unicellular

(C) Consumers and multicellular

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They make their own food and hence are called autotrophs. They reproduce asexually by vegetative propagation or sexually. These are multicellular eukaryotes. The plant cell contains the outer cell wall and a large central vacuole.

Concept note-2: -The kingdom Plantae includes organisms that range in size from tiny mosses to giant trees. Despite this enormous variation, all plants are multicellular and eukaryotic (i.e., each cell possesses a membrane-bound nucleus that contains the chromosomes).

Concept note-3: -Plantae is the kingdom of plants. Important characteristics of plants are: 1. Plants are multicellular.

Concept note-4: -Animalia. Animals are multicellular, and move with the aid of cilia, flagella, or muscular organs based on contractile proteins. They have organelles including a nucleus, but no chloroplasts or cell walls. Animals acquire nutrients by ingestion.
