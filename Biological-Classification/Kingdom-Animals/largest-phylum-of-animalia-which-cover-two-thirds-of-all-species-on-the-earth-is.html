Largest phylum of Animalia which cover two-thirds of all species on the earth is:

(A) Platyhelminthes.

(B) Nematahelminthes.

(C) ** Arthropods.

(D) Chordates.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Phylum Arthropoda is the largest phylum of the kingdom Animalia. Over two-thirds of all named species on the Earth are arthropods. It includes around a million species. Arthropods have jointed legs.

Concept note-2: -arthropod, (phylum Arthropoda), any member of the phylum Arthropoda, the largest phylum in the animal kingdom, which includes such familiar forms as lobsters, crabs, spiders, mites, insects, centipedes, and millipedes. About 84 percent of all known species of animals are members of this phylum.

Concept note-3: -Arthropoda is the largest phylum with about nine lakh species. They may be aquatic, terrestrial or even parasitic. They have jointed appendages and a chitinous exoskeleton.

Concept note-4: -Final answer: The largest phylum in the animal kingdom is Arthropoda.

Concept note-5: -Phylum Arthropoda is the largest phylum in the animal world, and insects form the single largest class within this phylum.
