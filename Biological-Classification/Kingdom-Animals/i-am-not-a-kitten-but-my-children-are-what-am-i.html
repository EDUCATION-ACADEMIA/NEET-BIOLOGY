I am not a kitten but my children are. What am I?

(A) dog

(B) ** cat

(C) rabbit

(D) fox

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -One Year: No Longer a Kitten Although a cat is considered an adult by one year of age, developmentally speaking, kitten adolescence typically lasts until a cat reaches eighteen months or so.

Concept note-2: -Civet cats, genet cats, fossas, bearcats (also known as binturongs)…are these really cats? No. These animals belong to the familes Viverridae, or Eupleridae, found only in Africa, the Middle East, southern Asia and southern Europe.

Concept note-3: -Yes, cats are mammals. Cats are small mammals that belong to the Felidae family. This is a family of mammals that includes lions, leopards and pumas. Unlike those animals, cats are the only domesticated species in this family, which means they can be kept as pets in people’s homes.

Concept note-4: -Junior Cat Stage (Six Months to Two Years) The junior life stage is equivalent to human adolescence. During this stage, a cat loses their babyish appearance as they reach physical and sexual maturity. They also outgrows their kitten personality and settles into their true temperament.
