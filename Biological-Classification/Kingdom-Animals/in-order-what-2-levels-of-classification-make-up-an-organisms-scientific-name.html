KINGDOM ANIMALS

[SOURCES]
In order, what 2 levels of classification make up an organism’s scientific name?

(A) Kingdom Species

(B) Kingdom Phylum

(C) ** Genus Species

(D) Species Genus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each of this level of the hierarchy is called the taxonomic category or rank. In this system of classification, kingdom is always ranked the highest followed by division, class, order, family, genus, and species.

Concept note-2: -Scientists use a two-name system called a Binomial Naming System. Scientists name animals and plants using the system that describes the genus and species of the organism. The first word is the genus and the second is the species.

Concept note-3: -The genus and species level of classification make up the scientific name. The genus name is written first, followed by the species name.

Concept note-4: -The scientific name of each species is made up of a generic name (generic epithet) and a specific name (specific epithet).

Concept note-5: -1. Linnaeus’ hierarchical system of classification includes seven levels. They are, from largest to smallest, kingdom, phylum, class, order, family, genus, and species.
