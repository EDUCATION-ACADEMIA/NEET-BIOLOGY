Jane Goodall made amazing discoveries while studying animals in the wild in Africa. Which animal did she observe and live among for many years?

(A) lions

(B) giraffes

(C) gorillas

(D) ** chimpanzees

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1960, Jane Goodall entered the forests of what is now Gombe National Park, Tanzania, to study chimpanzees in the wild. Since her pioneering research in Gombe, it has now become the site of one of the longest continuously running and detailed wild animal studies in history at the Gombe Stream Research Center.

Concept note-2: -Jane Goodall is an expert on wild chimpanzees. Recognized for her ground breaking discoveries about their behavior – she discovered that chimpanzees make tools, eat and hunt for meat, and have similar social behavior to humans – she completely transformed our understanding of our closest relative in the animal kingdom.

Concept note-3: -Jane Goodall is a primatologist most known for her long-term study of wild chimpanzees in Tanzania. The Gombe chimp observation, which Jane began in 1960, is the world’s longest running continuous wildlife research project.

Concept note-4: -Goodall is best known for her landmark study of chimpanzee behavior in what is now Gombe, Tanzania. Her presentation will be titled “Gombe: 60 Years of Discovery, ” in light of 2020 being the 60 th anniversary of Goodall’s first trip to Gombe.
