KINGDOM ANIMALS

[SOURCES]
An animal that breathes through lungs, has scales on its skin, and has leathery eggs is a(n) ____

(A) ** reptile

(B) mammal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Crocodile is large semiaquatic reptiles that live throughout the tropics of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia. They are carnivorous animals, feeding mostly on vertebrates such as fish, reptiles, birds, etc. They are cold-blooded animals, have scales on their body and breathe through lungs.

Concept note-2: -Reptiles hatch from eggs that have a protective outer layer such as a brittle or leathery shell. Hatchlings are usually miniature versions of an adult, ready to take off in the world.

Concept note-3: -Reptiles live on land, have scaly skin and lay eggs with shells. They have lungs rather than gills and may or may not have teeth. Some examples of reptiles are turtles, tortoises, lizards, snakes, crocodiles, and dinosaurs (now extinct).

Concept note-4: -How do amphibians breathe? Most amphibians breathe through lungs and their skin.

Concept note-5: -Unlike Page 2 amphibians, reptiles can spend their whole life-cycles on land. Their bodies are covered with scales. They reproduce by laying eggs. Unlike amphibians’ eggs, reptiles’ eggs have soft, leathery shells that keep them from drying out.
