BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM PROTISTA

[SOURCES]
Ferns and mosses belong to which kingdom?

(A) Kingdom Protista

(B) Kingdom Fungi

(C) ** Kingdom Plantae

(D) Kingdom Eubacteria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kingdom Plantae includes green, brown and red algae, liverworts, mosses, ferns and seed plants with or without flowers. They have the following characteristics: They are multicellular organisms with walled and frequently vacuolate eukaryotic cells.

Concept note-2: -Mosses, ferns, conifers, and flowering plants are all members of the plant kingdom. The plant kingdom contains mostly photosynthetic organisms; a few parasitic forms have lost the ability to photosynthesize. The process of photosynthesis uses chlorophyll, which is located in organelles called chloroplasts.

Concept note-3: -The Plantae includes all land plants: mosses, ferns, conifers, flowering plants, and so on-an amazing range of diverse forms. With more than 250, 000 species, they are second in size only to the arthropoda.

Concept note-4: -Mosses are nonvascular primitive plants that produce spores, whereas ferns are vascular plants. Furthermore, mosses lack genuine stems, leaves, and roots, whereas ferns have a plant body that is divided into actual stems, leaves, and roots.
