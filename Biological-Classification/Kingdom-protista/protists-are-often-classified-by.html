BIOLOGICAL CLASSIFICATION

KINGDOM PROTISTA

[SOURCES]
Protists are often classified by

(A) ** how they obtain their nutrition and how they move

(B) how they move and by their color

(C) where they live and what they eat

(D) if they are free living or parasitic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are heterotrophic in nature and thus obtain their food from an outside source. They are saprophytes that obtain their nutrients by feeding on dead and decaying organic matter. These protists can be classified into two categories-water molds and slime molds.

Concept note-2: -Protists get food in one of three ways. They may ingest, absorb, or make their own organic molecules. Ingestive protists ingest, or engulf, ba cteria and other small particles. They extend their cell wall an d cell membr ane around the food item, forming a food vacuole.

Concept note-3: -Biologists generally categorize protists according to their Mode of movement, or locomotion. All protists can travel through water by one of three methods: cilia, flagella, or pseudo/axopodia.

Concept note-4: -Some make their own food, but most take in or absorb food. Most protists move with the help of flagella, pseudopods, or cilia. Some protists, like the one-celled amoeba and paramecium, feed on other organisms. Others, such as the one-celled euglena or the many-celled algae, make their food by photosynthesis.

Concept note-5: -A few forms can move by gliding or floating, although the vast majority move by means of “whips” or small “hairs” known as flagella or cilia, respectively. (Those organelles give their names to informal groups-flagellates and ciliates-of protists.) A lesser number of protists employ pseudopodia.
