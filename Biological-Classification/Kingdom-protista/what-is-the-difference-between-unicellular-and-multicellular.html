What is the difference between unicellular and multicellular?

(A) They are the same

(B) One has a nucleus and one doesnâ€™t

(C) ** Unicellular has one cell and multicellular has many

(D) One is bacteria and the is archaebacteria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The unicellular organisms contain a single cell whereas the multicellular organisms contain multiple cells. Unicellular organisms conciliate all the cellular activities by a single cell, while multicellular organisms carry out specific cell activities through a well-defined group of cells.

Concept note-2: -Unicellular organisms are made up of only one cell that carries out all of the functions needed by the organism, while multicellular organisms use many different cells to function. Unicellular organisms include bacteria, protists, and yeast.
