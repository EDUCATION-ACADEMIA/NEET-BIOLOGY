[SOURCES]
Dense cytoplasm found in the interior of many cells.

(A) ** endoplasm

(B) ectoplasm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -endoplasm Add to list Share. The dense, interior layer of a cell’s cytoplasm is its endoplasm. Most cellular processes take place in the endoplasm. Endoplasm comes from Greek roots endon, “within or inside, ” and plasm, “shape.” It’s the innermost part of a cell, aside from the nucleus.

Concept note-2: -Endoplasm generally refers to the inner (often granulated), dense part of a cell’s cytoplasm. This is opposed to the ectoplasm which is the outer (non-granulated) layer of the cytoplasm, which is typically watery and immediately adjacent to the plasma membrane.

Concept note-3: -The fluid (gel) like substance inside the cell in which organelles are present is called the cytoplasm. When the contents of cytoplasm are closely compacted together, it is termed as dense cytoplasm.

Concept note-4: -Plants have denser cytoplasm compared to plant cells.
