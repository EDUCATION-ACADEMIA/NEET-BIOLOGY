CELL DIVISION

[SOURCES]
Which of the following is the shortest phase of mitosis in which the chromosome line up in the middle of the cell and the spindle fibers connect to the centromere?

(A) prophase

(B) ** metaphase

(C) telophase

(D) anaphase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chromosomes, consisting of sister chromatids, line up at the equator or middle of the cell during metaphase.

Concept note-2: -Anaphase is the shortest phase of mitosis. In anaphase, the sister chromatids are pulled apart to opposite ends of the cell. In metaphase, chromosomes line up along the metaphase plate.

Concept note-3: -Anaphase. The shortest stage of mitosis. The centromeres divide, and the sister chromatids of each chromosome are pulled apart-or ‘disjoin’-and move to the opposite ends of the cell, pulled by spindle fibres attached to the kinetochore regions.

Concept note-4: -In anaphase, the shortest stage of mitosis, the sister chromatids break apart, and the chromosomes begin moving to opposite ends of the cell.

Concept note-5: -Metaphase. Chromosomes line up at the metaphase plate, under tension from the mitotic spindle. The two sister chromatids of each chromosome are captured by microtubules from opposite spindle poles. In metaphase, the spindle has captured all the chromosomes and lined them up at the middle of the cell, ready to divide.
