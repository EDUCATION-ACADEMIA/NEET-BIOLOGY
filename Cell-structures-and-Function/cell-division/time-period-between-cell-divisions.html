CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL DIVISION

[SOURCES]
Time period between cell divisions.

(A) Anaphase

(B) Mitosis

(C) Cytokinesis

(D) ** Interphase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The period between two cell divisions is called ‘Interphase’. This is actually the period when the genetic material makes its copy so that it is equally distributed to the daughter cells during mitosis.

Concept note-2: -The interphase is the period when the cell is in a non-dividing state and this can be in different stages: the first gap (G1) between the last mitosis and the S phase (phase of DNA synthesis) and the second gap (G2) between the completion of the S phase and the next mitosis (M).

Concept note-3: -However, mitosis and cytokinesis last only about an hour, so approximately 95% of the cell cycle is spent in interphase-the period between mitoses.

Concept note-4: -The G1 phase is the longest in the cell cycle. It’s the beginning of the interphase. The cell prepares for division during the G1 phase.
