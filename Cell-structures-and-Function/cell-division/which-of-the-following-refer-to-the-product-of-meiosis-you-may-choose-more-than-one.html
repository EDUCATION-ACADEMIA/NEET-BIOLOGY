CELL DIVISION

[SOURCES]
Which of the following refer to the product of meiosis (you may choose more than one)?

(A) ** haploid

(B) diploid

(C) somatic cells

(D) ** gametes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Since cell division occurs twice during meiosis, one starting cell can produce four gametes (eggs or sperm).

Concept note-2: -Meiosis produces our sex cells or gametes? (eggs in females and sperm in males).

Concept note-3: -The haploid cells produced by meiosis are germ cells, also known as gametes, sex cells or spores in plants and fungi.

Concept note-4: -Meiosis contains two separate cell divisions, meaning that one parent cell can produce four gametes (eggs in females, sperm in males). In each round of division, cells go through four stages: prophase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase. Before entering meiosis I, a cell must first go through interphase.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: The final product of meiosis is four haploid daughter cells that contain chromosomal differences from the original parent cell and half the number of chromosomes as the original parent cell (diploid).
