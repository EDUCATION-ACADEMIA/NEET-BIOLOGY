At which sub-stage of Meiosis does crossing over take place

(A) ** Pachytene

(B) Zygotene

(C) Diplotene

(D) Diakinesis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Crossing over is an enzyme-mediated process, where the exchange of genetic material between non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes takes place. It occurs at the pachytene stage of prophase I of meiosis.

Concept note-2: -Crossing over occurs during the pachytene stage of prophase in meiosis, however the chiasma are visible during. No worries!

Concept note-3: -Crossing over takes place during the pachytene stage. During this stage, non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes may exchange segments over regions of homology.

Concept note-4: -Crossing over occurs during pachytene stage between non sister chromatids of a pair of homologous chromosomes. Diplotene is characterised by appearance of chiasma marking the locations at which crossing over has already occurred in the pachytene stage.

Concept note-5: -Pachytene is the third stage of meiosis prophase-1 (a five-stage process). It is preceded by the zygotene stage, where synapsis, i.e. pairing of homologous chromosomes take place. Recombination or crossing over takes place at this stage.
