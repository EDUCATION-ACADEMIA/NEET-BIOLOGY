CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL STRUCTURE

[SOURCES]
Make proteins; found in nucleolus, in the cytoplasm or on rough ER

(A) lysosomes

(B) mitochondria

(C) chloroplasts

(D) ** ribosomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The genes that encode ribosomal proteins are transcribed outside of the nucleolus by RNA polymerase II, yielding mRNAs that are translated on cytoplasmic ribosomes. The ribosomal proteins are then transported from the cytoplasm to the nucleolus, where they are assembled with rRNAs to form preribosomal particles.

Concept note-2: -rough endoplasmic reticulum (RER), series of connected flattened sacs, part of a continuous membrane organelle within the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells, that plays a central role in the synthesis of proteins.

Concept note-3: -Proteins are made at the ribosome. In the cytoplasm, ribosomes attach to messenger RNA (mRNA). They read the nucleotides in groups of three called codons. Each codon codes for a specific amino acid.

Concept note-4: -Rough ER contains two integral membrane proteins, ribophorins I and II, that have molecular masses of 65 kDa and 63 kDa, respectively. These proteins remain attached to ribosomes when the ER membrane is dissolved with detergent and they can be crosslinked to ribosomes by chemical reagents.

Concept note-5: -Plasma membrane and lysosomal proteins also travel from the rough ER to the Golgi and then to their final destinations. Still other proteins travel through the initial steps of the secretory pathway but are then retained and function within either the ER or the Golgi apparatus.
