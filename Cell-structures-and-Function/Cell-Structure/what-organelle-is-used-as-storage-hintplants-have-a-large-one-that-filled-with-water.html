CELL STRUCTURE

[SOURCES]
What organelle is used as storage? Hint:plants have a large one that filled with water.

(A) ** Vacuole

(B) Cell

(C) Cell Wall

(D) Ribosome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vacuole acts as an organelle that maintains the turgor pressure of the cell. Also, they store water, metabolites and nutrients.

Concept note-2: -Vacuoles-Storage Bins to the Cells Vacuoles are storage bubbles found in cells. They are found in both animal and plant cells but are much larger in plant cells. Vacuoles might store food or any variety of nutrients a cell might need to survive.

Concept note-3: -The vacuole is the most conspicuous organelle in plant cells. Because of its large volume, it is involved in storing many inorganic and organic molecules, and in so doing, functions in homeostasis.

Concept note-4: -Plant cells contain vacuoles. In addition to storing wastes and water, the vacuole also helps to support the cell. This is because the liquid inside the vacuole exerts an outward pressure on the cell, much like the water inside a water balloon.

Concept note-5: -Vacuoles are membrane-bound sacs within the cytoplasm of a cell that function in several different ways. In mature plant cells, vacuoles tend to be very large and are extremely important in providing structural support, as well as serving functions such as storage, waste disposal, protection, and growth.
