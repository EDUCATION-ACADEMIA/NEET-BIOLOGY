CELL STRUCTURE

[SOURCES]
The movement of molecules into or out of the cell that requires energy is called ____

(A) osmosis

(B) photosynthesis

(C) ** active transport

(D) passive transport

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This is the biological process in which molecules move against the concentration gradient and require chemical energy to move biochemical compounds from a lower region to the high region. Therefore, this process uses ATP – Adenosine triphosphate to pump molecules through a concentration gradient.

Concept note-2: -Active transport is the movement of molecules across a cell membrane in the direction against their concentration gradient, going from a low concentration to a high concentration.

Concept note-3: -“Active Transport is defined as a process that involves the movement of molecules from a region of lower concentration to a region of higher concentration against a gradient or an obstacle with the use of external energy.”

Concept note-4: -Active transport uses energy to transport molecules across the plasma membrane. This uses energy from ATP. They also use pumps to get molecules in or out of the cell. Passive Transport does NOT use energy to transport molecules across the membrane.

Concept note-5: -Active transport is called “active” because this type of transport requires energy to move molecules. ATP is the most common source of energy for active transport. As molecules are moving against their concentration gradients, active transport cannot occur without assistance.
