CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL STRUCTURE

[SOURCES]
The “filling” of the cell that provides the environment which all organelles live in; made up of about 90% water

(A) cell wall

(B) cell membrane

(C) chorophyll

(D) ** cytoplasm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The vacuole (tonoplast) is one of the most significant intracellular components of plant cells, occupying nearly 90% of the cell volume.

Concept note-2: -Cytoplasm Cytoplasm is the gelatinous liquid that fills the inside of a cell. It is composed of water, salts, and various organic molecules. Some intracellular organelles, such the nucleus and mitochondria, are enclosed by membranes that separate them from the cytoplasm.

Concept note-3: -Golgi Apparatus is found within the cytoplasm of a cell and is present in both plant and animal cells.

Concept note-4: -Quick look: A vacuole is a membrane-enclosed fluid filled sac found in the cells of plants including fungi. Vacuoles can be large organelles occupying between 30% and 90% of a cell by volume.
