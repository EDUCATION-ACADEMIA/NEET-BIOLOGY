Nucleus is separated from cytoplasm by

(A) ** nuclear membrane

(B) nucleoplasm

(C) organs

(D) cell membrane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nuclear envelope separates the contents of the nucleus from the cytoplasm and provides the structural framework of the nucleus. The nuclear membranes, acting as barriers that prevent the free passage of molecules between the nucleus and the cytoplasm, maintain the nucleus as a distinct biochemical compartment.

Concept note-2: -The nuclear pore is a protein-lined channel in the nuclear envelope that regulates the transportation of molecules between the nucleus and the cytoplasm. In eukaryotic cells, the nucleus is separated from the cytoplasm and surrounded by a nuclear envelope.

Concept note-3: -Nuclear Membrane The nuclear membrane is a double layer that encloses the cell’s nucleus, where the chromosomes reside. The nuclear membrane serves to separate the chromosomes from the cell’s cytoplasm and other contents.

Concept note-4: -The nucleus is spheroid in shape and separated from the cytoplasm by a membrane called the nuclear envelope.

Concept note-5: -The nucleus is a double-membrane bound cell organelle that is located in the eukaryotic cells and composes of the cell’s genetic material – the DNA.
