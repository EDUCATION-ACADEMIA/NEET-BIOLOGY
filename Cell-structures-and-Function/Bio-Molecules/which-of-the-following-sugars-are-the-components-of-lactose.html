Which of the following sugars are the components of lactose?

(A) ** glucose and galactose

(B) fructose and galactose

(C) glucose and fructose

(D) glucose and glucose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Galactose is a sugar found in milk. Galactose is a monosaccharide. So, it is true that D-glucose and galactose are components of lactose.

Concept note-2: -lactose, carbohydrate containing one molecule of glucose and one of galactose linked together.

Concept note-3: -Common disaccharides include lactose, maltose, and sucrose. Lactose is a disaccharide consisting of glucose and galactose and is found naturally in milk.

Concept note-4: -The major dietary source of galactose is lactose, a disaccharide formed from one molecule of glucose plus one of galactose. Lactose is found only in milk; after weaning, significant quantities of dietary lactose are found only in dairy products (Table 1).

Concept note-5: -Lactose is one of the main constituents of human and animal milk (Table 1). As a disaccharide consisting of glucose and galactose, lactose acts as an energy-carrier in milk.
