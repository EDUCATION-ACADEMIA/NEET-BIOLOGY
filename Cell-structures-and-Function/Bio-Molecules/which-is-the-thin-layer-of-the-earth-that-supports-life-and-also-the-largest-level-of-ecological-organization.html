Which is the thin layer of the earth that supports life and also the largest level of ecological organization?

(A) A community

(B) ** The biosphere

(C) A population

(D) The biome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The biosphere is a relatively thin layer of the Earth’s surface that supports life, reaching from a few kilometers into the atmosphere to deep-sea vents. The biosphere is a global ecosystem made up of living organisms (biota) and the nonliving (abiotic) factors that provide them with energy and nutrients.

Concept note-2: -The biosphere is the thin life-supporting stratum of Earth’s surface, extending from a few kilometres into the atmosphere to the deep-sea vents of the ocean. It is composed of living organisms and nonliving factors from which the organisms derive energy and nutrients.

Concept note-3: -The biosphere is made up of the parts of Earth where life exists. The biosphere extends from the deepest root systems of trees to the dark environment of ocean trenches, to lush rain forests and high mountaintops.

Concept note-4: -What are five levels of organization in an environment? Levels of organization in ecology include the population, community, ecosystem, biome, and biosphere.

Concept note-5: -They are organized from smallest to largest; organism, population, community, ecosystem.
