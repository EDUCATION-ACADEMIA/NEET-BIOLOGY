What are the four biomolecules?

(A) ** Proteins, Carbohydrates, Lipids, Nucleic Acids

(B) Fatty Acids, Amino Acids, Monosaccharides, Nucleotides

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four major types of biomolecules are carbohydrates, lipids, nucleic acids, and proteins.

Concept note-2: -There are four major classes of biological macromolecules (carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids), and each is an important component of the cell and performs a wide array of functions. Combined, these molecules make up the majority of a cellâ€™s mass.

Concept note-3: -Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids are organic molecules found in every living organism. These macromolecules are large carbon based structures. The macromolecules are assembled by joining several smaller units, called monomers, together through a chemical reaction called dehydration synthesis.
