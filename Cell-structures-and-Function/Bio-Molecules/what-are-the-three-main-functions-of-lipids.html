What are the three main functions of Lipids?

(A) Short term energy, storage of energy, structure for plants and insects

(B) catalyst, immune system support, hair and nails

(C) Stores genetic information, hereditary information

(D) ** long term energy, water proof, insulation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lipids perform three primary biological functions within the body: they serve as structural components of cell membranes, function as energy storehouses, and function as important signaling molecules.

Concept note-2: -Lipids are fatty compounds that perform a variety of functions in your body. They’re part of your cell membranes and help control what goes in and out of your cells. They help with moving and storing energy, absorbing vitamins and making hormones. Having too much of some lipids is harmful.

Concept note-3: -The main biological functions of lipids include storing energy, as lipids may be broken down to yield large amounts of energy. Lipids also form the structural components of cell membranes, and form various messengers and signaling molecules within the body.

Concept note-4: -Lipids are a diverse group of compounds that are united by a common feature. Lipids are hydrophobic (“water-fearing”), or insoluble in water.
