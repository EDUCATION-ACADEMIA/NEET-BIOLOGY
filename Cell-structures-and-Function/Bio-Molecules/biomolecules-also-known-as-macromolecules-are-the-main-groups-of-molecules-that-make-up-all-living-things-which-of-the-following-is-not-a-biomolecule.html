(A) Protein

(B) ** Amino Acids

(C) Carbohydrates

(D) Nucleic Acids

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, lipids are biomolecules which are not strictly macromolecules.

Concept note-2: -11.1 Introduction: The Four Major Macromolecules These are the carbohydrates, lipids (or fats), proteins, and nucleic acids. All of the major macromolecule classes are similar, in that, they are large polymers that are assembled from small repeating monomer subunits.
