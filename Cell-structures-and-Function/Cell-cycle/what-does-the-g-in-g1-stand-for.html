What does the G in G1 stand for?

(A) Gestation

(B) Gum

(C) ** Growth

(D) Great

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Interphase is divided into the first growth (G1), Synthesis (S), and the second growth (G2) phases (figure 1).

Concept note-2: -The G1 phase, gap 1 phase, or growth 1 phase, is the first of four phases of the cell cycle that takes place in eukaryotic cell division. In this part of interphase, the cell synthesizes mRNA and proteins in preparation for subsequent steps leading to mitosis.

Concept note-3: -The G1 stage stands for “GAP 1". The S stage stands for “Synthesis". This is the stage when DNA replication occurs. The G2 stage stands for “GAP 2". The M stage stands for “mitosis", and is when nuclear (chromosomes separate) and cytoplasmic (cytokinesis) division occur.

Concept note-4: -The first part of interphase is called the first gap phase (G1). Cell growth marks the G1 phase of the cell cycle.

Concept note-5: -The interphase is divided into three phases:-G1 phase (Gap 1) – G1 phase is the phase of the cell between mitosis and initiation of replication of the genetic material of the cell. During this phase, the cell is metabolically active and continues to grow without replicating its DNA.
