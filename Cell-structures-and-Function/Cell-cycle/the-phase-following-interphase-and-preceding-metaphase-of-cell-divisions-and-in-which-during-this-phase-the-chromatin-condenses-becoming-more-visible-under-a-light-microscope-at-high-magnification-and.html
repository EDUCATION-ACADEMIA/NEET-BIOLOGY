CELL CYCLE

[SOURCES]
The phase following interphase and preceding metaphase of cell divisions, and in which during this phase the chromatin condenses (becoming more visible under a light microscope at high magnification) and initiating spindle formation. What phase am I?

(A) Anaphase

(B) Metaphase

(C) ** Prophase

(D) S Phase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chromatin is in its least condensed stage during the interphase stage. It begins its condensation during the prophase stage and becomes chromosomes. The chromosomes are visible until the end of cell division.

Concept note-2: -Initially in G1 phase, the cell grows physically and increases the volume of both protein and organelles. In S phase, the cell copies its DNA to produce two sister chromatids and replicates its nucleosomes. Finally, G2 phase involves further cell growth and organisation of cellular contents.

Concept note-3: -The S phase of a cell cycle occurs during interphase, before mitosis or meiosis, and is responsible for the synthesis or replication of DNA.

Concept note-4: -The G1 phase, gap 1 phase, or growth 1 phase, is the first of four phases of the cell cycle that takes place in eukaryotic cell division. In this part of interphase, the cell synthesizes mRNA and proteins in preparation for subsequent steps leading to mitosis.

Concept note-5: -During metaphase, all of the chromosomes are aligned in a plane called the metaphase plate, or the equatorial plane, midway between the two poles of the cell.
