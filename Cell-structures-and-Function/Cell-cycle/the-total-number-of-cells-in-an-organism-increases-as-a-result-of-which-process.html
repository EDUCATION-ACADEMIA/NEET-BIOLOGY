The total number of cells in an organism increases as a result of which process?

(A) respiration

(B) photosynthesis

(C) ** cell division

(D) fermentation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two types of cell division: mitosis and meiosis. Most of the time when people refer to “cell division, ” they mean mitosis, the process of making new body cells. Meiosis is the type of cell division that creates egg and sperm cells. Mitosis is a fundamental process for life.

Concept note-2: -The process of mitosis generates new cells that are genetically identical to each other. Mitosis helps organisms grow in size and repair damaged tissue.

Concept note-3: -Cell proliferation is the process of generating an increased number of cells through cell division. Both cell division and growth are tightly linked to the cell cycle and its regulation.

Concept note-4: -Hint: The growth of any living organism occurs by the process of mitotic cell division. During mitosis, one cell divides to form two identical daughter cells. All cells perform mitosis and in this way the number of the cells in the body increases and causes the organism’s growth.

Concept note-5: -Mitogens stimulate the rate of cell division by removing intracellular molecular brakes that restrain cell-cycle progression in G1. Growth factors promote an increase in cell mass by stimulating the synthesis and inhibiting the degradation of macromolecules.
