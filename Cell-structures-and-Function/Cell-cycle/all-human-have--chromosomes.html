All human have ____ chromosomes

(A) 23

(B) ** 46

(C) Diploid

(D) Haploid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Humans typically have 23 pairs of chromosomes, or 46 chromosomes in total. Chromosomes are made up of long strands of DNA, which contain all the bodyâ€™s genes.

Concept note-2: -Normally, people are born with 23 chromosome pairs, or 46 chromosomes, in each cell-one inherited from the mother and one from the father. A numerical chromosome abnormality can cause each cell to have 45 or 47 chromosomes in each cell.

Concept note-3: -Monosomy means that a person is missing one chromosome in the pair. Instead of 46 chromosomes, the person has only 45 chromosomes. This means a girl with TS has only one X chromosome in her 23 rd pair. Sometimes an error occurs when an egg or sperm cell is forming.

Concept note-4: -Females have two X chromosomes, while males have one X and one Y chromosome. A picture of all 46 chromosomes in their pairs is called a karyotype. A normal female karyotype is written 46, XX, and a normal male karyotype is written 46, XY.
