CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL CYCLE

[SOURCES]
The larger the surface area to volume ratio, the ____ efficient the cell is in transporting nutrients and waste in and out of the cell.

(A) less

(B) ** more

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A larger surface area to volume ratio is more efficient than a smaller ratio. This is due to the amount of plasma membrane relative to the volume of the cell. The more plasma membrane available to transport materials in and out of the cell, the more efficient the cell will be in completing its specific functions.

Concept note-2: -1: Surface Area to Volume Ratios: Notice that as a cell increases in size, its surface area-to-volume ratio decreases. When there is insufficient surface area to support a cell’s increasing volume, a cell will either divide or die.

Concept note-3: -If a cell is too large, nutrients simply aren’t able to diffuse through the entire volume of the cell quickly enough. Materials must be able to reach all parts of a cell quickly, and when volume is too large relative to surface area, diffusion cannot occur at sufficiently high rates to ensure this.

Concept note-4: -A smaller cell is more effective and transporting materials, including waste products, than a larger cell. Cells come in many different shapes. A cell’s function is determined, in part, by its shape.
