A membrane that encloses the cells nucleus and separates the chromosomes from the rest of the cell is

(A) cell membrane

(B) cell wall

(C) ** nuclear membrane

(D) spindle fibers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nuclear envelope (NE) is a highly regulated membrane barrier that separates the nucleus from the cytoplasm in eukaryotic cells. It contains a large number of different proteins that have been implicated in chromatin organization and gene regulation.

Concept note-2: -Nuclear Membrane The nuclear membrane is a double layer that encloses the cell’s nucleus, where the chromosomes reside. The nuclear membrane serves to separate the chromosomes from the cell’s cytoplasm and other contents.

Concept note-3: -The nuclear envelope is also called nuclear membrane. It is made up of two lipid bilayer membranes. The nuclear membrane is present in both the plant and animal cells.

Concept note-4: -The nuclear envelope, also known as the nuclear membrane, is made up of two lipid bilayer membranes that in eukaryotic cells surround the nucleus, which encloses the genetic material.

Concept note-5: -The nuclear membrane, also called the nuclear envelope, is a double membrane layer that separates the contents of the nucleus from the rest of the cell. It is connected to the endoplasmic reticulum.
