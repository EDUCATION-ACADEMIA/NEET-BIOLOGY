The cells produced by the process of meiosis are called:

(A) ** gametes

(B) somatic cells

(C) body cells

(D) skin cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes in the parent cell by half and produces four gamete cells. This process is required to produce egg and sperm cells for sexual reproduction.

Concept note-2: -Gametes, or reproductive cells, are produced in a special kind of cell division called meiosis. During this process the normal number of the cellâ€™s chromosomes is reduced by half. Each gamete ends up with a copy of one chromosome from each pair.

Concept note-3: -Meiosis is followed by gametogenesis, the process during which the haploid daughter cells change into mature gametes. Males produce gametes called sperm in a process known as spermatogenesis, and females produce gametes called eggs in the process known as oogenesis .

Concept note-4: -Meiosis Consists of a Reduction Division and an Equational Division. Two divisions, meiosis I and meiosis II, are required to produce gametes (Figure 3). Meiosis I is a unique cell division that occurs only in germ cells; meiosis II is similar to a mitotic division.
