CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL CYCLE

[SOURCES]
Cells must pass through a critical checkpoint during which stages of the cell cycle?

(A) G1

(B) G2

(C) M phase

(D) ** All of these are correct.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each step of the cell cycle is monitored by internal controls called checkpoints. There are three major checkpoints in the cell cycle: one near the end of G1, a second at the G2/M transition, and the third during metaphase. Positive regulator molecules allow the cell cycle to advance to the next stage.

Concept note-2: -The G2 checkpoint is used to prevent the cell from entering mitosis if there were errors in the replication of DNA during the S phase. Failure to pass the G2 checkpoint can result in apoptosis.

Concept note-3: -The main cell cycle checkpoints are the G1/S checkpoint, the intra-S checkpoint, and the G2/M checkpoint [60]. The transition through stages of the cell cycle is regulated by the action of cyclin-dependent kinases, which are key targets for modulations induced by different cellular stimuli, including DNA damage.

Concept note-4: -A checkpoint is one of several points in the eukaryotic cell cycle at which the progression of a cell to the next stage in the cycle can be halted until conditions are favorable. These checkpoints occur near the end of G1, at the G2/M transition, and during metaphase (Figure 1).

Concept note-5: -There exist three major cell-cycle checkpoints; the G1/S checkpoint, the G2/M checkpoint, and the spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC).
