CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL ORGANELLE

[SOURCES]
These may be found free-floating in the cytoplasm or atached to rough ER

(A) Centriole

(B) ** Ribosome

(C) Smooth er

(D) Lysosome

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ribosomes are found ‘free’ in the cytoplasm or bound to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) to form rough ER. In a mammalian cell there can be as many as 10 million ribosomes. Several ribosomes can be attached to the same mRNA strand, this structure is called a polysome. Ribosomes have only a temporary existence.

Concept note-2: -Eukaryotic ribosomes may be either free, meaning that they are floating around in the cytoplasm, or bound, meaning that they are attached to the endoplasmic reticulum or the outside of the nuclear envelope.

Concept note-3: -Ribosomes float freely in the cytoplasm or sometimes bind to another organelle called the endoplasmic reticulum.

Concept note-4: -In prokaryotes, ribosomes are free floating the cytoplasm; in eukaryotes, ribosomes can be free floating in the cytoplasm, bound to the rough endoplasmic reticulum, in mitochondria, or in chloroplasts.

Concept note-5: -Ribosomes on the rough endoplasmic reticulum are called ‘membrane bound’ and are responsible for the assembly of many proteins. This process is called translation. Certain cells of the pancreas and digestive tract produce a high volume of protein as enzymes.
