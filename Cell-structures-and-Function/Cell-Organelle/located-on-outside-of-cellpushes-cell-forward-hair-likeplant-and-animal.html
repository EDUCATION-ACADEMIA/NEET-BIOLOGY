Concept note-1: -Cilia and flagella are motile cellular appendages found in most microorganisms and animals, but not in higher plants.

Concept note-2: -Plant cells do not contain cilia on their cells because they have no real purpose for the ciliaâ€™s mobility properties. It is believed that ancient plant cells may have possessed cilia when they lived in wet environments.

Concept note-3: -The flagella (singular = flagellum) are long, hair-like structures that extend from the plasma membrane and enable an entire cell to move (for example, sperm, Euglena, and some prokaryotes). When present, the cell has just one flagellum or a few flagella.

Concept note-4: -Cilia and Flagella are complex filamentous cytoplasmic structures protruding through a cell wall. They are minute, especially differentiated appendices of the cell. Flagella (singular = flagellum) are long, hair-like structures that extend from the plasma membrane and are used to move an entire cell.
