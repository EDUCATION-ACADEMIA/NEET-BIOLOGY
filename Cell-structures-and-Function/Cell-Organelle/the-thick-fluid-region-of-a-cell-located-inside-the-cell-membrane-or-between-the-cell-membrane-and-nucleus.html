CELL STRUCTURES AND FUNCTION

CELL ORGANELLE

[SOURCES]
the thick fluid region of a cell located inside the cell membrane or between the cell membrane and nucleus

(A) ** cytoplasm

(B) cell wall

(C) organelle

(D) golgi apparatus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Inside the cell there is a large fluid-filled space called the cytoplasm, sometimes called the cytosol. In prokaryotes, this space is relatively free of compartments. In eukaryotes, the cytosol is the “soup” within which all of the cell’s organelles reside. It is also the home of the cytoskeleton.

Concept note-2: -Cytoplasm is the gelatinous liquid that fills the inside of a cell. It is composed of water, salts, and various organic molecules.

Concept note-3: -Final answer: The fluid content inside the plasma membrane of a cell is called Cytoplasm.

Concept note-4: -The cytoplasm refers to the entire region of a cell between the plasma membrane and the nuclear envelope. It is composed of organelles suspended in the gel-like cytosol, the cytoskeleton, and various chemicals (see figure below).

Concept note-5: -Cytoplasm Jelly-like fluid that surrounds and protects the organelles. Nucleus The control center of the cell. Contains the DNA Nuclear Membrane Surrounds the nucleus.
