CELL ORGANELLE

[SOURCES]
This organelle contains chlorophyll, a green pigment that traps energy from sunlight and gives plants their green color.

(A) ** Chloroplasts

(B) Vesicles

(C) Cytoskeleton

(D) Chromatin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chlorophyll is located in a plant’s chloroplasts, which are tiny structures in a plant’s cells. This is where photosynthesis takes place.

Concept note-2: -Chlorophyll, a green pigment found in chloroplasts, is an important part of the light-dependent reactions. Chlorophyll soaks up the energy from sunlight. It is also the reason why plants are green.

Concept note-3: -In plants, photosynthesis takes place in chloroplasts, which contain the chlorophyll. Chloroplasts are surrounded by a double membrane and contain a third inner membrane, called the thylakoid membrane, that forms long folds within the organelle.

Concept note-4: -The chloroplasts are the organelles which have the green coloured pigments.
