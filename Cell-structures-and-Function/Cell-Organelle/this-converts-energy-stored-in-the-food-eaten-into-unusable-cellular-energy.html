(A) Ribosome

(B) Rough ER

(C) Smooth ER

(D) ** Mitochondria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cellular respiration is the process by which the energy in food is converted into energy that can be used by the bodyâ€™s cells. The energy stored in glucose is transferred to ATP.

Concept note-2: -Mitochondria, using oxygen available within the cell convert chemical energy from food in the cell to energy in a form usable to the host cell. The process is called oxidative phosphorylation and it happens inside mitochondria.

Concept note-3: -Because the energy to drive ATP synthesis in mitochondria ultimately derives from the oxidative breakdown of food molecules, the phosphorylation of ADP to form ATP that is driven by electron transport in the mitochondrion is known as oxidative phosphorylation.

Concept note-4: -Mitochondria convert chemical energy, in the form of a chemical called adenosine triphosphate or ATP for short. ATP is an energy currency that every cell in our body can use.

Concept note-5: -The process that converts food energy into ATP is cellular respiration. Cellular respiration starts in the mitochondria of cells. It consists of sugar molecules being broken apart and used to produce ATP with oxygen present. Water and carbon dioxide are also products.
