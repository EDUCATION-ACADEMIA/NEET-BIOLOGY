PHOTOSYNTHESIS

[SOURCES]
All plants produce flowers.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -No. Although most of the world’s plants are flowering plants called angiosperms (from the Greek words for “vessel” and “seed”), there are hundreds of plants that do not make flowers.

Concept note-2: -Flowers are found on all sorts of plants, but not all plants have them. Moss, ferns, and pine trees, for example, don’t have flowers, but a lot of plants do. Plants have flowers because they need to make seeds.

Concept note-3: -All plants do not bear flowers. Mosses and ferns are example of non-flowering plants.

Concept note-4: -Flowers and fruits are major horticultural features of plants and can fail to form for many different reasons. Plants that do not flower are often too young, or there is not enough light. If there are no flowers on a plant, there can be no fruits formed.

Concept note-5: -Flowers produce the male and female gametes for sexual reproduction. After fertilization they give rise to fruits. They produce seeds which finally grow into new plants.
