[SOURCES]
Plants can create their own food, this food is called ____

(A) carbon dioxide

(B) ** glucose

(C) oxygen

(D) chlorophyll

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Most life on Earth depends on photosynthesis. The process is carried out by plants, algae, and some types of bacteria, which capture energy from sunlight to produce oxygen (O2) and chemical energy stored in glucose (a sugar).

Concept note-2: -Plants use the energy of the sun to change water and carbon dioxide into a sugar called glucose. Glucose is used by plants for energy and to make other substances like cellulose and starch. Cellulose is used in building cell walls.

Concept note-3: -Glucose can be used as a chemical building block and as an energy supplier. Plants use glucose to live and grow. For both plants and humans, glucose is like the fuel for a machine. Plants produce sugar in their leaves, but these leaves do not taste sweet.

Concept note-4: -Plants synthesise their own food by the process of photosynthesis. Food is synthesised by the plants in the form of glucose, stored in the form of starch and is transported in the form of sucrose.

Concept note-5: -Green plants manufacture glucose through a process that requires light, known as photosynthesis. This process takes place in the leaf chloroplasts. Carbon dioxide and water molecules enter a sequence of chemical reactions within the chloroplasts.
