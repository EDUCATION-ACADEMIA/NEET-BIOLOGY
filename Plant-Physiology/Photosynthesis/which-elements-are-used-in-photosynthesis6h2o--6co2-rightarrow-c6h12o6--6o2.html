PHOTOSYNTHESIS

[SOURCES]
Which elements are used in Photosynthesis?6H2O + 6CO2 → C6H12O6 + 6O2

(A) Carbon

(B) Hydrogen

(C) Oxygen

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The process of photosynthesis is commonly written as: 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2. This means that the reactants, six carbon dioxide molecules and six water molecules, are converted by light energy captured by chlorophyll (implied by the arrow) into a sugar molecule and six oxygen molecules, the products.

Concept note-2: -The chlorophyll in the chloroplasts inside leaf cells absorbs sunlight. Cellular respiration allows organisms to use (release) energy stored in the chemical bonds of glucose (C6H12O6). The energy in glucose is used to produce ATP.

Concept note-3: -Key Points. The chemical equation for photosynthesis is 6CO2+6H2O→C6H12O6+6O2. 6CO2+6H2O→C6H12O6+6O2. In plants, the process of photosynthesis takes place in the mesophyll of the leaves, inside the chloroplasts.

Concept note-4: -Photosynthesis converts carbon dioxide and water into oxygen and glucose. Glucose is used as food by the plant and oxygen is a by-product. Cellular respiration converts oxygen and glucose into water and carbon dioxide. Water and carbon dioxide are by-products and ATP is energy that is transformed from the process.
