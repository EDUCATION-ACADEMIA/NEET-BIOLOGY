Is Glucose (C6H12O6) a Raw Material (Ingredient) or Product of Photosynthesis?

(A) ** Product

(B) Raw Material

(C) Niether

(D) Whatâ€™s Photosynthesis?

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants make sugar by using energy from sunlight to transform carbon dioxide (CO2), a gas absorbed from the air, and water (H20) taken from the ground by roots into glucose (C6H12O6) and oxygen (O2). This process is called photosynthesis and occurs in the chloroplast of the plant cell.

Concept note-2: -No, glucose is produced in photosynthesis. The light energy that reaches plants is converted into chemical energy. In the process, chlorophyll, light, carbon dioxide, and water are all used as raw materials.

Concept note-3: -Sunlight, water and carbon dioxide are the raw materials; food and oxygen are the products of photosynthesis.

Concept note-4: -Plants use a process called photosynthesis to make food. During photosynthesis, plants trap light energy with their leaves. Plants use the energy of the sun to change water and carbon dioxide into a sugar called glucose. Glucose is used by plants for energy and to make other substances like cellulose and starch.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: The raw materials of cellular respiration are glucose and oxygen and the products of cellular respiration are ATP, carbon dioxide, and water.
