What group of pigments break down later in the year and give leaves their orange, red, and yellow colors?

(A) ** carotenoids

(B) chlorophyll a

(C) chlorophyll b

(D) rhodopsin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yellow and Orange Colors are Revealed When the Chlorophyll Degrades. As I have noted in several previous essays, the yellow and orange colors in leaves are revealed when chlorophyll, the pigment responsible for making leaves appear green, is lost from the leaf.

Concept note-2: -Chlorophyll Breaks Down But in the fall, because of changes in the length of daylight and changes in temperature, the leaves stop their food-making process. The chlorophyll breaks down, the green color disappears, and the yellow to orange colors become visible and give the leaves part of their fall splendor.

Concept note-3: -Plant pigments that are responsible for their yellow, bright red, and orange color are carotenoids that play a key role in plant health. Therefore, consumption of foods that have carotenoids provide various health benefits to the human body.

Concept note-4: -Yellow, orange, and red colours usually come from a group of pigments called carotenoids. A common carotenoid, beta-carotene, is produced in the chromoplasts of sunflower petals. This produces the bright yellow and orange colours we see in these flowers.

Concept note-5: -The three types of pigments that give fruits and vegetables their colors are carotenoids, flavonoids, and chlorophyll. Carotenoids – this pigment causes orange, red, and yellow colors.
