PHOTOSYNTHESIS

[SOURCES]
Which of the following best explains why the rate of photosynthesis dramatically drops off at extreme temperatures (hot and cold)?

(A) there is not enough CO2 to fuel the reaction

(B) there is not enough light to power the reaction

(C) ** the shape of enzymes involved in the reaction denature

(D) the mitochondria cannot function at these temperatures

(E) all of the above could explain why the rate drops off

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Photosynthesis is an enzyme specific process. All enzymes act at an optimum temperature (i. e., 25−35∘C). if temperature increases, enzyme gets denatured, thus leading to a fall in the rate of photosynthesis.

Concept note-2: -Because it is an enzyme-controlled process, this is the case. It occurs as a result of enzymes being involved in temperature-sensitive chemical processes. At lower temperatures, the number of molecular collisions between the substrate and the enzymes limits the pace of photosynthesis.

Concept note-3: -Excess water loss (transpiration) causes stomata closure and therefore decreases the photosynthetic rate.

Concept note-4: -At high intensities, the temperature of the plant increases which leads to increased transpiration in the plant. This leads to the closing of the stomata which leads to a reduced CO2 intake. Thus, leading to a reduction and finally stoppage of photosynthesis. Therefore, excessive light inhibits photosynthesis.
