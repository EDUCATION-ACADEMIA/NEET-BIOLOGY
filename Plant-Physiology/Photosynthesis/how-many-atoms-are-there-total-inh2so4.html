How many atoms are there TOTAL in:H2SO4

(A) 6

(B) 5

(C) ** 7

(D) 3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So, in this molecule, total 7 atoms are present.

Concept note-2: -One sulphuric acid (

H 2 SO 4

) molecule has 2 hydrogen atoms, 1 sulfur atom, and 4 oxygen atoms.

Concept note-3: -If it is 2 H 2 SO 4 there are two molecules of sulphuric acid. Since number of atoms in one molecule of sulphuric acid is, therefore total number of atoms in 2 H 2 SO 4 is fourteen.

Concept note-4: -Total number of atoms in H2SO4 = 1x2 + 1 + 4x1 = 7 atoms. Now as there are 3 molecules of sulphuric acid so 7x3 = 21 atoms in total.

Concept note-5: -Atomic mass of one mole of H2SO4 is 98 grams. So 98 grams of H2SO4 contains 1 mole of H2SO4 molecule i.e., 7 moles atoms.
