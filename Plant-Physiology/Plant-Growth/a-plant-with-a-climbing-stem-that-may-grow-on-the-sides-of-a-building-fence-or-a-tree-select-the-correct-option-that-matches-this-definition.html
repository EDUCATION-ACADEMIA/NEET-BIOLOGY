(B) Shrub

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cucumber is a climber plant, which has a very weak stem due to which it cannot stand on itâ€™s own. It can climb onto other plants or walls for support. It makes use of tendrils which grab fences or stems of other plants and starts climbing.

Concept note-2: -: a plant whose stem requires support and which climbs by tendrils or twining or creeps along the ground. also : the stem of such a plant. : any of various sprawling herbaceous plants (such as a tomato or potato) that lack specialized adaptations for climbing. : grape sense 2.

Concept note-3: -Lianas (also known as vines, climbing plants or climbers) are plants with long, flexible, climbing stems that are rooted in the ground, and usually have long dangling branches.

Concept note-4: -In botany, a tendril is a specialized stem, leaf or petiole with a threadlike shape used by climbing plants for support and attachment, as well as cellular invasion by parasitic plants such as Cuscuta.
