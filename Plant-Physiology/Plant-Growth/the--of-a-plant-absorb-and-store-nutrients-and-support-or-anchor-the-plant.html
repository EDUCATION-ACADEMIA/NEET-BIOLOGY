PLANT GROWTH

[SOURCES]
The ____ of a plant absorb and store nutrients and support or anchor the plant.

(A) water

(B) ** roots

(C) floods

(D) stem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Roots absorb water and nutrients from the soil. They anchor the plant firmly. They help in storing food and nutrients.

Concept note-2: -Roots. The roots’ functions are to absorb nutrients and moisture, anchor the plant in the soil, support the stem and store food.

Concept note-3: -Anchoring and supporting the plant: Root systems help anchor plants to the ground, allowing plants to grow tall without toppling over. A tough covering may replace the epidermis in older roots, making them ropelike and even stronger.

Concept note-4: -The roots absorb water and minerals from the soil and anchor the plant in the ground. The stem supports the plant above ground, and carries the water and minerals to the leaves.

Concept note-5: -► Roots anchor the plants in the soil and absorb nutrients and water that are needed by the rest of the plant. ► Stems support the upper part of the plant and act as a transport system for nutrients, water, sugar, and starches.
