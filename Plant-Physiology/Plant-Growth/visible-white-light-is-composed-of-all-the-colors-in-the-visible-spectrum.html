Visible white light is composed of all the colors in the visible spectrum

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -White light is a combination of all colors in the color spectrum. It has all the colors of the rainbow. Combining primary colors of light like red, blue, and green creates secondary colors: yellow, cyan, and magenta. All other colors can be broken down into different combinations of the three primary colors.

Concept note-2: -The spectrum is divided up into seven colors (ROYGBIV): red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet. On the red end it fades into infrared, and on the violet end it moves into ultraviolet, both of which are invisible to the human eye.

Concept note-3: -The white light coming from the sun is the spectrum of the seven colors, violet, indigo, blue, green, yellow, orange, red. This can be proved to bypass the white light through the prism As shown in the figure below.

Concept note-4: -The Wavelength of White Light Above 700 mm and below 400 mm, light is not visible. White light is made up of seven colors viz: violet, green, indigo, orange, blue, yellow, and red.
