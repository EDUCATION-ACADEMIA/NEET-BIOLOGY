PLANT PHYSIOLOGY

PLANT GROWTH

[SOURCES]
A hormone manufactured primarily in the shoot tips, in embryos, and in parts of developing flowers and seeds.

(A) ** auxin

(B) cytokinin

(C) gibberellins

(D) ethylene

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An auxin, indole‐3‐acetic acid (IAA), was the first plant hormone identified. It is manufactured primarily in the shoot tips (in leaf primordia and young leaves), in embryos, and in parts of developing flowers and seeds.

Concept note-2: -The chemical name of auxin is indole-3-acetic acid (IAA).

Concept note-3: -Yes, it is true that shoot of a plant bends towards light during its growth and it is due to the presence of growth hormone, Auxin.

Concept note-4: -(a) Auxins are group of plant hormones synthesized at the shot-tip of the plant.

Concept note-5: -The hormones auxin and cytokinin are key regulators of plant growth and development. As they are active at minute concentrations and regulate dynamic processes, cell and tissue levels of the hormones are finely controlled developmentally, diurnally, and in response to environmental variables.
