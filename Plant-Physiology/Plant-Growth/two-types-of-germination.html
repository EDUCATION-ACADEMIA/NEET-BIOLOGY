Two types of germination

(A) epigeal and endogeal

(B) hypogeal and hypergeal

(C) ** epigeal and hypogeal

(D) none

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When the cotyledons remain under the soil during the seed germination, it is called hypogeal germination whereas when the cotyledons grow out of the soil during the seed germination, it is called epigeal germination.

Concept note-2: -Epigeal germination refers to the germination of a plant that takes place above the ground while hypogeal germination refers to the germination of a plant that takes place below the ground.
