Which type of cell produces growth in the girth of the stem?

(A) ** Lateral meristems

(B) Apical meristems

(C) Vascular cambium

(D) Cork cambium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lateral meristems are known as secondary meristems because they are responsible for secondary growth, or increase in stem girth and thickness. Meristems form anew from other cells in injured tissues and are responsible for wound healing.

Concept note-2: -Plants with secondary growth produce a lateral meristem called the cork cambium that produces cells that form a new skin that is called the periderm. Unlike the vascular cambium, the cork cambium usually is not a continuous cylinder.

Concept note-3: -Cambium (lateral meristem) is situated below the bark and is responsible for the increase in the girth of the stem or root.

Concept note-4: -The intercalary meristem is responsible for the increase in girth of the plant.

Concept note-5: -Apical meristem helps in increasing the girth of a plant.
