PLANT MOVEMENT

[SOURCES]
Pulling tendrils move in ____ and pulling roots move in ____

(A) ** a. air, soil

(B) 2. soil, air

(C) 3. air, air

(D) 4. soil, soil

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The curling of tendrils is a growth movement induced due to contact or touch. Such a movement is called thigmotropic movement. When the tip of the tendril comes in contact with an uneven surface, it coils around the support.

Concept note-2: -Roots of a plant move towards water, so that they show positive hydrotropism. Roots always grow within the direction of water, whether or not they need to grow against the pull of gravity or sideways.

Concept note-3: -The movement of the roots of the plant towards the gravity or the interior of the earth is known as geotropism. This response is shown during movement of roots in search of water. So, the correct answer is ‘Geotropism’ Was this answer helpful?

Concept note-4: -Tendrils grow towards a support in response to their touch. This phenomenon is known as thigmotropism.
