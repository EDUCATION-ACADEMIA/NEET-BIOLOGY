The process of converting sunlight energy into chemical energy stored in a molecule is called

(A) Cellular respiration

(B) ** Photosynthesis

(C) Mitochondria

(D) Carbon dioxide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Photosynthesis is the process in which light energy is converted to chemical energy in the form of sugars. In a process driven by light energy, glucose molecules (or other sugars) are constructed from water and carbon dioxide, and oxygen is released as a byproduct.

Concept note-2: -Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create oxygen and energy in the form of sugar.

Concept note-3: -photosynthesis, the process by which green plants and certain other organisms transform light energy into chemical energy. During photosynthesis in green plants, light energy is captured and used to convert water, carbon dioxide, and minerals into oxygen and energy-rich organic compounds.

Concept note-4: -Solar energy (sunlight) is the foundation of photosynthesis; it is captured by plants and algae and converted into chemical energy (ATP).

Concept note-5: -Sunlight is converted to chemical energy in the form of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which is the main energy-storing molecule in living organisms. ATP is then transported throughout the chloroplast and used to provide the chemical energy necessary to power other metabolic reactions.
