TRANSPORT IN PLANT

[SOURCES]
When a farmer sprays a chemical on to crop plants, how does the chemical travel to the roots of the plants?

(A) ** In the phloem, by active translocation

(B) In the phloem, by transpiration pull

(C) In the xylem, by transpiration pull

(D) In the xylem, by active translocation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Plants use two different transport systems, both of which are rows of cells which form tubes around the plant.. The xylem transports water and minerals from the roots to the leaves while the phloem moves food substances from leaves to the rest of the plant.

Concept note-2: -When the plant opens its stomata to let in carbon dioxide, water on the surface of the cells of the spongy mesophyll and palisade mesophyll evaporates and diffuses out of the leaf. This process is called transpiration . Water is drawn from the cells in the xylem to replace that which has been lost from the leaves.

Concept note-3: -Translocation is the movement of elements within a plant from leaves to other tissue. Plantsâ€™ phloem, a type of vascular tissue, is where translocation takes place. Sugars that are produced by photosynthesis or that are stored in plant tissues like roots, bulbs, and tubers are examples of sugar sources.

Concept note-4: -Translocation occurs within a series of cells known as the phloem transport system, which is the most important digestive tissue of plants. Nutrients move to phloem as solutes in a solution called phloem liquid.
