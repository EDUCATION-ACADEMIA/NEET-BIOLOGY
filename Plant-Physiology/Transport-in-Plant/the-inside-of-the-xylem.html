EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lumen is the inside space of a cellular structure, for example xylem. Lumen also refers to the tubular structure, for example xylem, an artery or an intestine. Xylem vessels are responsible for transporting water and mineral ions from a plantâ€™s roots to its leaves.

Concept note-2: -Xylem lumen may be defined as the inner open space or a central hollow cavity present inside the xylem. It serves as the passage for the water and minerals to flow through it.

Concept note-3: -xylem, plant vascular tissue that conveys water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the rest of the plant and also provides physical support. Xylem tissue consists of a variety of specialized, water-conducting cells known as tracheary elements.

Concept note-4: -Xylem is a tissue consisting of dead, hollowed-out cells that form a system of pipes. The walls of xylem cells are lignified (strengthened with a substance called lignin ). This allows the xylem to withstand pressure changes as water moves through the plant.

Concept note-5: -Xylem vessels are hollow tubes or lumen with a thick strengthened cellulose cell wall. The hollow tubes act like pipes allowing water and dissolved minerals to flow through them.
