PLANT PHYSIOLOGY

TRANSPORT IN PLANT

[SOURCES]
which of the following substances are transportedby the plant transport system to different parts?A oxygenB waterC carbon dioxideD sugar

(A) A and D only

(B) A and C only

(C) ** B and D only

(D) A, B, C and D

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Oxygen and carbon dioxide are transported through tiny holes (pores) on the surface of leaves and stems through a network of air spaces within the plant to and from all living cells.

Concept note-2: -Plants have two transport systems-xylem and phloem . Xylem transports water and minerals. Phloem transports sugars and amino acids dissolved in water.

Concept note-3: -The xylem vessels and tracheids transport water and minerals from the roots to all parts of the plants.

Concept note-4: -These include oxygen, carbon dioxide, nutrients (such as glucose and amino acids), hormones and waste chemicals such as urea. These substances are transported in a medium called blood through the body through tubes called blood vessels.

Concept note-5: -Substances transported are glucose, glycerol, minerals, vitamins, fatty acids, amino acids, and hormones. Water enters the xylem to reach the leaves and is further diffused out through the process of transpiration. The movement of water is unidirectional.
