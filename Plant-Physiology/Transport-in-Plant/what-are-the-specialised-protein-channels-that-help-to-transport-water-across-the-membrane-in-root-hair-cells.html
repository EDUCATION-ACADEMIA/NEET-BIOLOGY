What are the specialised protein channels that help to transport water across the membrane in root hair cells?

(A) proton pumps

(B) apoptosis channels

(C) water channels

(D) ** aquaporins

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Aquaporins (AQP) are integral membrane proteins that serve as channels in the transfer of water, and in some cases, small solutes across the membrane. They are conserved in bacteria, plants, and animals. Structural analyses of the molecules have revealed the presence of a pore in the center of each aquaporin molecule.

Concept note-2: -Aquaporins are transmembrane proteins that regulate the flow of water into and out of cells.

Concept note-3: -Aquaporins are a type of membrane protein. These work as water channels in the membrane. They help in transporting water molecules and sometimes solutes across the membranes. This type of transport is known as facilitated diffusion.

Concept note-4: -Specialized membrane channel, proteins called aquaporins, facilitate water transport across cell membranes.

Concept note-5: -The primary function of most aquaporins is to transport water across cell membranes in response to osmotic gradients created by active solute transport.
