The equation for photosynthesis is

(A) oxygen + water → glucose + carbon dioxide

(B) oxygen + glucose → carbon dioxide + water

(C) ** carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen

(D) carbon dioxide + glucose → oxygen + water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The balanced version is: 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2.) In simple terms: carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen.

Concept note-2: -The process of photosynthesis is commonly written as: 6CO2 + 6H2O → C6H12O6 + 6O2. This means that the reactants, six carbon dioxide molecules and six water molecules, are converted by light energy captured by chlorophyll (implied by the arrow) into a sugar molecule and six oxygen molecules, the products.

Concept note-3: -Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen + Water.

Concept note-4: -Explanation: The chemical equation for photosynthesis is 6CO2+6H2O→C6H12O6+6O2. 6CO2+6H2O→C6H12O6+6O2. In plants, the process of photosynthesis takes place in the mesophyll of the leaves, inside the chloroplasts.
