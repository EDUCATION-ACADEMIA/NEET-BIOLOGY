True or false:Cells in the xylem vessel have no protoplasm.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem is a complex permanent tissue specialized for transport of water and minerals. The cells of xylem are dead at maturity and devoid of protoplasm except for xylem parenchyma cells, which are living cells.

Concept note-2: -They do not have any protoplasm at maturity. Tracheids are dead, tapered, and long lignified cells that are present in the xylem of plants. They are one of the water-conducting components in the xylem, which doesnâ€™t have any protoplasm.

Concept note-3: -Sclerenchyma cells are dead cells, and their cell walls are narrow and thick without any intercellular spaces. They do not contain any protoplasm at maturity.

Concept note-4: -Vessels are the main water transporting elements and devoid of protoplasm.

Concept note-5: -Mature xylem consists of elongated dead cells, arranged end to end to form continuous vessels (tubes). Mature xylem vessels: contain no cytoplasm.
