What happens to the water potential when sucrose is transported into the sieve tubes?

(A) No change

(B) Increases

(C) ** Decreases

(D) Random change

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1: Translocation to the sink: Sucrose is actively transported from source cells into companion cells and then into the sieve-tube elements. This reduces the water potential, which causes water to enter the phloem from the xylem.

Concept note-2: -Phloem sap is an aqueous solution that contains up to 30 percent sugar, minerals, amino acids, and plant growth regulators. The high percentage of sugar decreases s, which decreases the total water potential, causing water to move by osmosis from the adjacent xylem into the phloem tubes.

Concept note-3: -Once sugar is unloaded at the sink cells, the s increases, causing water to diffuse by osmosis from the phloem back into the xylem. This movement of water out of the phloem causes p to decrease, reducing the turgor pressure in the phloem at the sink and maintaining the direction of bulk flow from source to sink.

Concept note-4: -The water absorbed into the sieve tube creates hydrostatic pressure that forces the phloem sap to flow (bulk flow) towards the sink.

Concept note-5: -Meanwhile, at the sink, incoming sugars are actively transported out of the phloem and removed as complex carbohydrates. he loss of solutes produces a high water potential in the phloem and water asses out, returning eventually to xylem.
