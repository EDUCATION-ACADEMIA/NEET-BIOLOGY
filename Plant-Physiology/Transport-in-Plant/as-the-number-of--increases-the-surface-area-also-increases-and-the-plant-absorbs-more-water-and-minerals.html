TRANSPORT IN PLANT

[SOURCES]
As the number of ____ increases, the surface area also increases, and the plant absorbs more water and minerals.

(A) Root cap

(B) ** Root hair

(C) Root tip

(D) Root cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Root hairs are the small tubular extensions of the root surface which help in the absorption of water and minerals from the soil. It increases the surface area for absorption. More number of root hairs will increase the surface area and increase water absorption also.

Concept note-2: -Root hairs collect water and minerals that are present in the soil. They also increase the surface area for absorption.

Concept note-3: -Because they vastly increase the root surface area and effectively increase the root diameter, root hairs are generally thought to aid plants in nutrient acquisition, anchorage, and microbe interactions (Cutter, 1978; Hofer, 1991).

Concept note-4: -Root hairs are the structures which increase the surface area of absorption of water and minerals. They are delicate and very fine, thread-like structures, which arise from the region of maturation of the root.

Concept note-5: -Root hairs. Single celled and large surface area, perfect for absorption of water and nutrients! Root hairs are long, thin, single cell extensions from the epidermis. They profoundly increase the overall root surface area and connection with the soil and are responsible for absorbing water and mineral nutrients.
