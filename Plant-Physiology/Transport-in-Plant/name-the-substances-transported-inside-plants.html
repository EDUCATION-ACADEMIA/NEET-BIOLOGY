PLANT PHYSIOLOGY

TRANSPORT IN PLANT

[SOURCES]
Name the substances transported inside plants.

(A) Air and water

(B) food and water

(C) air, food and water

(D) ** food. water and minerals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem transports water, minerals, and nutrients from the soil to all the plant parts. There are two types of “transport” tissues in plants-xylem and phloem. Water and solutes are transported by the xylem from the roots to the leaves, and food is transported from the leaves to the rest of the plant by the phloem.

Concept note-2: -Plants have two transport systems-xylem and phloem . Xylem transports water and minerals. Phloem transports sugars and amino acids dissolved in water.

Concept note-3: -Substances transported are glucose, glycerol, minerals, vitamins, fatty acids, amino acids, and hormones. Water enters the xylem to reach the leaves and is further diffused out through the process of transpiration. The movement of water is unidirectional.

Concept note-4: -Plants require two types of nutrients-macronutrients and micronutrients. Macronutrients include nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium and sulphur. The micronutrients include boron, chlorine, copper, iron, manganese, molybdenum, and zinc.
