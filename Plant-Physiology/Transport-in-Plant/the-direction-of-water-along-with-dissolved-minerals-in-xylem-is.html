PLANT PHYSIOLOGY

TRANSPORT IN PLANT

[SOURCES]
The direction of water along with dissolved minerals in xylem is

(A) Bidirectional

(B) Multidirectional

(C) From shoot to roots

(D) ** From roots to shoot

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem transports water, minerals, and nutrients from the soil to all the plant parts. There are two types of “transport” tissues in plants-xylem and phloem. Water and solutes are transported by the xylem from the roots to the leaves, and food is transported from the leaves to the rest of the plant by the phloem.

Concept note-2: -The direction of xylem transport is upwards and unidirectional i.e. from roots to other plant parts. The water column moves only upwards because of the two forces, transpirational pull, and positive root pressure.

Concept note-3: -Water from the soil enters the root hairs by moving along a water potential gradient and into the xylem through either the apoplast or symplast pathway. It is carried upward through the xylem by transpiration, and then passed into the leaves along another water potential gradient.

Concept note-4: -Water Transport from Roots to Shoots The phloem and xylem are the main tissues responsible for this movement. Water potential, evapotranspiration, and stomatal regulation influence how water and nutrients are transported in plants.

Concept note-5: -Biology definition: Xylem is a type of vascular tissue in plants. It is primarily involved in transporting water and minerals (from the roots to the shoot and leaves) and providing structural support. It is found in the stems and leaves of vascular plants.
