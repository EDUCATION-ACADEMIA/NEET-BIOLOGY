A white blood cell that destroys pathogens by digesting them is called a ____

(A) ** macrophage

(B) T cell

(C) B cell

(D) antibody

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Listen to pronunciation. (MA-kroh-fayj) A type of white blood cell that surrounds and kills microorganisms, removes dead cells, and stimulates the action of other immune system cells.

Concept note-2: -A type of immune cell that is made in the bone marrow and travels through the blood to tissues in the body where it becomes a macrophage or a dendritic cell. Macrophages surround and kill microorganisms, ingest foreign material, remove dead cells, and boost immune responses.

Concept note-3: -Macrophages and neutrophils (phagocytes) are the front-line defenders in your bodyâ€™s immune system. They seek out, ingest, and destroy pathogens and other debris through a process called phagocytosis.

Concept note-4: -Within the liver, macrophages are given the specific name of Kupffer cells. Kupffer cells scavenge the liver for potential pathogens that could have entered the bloodstream from the gastrointestinal tract.

Concept note-5: -The process where these white blood cells surround, engulf, and destroy foreign substances is called phagocytosis, and the cells are collectively referred to as phagocytes. Phagocytes eventually die. Pus is formed from a collection of dead tissue, dead bacteria, and live and dead phagocytes.
