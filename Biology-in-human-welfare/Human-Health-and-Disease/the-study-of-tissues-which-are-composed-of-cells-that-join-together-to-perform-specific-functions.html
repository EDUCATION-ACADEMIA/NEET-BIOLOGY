HUMAN HEALTH AND DISEASE

[SOURCES]
The study of tissues, which are composed of cells that join together to perform specific functions.

(A) Pathology

(B) ** Histology

(C) Biology

(D) Cytology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -histology, branch of biology concerned with the composition and structure of plant and animal tissues in relation to their specialized functions. The terms histology and microscopic anatomy are sometimes used interchangeably, but a fine distinction can be drawn between the two studies.

Concept note-2: -Histology is a discipline of science concerned with the study of various tissues and their structures. The structure of every single tissue in the human body is linked to its various functions. As a result, histology is intimately linked to both physiology and anatomy.

Concept note-3: -The correct option is A histology. The study of tissues is called histology. Cytology is the study of cells.

Concept note-4: -(HIS-toh-puh-THAH-loh-jee) The study of diseased cells and tissues using a microscope.

Concept note-5: -There are 4 basic types of tissue: connective tissue, epithelial tissue, muscle tissue, and nervous tissue.
