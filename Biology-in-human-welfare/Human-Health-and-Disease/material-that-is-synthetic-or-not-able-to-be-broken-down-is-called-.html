Material that is synthetic or not able to be broken down is called ____

(A) biodegradable

(B) trash

(C) ** non-degradable

(D) hazardous waste

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Other substances or materials like plastics, metallic cans and pesticides which cannot be broken down easily by biological processes are named non-biodegradable substances.

Concept note-2: -Non-biodegradable materials are often synthetic products like plastic, glass and batteries. Because they donâ€™t break down easily, if not disposed of properly, non-biodegradable waste can cause pollution, block drains and harm animals.

Concept note-3: -A Non-Biodegradable material can be defined as a kind of substance which cannot be broken down by natural organisms and acts as a source of pollution. Unlike biodegradable wastes, non-biodegradable cannot be easily handled. Non-biodegradable wastes are those that cannot be decomposed or dissolved by natural agents.

Concept note-4: -Meaning of non-degradable in English A non-degradable substance will not degrade (= change into a more simple chemical structure over time): One of the dangers of lead is the fact that it is non-degradable, which means that it remains in the atmosphere forever.

Concept note-5: -Some common examples of non-biodegradable wastes are plastic, glass, metals, etc. Metals, glass and plastic can be recycled if they are conserved properly after using them once.
