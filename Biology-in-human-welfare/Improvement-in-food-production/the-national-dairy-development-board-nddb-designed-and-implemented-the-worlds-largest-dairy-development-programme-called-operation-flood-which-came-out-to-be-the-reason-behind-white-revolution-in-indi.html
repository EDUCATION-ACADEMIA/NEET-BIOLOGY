IMPROVEMENT IN FOOD PRODUCTION

[SOURCES]
The National Dairy Development Board (NDDB) designed and implemented the world’s largest dairy development programme, called “Operation Flood”, which came out to be the reason behind White Revolution in India. Can you name the man who is known as the Father of White Revolution?

(A) Dr. G Nammalvar

(B) Abdul Rashid

(C) Dr. Jagadish Chandra Bose

(D) ** Dr. V. Kurien

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Verghese Kurien was the Founder Chairman of National Dairy Development Board from 1965 to 1998. He is the architect of India’s White Revolution, which helped India emerge as the largest milk producer in the world. In the late 60s Dr. Kurien drew up a project called Operation Flood.

Concept note-2: -Operation Flood: one of the world’s largest rural development programmes. Launched in 1970, Operation Flood has helped dairy farmers direct their own development, placing control of the resources they create in their own hands.

Concept note-3: -Operation Flood was launched in 1970 under Dr. Kurien. This program linked all the milk producers across India to the consumers in over more than 600 towns and cities reducing the seasonal and regional price of milk.

Concept note-4: -Verghese Kurien (26 November 1921-9 September 2012), known as the “Father of the White Revolution” in India, was a social entrepreneur whose “billion-litre idea", Operation Flood, made dairy farming India’s largest self-sustaining industry and the largest rural employment sector providing a third of all rural income.

Concept note-5: -Operation Flood started in the year 1970 and was aimed to create a nationwide milk grid. It was a rural development programme initiated by NDDB – National Dairy Development Board of India.
