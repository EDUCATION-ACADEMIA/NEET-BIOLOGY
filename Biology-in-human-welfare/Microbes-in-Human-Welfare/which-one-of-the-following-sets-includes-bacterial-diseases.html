Which one of the following sets includes bacterial diseases?

(A) Tetanus, tuberculosis, measles

(B) ** Diphtheria, leprosy, plague

(C) Cholera, typhoid, mumps

(D) Malaria, mumps, poliomyelitis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Diptheria, leprosy, plague, tetanus, tuberculosis, cholera and typhoid are diseases caused by bacteria. Mumps, polio and measles are caused by viruses.

Concept note-2: -Thus, the correct answer is ‘Diphtheria, Leprosy, Plague. ‘

Concept note-3: -The bacteria Mycobacterium leprae causes leprosy, which is a chronic infection. Corynebacterium diphtheria causes diphtheria, a deadly bacterial infection.

Concept note-4: -Tuberculosis, cholera, tetanus, typhoid, diphtheria, pertussis (whooping cough), plague and pneumonia are bacterial diseases.

Concept note-5: -Common bacterial diseases include UTIs, food poisoning, STIs and some skin, sinus and ear infections. They’re often treated with antibiotics.
