What is the name for the part of the sex cells where the genetic material (chromosomes) are located?

(A) Cytoplasm

(B) ** Nucleus

(C) Mitochondrion

(D) Stomata

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is the cellâ€™s genetic material, contained in chromosomes within the cell nucleus and mitochondria.

Concept note-2: -The nucleus of each sex cell contains half of the original genetic material.

Concept note-3: -In the nucleus of each cell, the DNA molecule is packaged into thread-like structures called chromosomes. Each chromosome is made up of DNA tightly coiled many times around proteins called histones that support its structure.

Concept note-4: -Two of the chromosomes (the X and the Y chromosome) determine your sex as male or female when you are born. They are called sex chromosomes: Females have 2 X chromosomes. Males have 1 X and 1 Y chromosome.

Concept note-5: -The X chromosome is part of chromosome group C, and the Y chromosome is part of group G. Bands and sub-bands are annotated to the right of each chromosome (or chromosome pair), and the gene for the sex-determining region Y protein is located at Yp11. 2.
