[SOURCES]
In uterus, endometrium increases in thickness in response to

(A) Oxytocin

(B) ** oestrogen

(C) LH

(D) Relaxin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The normal endometrium increases in thickness during the proliferative phase of the menstrual cycle in response to estrogen. Following ovulation, the endometrium provides an attachment site for and a source of nourishment to an early embryo until the placenta develops.

Concept note-2: -Estrogens act through specific receptors located in the nuclei of epithelial and stromal cells of the endometrium. Estrogens stimulate the synthesis of these receptors and the progesterone inhibits their synthesis. Estrogens induce the proliferation of the mucosa during the proliferative phase.

Concept note-3: -During the first part of the cycle, the hormone estrogen is made by the ovaries. Estrogen causes the lining to grow and thicken to prepare the uterus for pregnancy.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct answer is ‘Oestrogen’.

Concept note-5: -When the follicular phase begins, levels of estrogen and progesterone are low. As a result, the top layers of the thickened lining of the uterus (endometrium) break down and are shed, and menstrual bleeding occurs.
