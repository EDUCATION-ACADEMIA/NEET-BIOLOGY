What are the major female sex organs that produce eggs?

(A) Fallopian Tubes

(B) Testes

(C) ** Ovaries

(D) Gametes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ovaries: Ovaries are small, oval-shaped glands that are located on either side of your uterus. Your ovaries produce eggs and hormones. Fallopian tubes: These are narrow tubes that are attached to the upper part of your uterus and serve as pathways for your egg (ovum) to travel from your ovaries to your uterus.

Concept note-2: -The ovaries are two oval-shaped organs that lie to the upper right and left of the uterus. They produce, store, and release eggs into the fallopian tubes in the process called ovulation (av-yoo-LAY-shun).

Concept note-3: -The major hormones secreted by the ovaries are oestrogen and progesterone, both important hormones in the menstrual cycle.

Concept note-4: -Taken together, in both fertile and infertile women the fertility potential of oocytes from the right ovary surpasses that of the left ovary.

Concept note-5: -One of a pair of female glands in which the eggs form and the female hormones estrogen and progesterone are made. These hormones play an important role in female traits, such as breast development, body shape, and body hair. They are also involved in the menstrual cycle, fertility, and pregnancy.
