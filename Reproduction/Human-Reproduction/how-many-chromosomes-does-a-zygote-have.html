How many chromosomes does a zygote have?

(A) ** 46

(B) 23

(C) Half

(D) 42

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In humans, each cell normally contains 23 pairs of chromosomes, for a total of 46. Twenty-two of these pairs, called autosomes, look the same in both males and females. The 23rd pair, the sex chromosomes, differ between males and females.

Concept note-2: -Egg and sperm cells have just 23 chromosomes each. Thatâ€™s half as many chromosomes as regular cells. Through the process of fertilization, egg and sperm join to make a cell with 46 chromosomes (23 pairs), called a zygote.

Concept note-3: -The number of chromosomes in the gamete is haploid and human gametes contain 23 chromosomes. As zygote is from the fusion of two gametes, the chromosome number is diploid and is double of that in gamete and thus has 46 chromosomes.

Concept note-4: -Zygote is formed due to the fusion of male and female gametes. Gametes are haploid cells. Thus fusion of two haploid cells results in the formation of a diploid cell. Therefore zygote is a diploid cell with 46 chromosomes.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: There are 46 chromosomes in a human zygote. Human zygotes are formed from the fusion of a sperm cell with an egg cell.
