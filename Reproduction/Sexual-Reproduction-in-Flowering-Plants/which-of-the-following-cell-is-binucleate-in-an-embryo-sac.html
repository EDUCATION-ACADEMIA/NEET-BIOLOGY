Which of the following cell is binucleate in an embryo sac?

(A) Antipodal cell

(B) ** Central cell

(C) Female gamete

(D) synergid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Polygonum-type central cell is a binucleate cell that, upon fertilization with one of the two sperm cells, forms triploid endosperm to nourish embryo development.

Concept note-2: -So, the correct answer is ‘Binucleate’

Concept note-3: -Binucleated cells are cells that contain two nuclei. This type of cell is most commonly found in cancer cells and may arise from a variety of causes.

Concept note-4: -The nucellus forms the central part of the ovule comprising the embryo sac.

Concept note-5: -The mature embryo sac consists of four cell types; the egg cell and the two synergids at the micropylar end, the antipodals at the chalazal end and the central cell containing the two polar nuclei and a large central vacuole (B).
