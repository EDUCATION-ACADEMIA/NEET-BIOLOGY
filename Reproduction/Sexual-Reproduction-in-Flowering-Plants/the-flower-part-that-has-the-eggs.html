The flower part that has the eggs

(A) Stamen

(B) Stigma

(C) ** Pistil

(D) Style

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -ovary, in botany, enlarged basal portion of the pistil, the female organ of a flower. The ovary contains ovules, which develop into seeds upon fertilization.

Concept note-2: -The ovary contains eggs, which reside in ovules. If an egg is fertilized, the ovule develops into a seed. Sepals are small, green, leaflike structures located at the base of a flower. They protect the flower bud.

Concept note-3: -Ovary is the enlarged basal portion of the pistil which is the female organ of a flower. It contains ovules that develop into seeds upon fertilization.

Concept note-4: -Pistil: The ovule producing part of a flower. The ovary often supports a long style, topped by a stigma. The mature ovary is a fruit, and the mature ovule is a seed. Stigma: The part of the pistil where pollen germinates.

Concept note-5: -Once the pollen is in the ovary of the pistil, the pollen will fertilize the ovules, which are the female egg cells in the pistil. When the ovules become fertilized, they will ripen and develop into seeds.
