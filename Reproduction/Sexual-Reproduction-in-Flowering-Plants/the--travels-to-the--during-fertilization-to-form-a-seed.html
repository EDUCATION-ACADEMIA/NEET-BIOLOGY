The ____ travels to the ____ during fertilization to form a seed.

(A) anther travels to the ovule

(B) ** pollen travels to the ovule

(C) anther travels to thestigma

(D) pollen travels to the stigma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Step one: After pollen has landed on the stigma, it grows a pollen tube down through the style to the ovary. Step two: The nucleus of the pollen grain travels down the pollen tube and fertilises the nucleus in the ovule. Step three: The fertilised ovule develops into a seed.

Concept note-2: -From the anther the pollen grains are first deposited on the pistilâ€™s surface, the stigma. There they germinate and form pollen tubes, which grow downward through the style toward the ovules. Fertilization occurs as a sperm cell in a pollen tube fuses with the egg cell of an ovule, resulting in a plant embryo.

Concept note-3: -A pollen grain lands on the sticky stigma. A pollen tube grows from the pollen grain, and down the style, carrying a male gamete with it down the tube to meet the female gamete in an ovule. The male and female gamete fuse and fertilization occurs.

Concept note-4: -After pollen lands on the stigma, it initiates pollen tube formation. Pollen tubes are channels which grow down the length of the style to the ovules. After a pollen grain travels down a pollen tube it intercepts an ovule, fertilizes the female germ cells and initiates seed development.

Concept note-5: -A pollen grain on the stigma grows a tiny tube, all the way down the style to the ovary. This pollen tube carries a male gamete to meet a female gamete in an ovule.
