SEXUAL REPRODUCTION IN FLOWERING PLANTS

[SOURCES]
I have hooks to cling to animal fur. What is likely my dispersal method?

(A) wind

(B) water

(C) ** animals

(D) explosive action

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Animals can disperse seeds by excreting or burying them; other fruits have structures, such as hooks, that attach themselves to animals’ fur.

Concept note-2: -Most common of the stick-to-fur weed seeds are those burdocks with the hooked spines. Fortunately, most gardeners are able to recognize this weed and pull it up to to keep it out of their gardens.

Concept note-3: –Seeds with hooks attach to the fur of animals or clothes of humans and are dispersed to a sufficient distance. They might then fall off and germinate into a new plant under suitable conditions. So the correct answer is option C) Animals.

Concept note-4: -Many plants produce fruits or individual seeds covered in hooks or spines which attach the seed to the animals’s fur or feathers-or, in the case of humans, to our clothes or bags. The seeds are then carried a sufficient distance from the parent plant to give them space to grow.

Concept note-5: -Xanthium and urena seeds are adapted to be dispersed by the hooks and furs of animals.
