SEXUAL REPRODUCTION IN FLOWERING PLANTS

[SOURCES]
One male gamete fertilises an egg cell to form ____

(A) ** diploid zygote

(B) triploid nuclei

(C) haploid embryo

(D) endosperm

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After reaching the micropyle of an ovule and breaking out of the end of the pollen tube, one sperm unites with the egg cell to form a diploid zygote (i.e., a fertilized egg with two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent). This is the first of the two fertilizations.

Concept note-2: -Fertilization is the process in which haploid gametes fuse to form a diploid cell called a zygote. To ensure that each zygote has the correct number of chromosomes, only one sperm can fuse with one egg.

Concept note-3: -Egg and sperm cells have just 23 chromosomes each. Thatâ€™s half as many chromosomes as regular cells. Through the process of fertilization, egg and sperm join to make a cell with 46 chromosomes (23 pairs), called a zygote.

Concept note-4: -During fertilization, the sperm penetrates and releases its nucleic material into the egg. This fertilized egg then has genetic material from both gametes, and is therefore a diploid zygote.

Concept note-5: -The zygote is endowed with genes from two parents, and thus it is diploid (carrying two sets of chromosomes). The joining of haploid gametes to produce a diploid zygote is a common feature in the sexual reproduction of all organisms except bacteria.
