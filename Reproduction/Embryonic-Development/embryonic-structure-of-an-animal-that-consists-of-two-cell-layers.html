EMBRYONIC DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
Embryonic structure of an animal that consists of TWO cell layers

(A) blastula

(B) ** gastrula

(C) morula

(D) blastocyst

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -gastrula, early multicellular embryo, composed of two or more germinal layers of cells from which the various organs later derive. The gastrula develops from the hollow, single-layered ball of cells called a blastula which itself is the product of the repeated cell division, or cleavage, of a fertilized egg.

Concept note-2: -During gastrulation, a hollow cluster of cells called a blastula reorganizes into two primary germ layers: an inner layer, called endoderm, and an outer layer, called ectoderm.

Concept note-3: -Gastrulation is defined as an early developmental process in which an embryo transforms from a one-dimensional layer of epithelial cells (blastula) and reorganizes into a multilayered and multidimensional structure called the gastrula.

Concept note-4: -Animals develop either two or three embryonic germs layers (Figure). The animals that display radial symmetry develop two germ layers, an inner layer (endoderm) and an outer layer (ectoderm). These animals are called diploblasts. Diploblasts have a non-living layer between the endoderm and ectoderm.

Concept note-5: -During gastrulation, the cells are differentiated into the ectoderm or mesendoderm, which then separates into the mesoderm and endoderm. The endoderm and mesoderm form due to the nodal signaling.
