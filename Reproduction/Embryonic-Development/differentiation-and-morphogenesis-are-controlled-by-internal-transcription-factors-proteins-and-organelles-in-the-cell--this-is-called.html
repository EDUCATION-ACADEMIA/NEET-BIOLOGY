Differentiation and morphogenesis are controlled by internal transcription factors, proteins and organelles in the cell ____ this is called

(A) ** cytoplasmic determinants

(B) inductive signals

(C) endosymbiotic theory

(D) epigenetic inheritance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cytoplasmic determinants are also found in some post-embryonic cells, where they produce cytoplasmic asymmetry (q.v.). In dividing cells, this leads to asymmetric cell division in which each of the daughter cells differentiates into a different cell type.

Concept note-2: -Moreover, cell differentiation is controlled by transcription factors, while morphogenesis is controlled by the spatial and temporal control of embryo mechanics. Also, cell movements play an important role in morphogenesis, unlike differentiation.

Concept note-3: -The main role of cytoplasmic determinants is the regulation of gene expression. Due to the uneven distribution of cytoplasmic determinants within the cytoplasm, the daughter cells inherit different amounts of them during cell division. Therefore, the gene expression in daughter cells may also differ from each other.

Concept note-4: -The localized cytoplasmic determinants are often mRNAs encoding transcription factors, or the transcription factors themselves.
