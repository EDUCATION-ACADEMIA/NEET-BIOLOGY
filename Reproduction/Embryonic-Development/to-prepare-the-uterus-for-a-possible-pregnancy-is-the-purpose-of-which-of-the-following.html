To prepare the uterus for a possible pregnancy is the purpose of which of the following?

(A) the reproductive system

(B) the endocrine system

(C) the urinary system

(D) ** the menstrual cycle

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Progesterone prepares your uterus for a fertilized egg to implant. If intercourse has taken place and sperm has fertilized the egg (conception), the fertilized egg (embryo) will travel through your fallopian tube to implant in your uterus. This is how pregnancy begins.

Concept note-2: -The luteal phase is one of four phases of the menstrual cycle. The main purpose of the luteal phase is to prepare your uterus for a possible pregnancy. During this phase of your cycle, an egg travels from your ovary through your fallopian tube and to your uterus.

Concept note-3: -The purpose of the menstrual cycle is to prepare the body for a possible pregnancy. During the menstrual cycle, a mature egg is produced by the ovaries and the lining of the uterus thickens to support a possible pregnancy.

Concept note-4: -The Luteal Phase This phase involves the preparation of the uterine lining, called the endometrium, for potential embryo implantation. A large spike in progesterone levels, another hormone produced by the ovaries, stimulates uterine lining growth to create a friendly environment for a developing embryo.

Concept note-5: -You’re most fertile at the time of ovulation (when an egg is released from your ovaries), which usually occurs 12 to 14 days before your next period starts. This is the time of the month when you’re most likely to get pregnant. It’s unlikely that you’ll get pregnant just after your period, although it can happen.
