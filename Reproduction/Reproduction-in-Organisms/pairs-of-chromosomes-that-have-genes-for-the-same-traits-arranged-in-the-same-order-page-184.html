Pairs of chromosomes that have genes for the same traits arranged in the same order. page 184

(A) Diploid

(B) ** homologous

(C) chromosomes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Homologous chromosomes are the chromosomes which contain alleles for the same type of genes (one from sperm and one from egg) or can be called similar. Homologous chromosomes are similiar but not identical. Each carries the same genes in the same order, but the alleles for each trait may not be the same.

Concept note-2: -The chromosomes in a homologous pair each have the same genes in the same order, but there may be variation between them, resulting in different alleles.

Concept note-3: -In humans, the twenty-third pair is the sex chromosomes, while the first 22 pairs are called autosomes. Typically, biologically female individuals have two X chromosomes (XX) while those who are biologically male have one X and one Y chromosome (XY).

Concept note-4: -The first 22 pairs of chromosomes are known as autosomes. These are homologous chromosomes, which means that their chromosomal sides have the same genes (DNA sections) in a similar sequence.

Concept note-5: -In biology, homologous chromosomes are paired chromosomes. They essentially have the same gene sequence, loci (gene position), centromere location, and chromosomal length.
