REPRODUCTION IN ORGANISMS

[SOURCES]
In general, asexual reproduction is used by ____ organisms.

(A) ** simple

(B) extinct

(C) complex

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Asexual reproduction is the primary form of reproduction for single-celled organisms such as archaea and bacteria. Many eukaryotic organisms including plants, animals, and fungi can also reproduce asexually.

Concept note-2: -Some species of plants, animals, and fungi are capable of both sexual and asexual reproduction, depending on the demands of the environment. Asexual reproduction is practiced by most single-celled organisms including bacteria, archaebacteria, and protists. It is also practiced by some plants, animals, and fungi.

Concept note-3: -Following are the examples of asexual reproduction: Bacterium undergoes binary fission in which the cell divides into two along with the nucleus. Blackworms or mud worms reproduce through fragmentation. Hydras reproduce through budding. Organisms such as copperheads undergo parthenogenesis.

Concept note-4: -Binary fission is a type of asexual reproduction wherein a cell divides to produce two identical cells. Concept note-5: -Final answer: A form of asexual reproduction used by a single-celled organism to produce two identical daughter cells is binary fission.
