In human meiosis, the gametes produced from the diploid parental cells will be

(A) diploid and have 46 chromosomes

(B) diploid and have 23 chromosomes

(C) ** haploid and have 23 chromosomes

(D) identical to the diploid parental cells

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A typical diploid human cell contains 23 pairs of chromosomes; 46 total, half of maternal origin and half of paternal origin. Meiosis produces haploid gametes with one set of 23 chromosomes. When two gametes fuse, the resulting zygote is once again diploid, with the mother and father each contributing 23 chromosomes.

Concept note-2: -Egg and sperm cells have just 23 chromosomes each. Thatâ€™s half as many chromosomes as regular cells. Through the process of fertilization, egg and sperm join to make a cell with 46 chromosomes (23 pairs), called a zygote. For each chromosome pair, one homologous chromosome came from each parent.

Concept note-3: -Normally, each cell in the human body has 23 pairs of chromosomes (46 total chromosomes). Half come from the mother; the other half come from the father. Two of the chromosomes (the X and the Y chromosome) determine your sex as male or female when you are born.

Concept note-4: -At this point, nuclear division begins, and the parent cell is divided in half, forming 2 daughter cells. Each daughter cell will have half of the original 46 chromosomes, or 23 chromosomes.

Concept note-5: -Gametes are produced through a type of cell division called meiosis and hence are haploid in nature. They carry only one copy of each chromosome and therefore contain only 23 chromosomes.
