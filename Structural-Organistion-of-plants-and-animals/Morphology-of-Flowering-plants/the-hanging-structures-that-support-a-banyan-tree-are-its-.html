The hanging structures that support a banyan tree are its ____

(A) stilt roots

(B) tap roots

(C) ** prop roots

(D) pneumatophores

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hanging roots of banyan trees are known as prop roots. These are hanging adventitious roots arising from the aerial branches growing downwards, to provide extra structural support to plants.

Concept note-2: -Banyan trees contain a prop root system, which gives mechanical help to the aerial branches. The roots of the banyan tree are mature and thick woody trunks. The roots of the banyan trees are helpful to trees for growing over a large area.

Concept note-3: -Answer: The banyan tree has a special type of roots, that is, aerial prop roots.

Concept note-4: -Prop roots are the roots that grows from the stem into the ground and gives support to the branches of the tree.

Concept note-5: -Roots that are modified to give support to the plant is known as prop root. They develop from the branches of the tree, hang downward reach the ground, and penetrate the ground to support the tree. Example of plant with prop roots is rubber plants and the banyan tree.
