[SOURCES]
A few millimeters above the root cap is the region of-

(A) Elongation

(B) Maturation

(C) ** Meristematic activity

(D) Root hairs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The region of meristematic activity is situated few millimeters above root cap.

Concept note-2: -Cells of region of meristematic activity are very large, thin-walled and with dense protoplasm. III. this zone, proximal to region of elongation, is called the region of maturation.

Concept note-3: -The region of Meristematic Cells or Region of Cell Division Meristematic cells are found just a few millimetres over the root cap. The cells in this region are thin-walled with dense protoplasm.

Concept note-4: -The root apical meristem, or root apex, is a small region at the tip of a root in which all cells are capable of repeated division and from which all primary root tissues are derived. The root apical meristem is protected as it passes through the soil by an outer region of living parenchyma cells called the root cap.
