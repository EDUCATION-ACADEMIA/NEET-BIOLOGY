ANATOMY OF FLOWERING PLANTS

[SOURCES]
Y-shaped arrangement of xylem vessels is found in

(A) ** Monocot stem

(B) Dicot stem

(C) Monocot root

(D) Dicot root

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem is in the form of the letter Y. It is endarch, i.e., protoxylem lies towards the center of the stem. Xylem is made up of vessels, tracheids, xylem parenchyma and a few xylem fibers. Metaxylem generally consists of two large oval of rounded vessels lying at the upper two angles of xylem.

Concept note-2: -Monocot stem The vascular tissue is arranged into bundles of xylem and phloem that are scattered throughout the ground tissue. Within each bundle, the phloem is located closer to the outside of the stem, and the xylem is located closer to the inside of the stem.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct answer is ‘Monocot stem’

Concept note-4: -In monocots, the vascular bundles have a random appearance scattered throughout the stem. In contrast, they are arranged in a circle near the outer edge just below the epidermis in dicots (Fig. 2). In addition, monocots usually do not have a vascular cambium, and thus they are unable to undergo secondary growth.

Concept note-5: -The xylem vessels are V or Y shaped. The protoxylem disintegrates forming protoxylem lacuna. Due to this, the vascular bundles appear skull-shaped.
