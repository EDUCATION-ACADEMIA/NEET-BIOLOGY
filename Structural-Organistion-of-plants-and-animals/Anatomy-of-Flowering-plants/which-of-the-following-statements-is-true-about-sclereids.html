Which of the following statements is true about Sclereids?

(A) These are constituent cells of Collenchyma tissue

(B) They are living cells with very narrow lumen (Cavity)

(C) They donot provide mechanical strength to plants

(D) ** The gritty pulp of fruits like guava and pear is due to it’s presence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sclereids are found in fruit walls of nuts, the pulp of fruits like guava, pear, and sapota, and seed coats of legumes.

Concept note-2: -Sclereids are a reduced form of sclerenchyma cells with highly thickened, lignified cellular walls that form small bundles of durable layers of tissue in most plants. The presence of numerous sclereids form the cores of apples and produce the gritty texture of guavas.

Concept note-3: -So, the correct answer is ‘Sclereids.

Concept note-4: -The grittiness of pear fruit is caused by stone cells which are also known as sclereids. Sclereids are sclerenchyma cells with thick, lignified walls that function in providing structural support and strength to tissues. It removes the stress in the fruit which is caused due to over-ripening.
