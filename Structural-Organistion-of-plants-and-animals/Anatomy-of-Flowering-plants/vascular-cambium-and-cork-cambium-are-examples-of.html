Vascular cambium and cork cambium are examples of

(A) Apical meristem

(B) ** Lateral meristem

(C) Intercalary meristem

(D) Promeristem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Assertion :Fascicular vascular cambium, interfascicular cambium and cork-cambium are examples of lateral meristems Reason: These are responsible for producing the secondary tissues.

Concept note-2: -Secondary, or lateral, meristems, which are found in all woody plants and in some herbaceous ones, consist of the vascular cambium and the cork cambium. They produce secondary tissues from a ring of vascular cambium in stems and roots.

Concept note-3: -The vascular cambium and cork cambium are secondary meristems that are formed in stems and roots after the tissues of the primary plant body have differentiated.

Concept note-4: -So, the correct answer is ‘Lateral meristem’.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is Lateral Meristem. Cork cambium is the type of cambium that is related to the formation of the wood in the plants. Lateral meristem is the meristematic tissue that is situated at the peripheral zone of the plant both at the stem and the roots. Hence, Option 2 is correct.
