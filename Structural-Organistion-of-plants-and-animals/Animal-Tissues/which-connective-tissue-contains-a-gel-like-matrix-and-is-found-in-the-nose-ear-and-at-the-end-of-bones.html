ANIMAL TISSUES

[SOURCES]
Which connective tissue contains a gel-like matrix and is found in the nose, ear, and at the end of bones.

(A) Bone

(B) ** Cartilage

(C) Blood

(D) Adipose

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cartilage is a unique tissue type because it doesn’t have blood vessels or nerves. Instead, cartilage cells (known as chondrocytes) are found in a gel-like “matrix” that provides nourishment to the cells.

Concept note-2: -The cartilage is the flexible connective tissue present in the ears, nose, and joints of the body.

Concept note-3: -Cartilage is a connective tissue with a large amount of the matrix and variable amounts of fibers. The cells, called chondrocytes, make the matrix and fibers of the tissue. Chondrocytes are found in spaces within the tissue called lacunae.

Concept note-4: -They are primarily composed of hyaline cartilage, which is densely packed with collagen, a structural protein.

Concept note-5: -Elastic cartilage tissue or yellow cartilage is the connective tissue that is found in body organs that do not bear body load, for example, the nose, ear, eustachian tube, and epiglottis. Elastic cartilage is a type of dense connective tissue.
