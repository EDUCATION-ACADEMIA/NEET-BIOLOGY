ANIMAL TISSUES

[SOURCES]
which is the heterocrine gland

(A) ovary

(B) testis

(C) ** pancreas

(D) kidney

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Heterocrine glands are glands that have both exocrine and endocrine parts. The pancreas is called a heterocrine gland because it secretes the hormone insulin as the endocrine ability of the blood and the chemicals as the exocrine ability of the gastrointestinal tract.

Concept note-2: -Final answer: One example of heterocrine gland is the pancreas.

Concept note-3: -The pancreas is called the heterochronous gland. The pancreas secretes the hormone insulin into the bloodstream which is an endocrine function.

Concept note-4: -Heterocrine glands (also known as mixed glands) are the glands which function as both exocrine gland and endocrine gland. These include the pancreas and the gonads (testes and ovaries).

Concept note-5: -A heterocrine gland is the one that serves as both exocrine (where the secretions are carried by ducts to specific organ) as well as endocrine gland (where secretions are released directly into bloodstream). Pancreas, which is located in the abdominal region is an example of heterocrine gland.
