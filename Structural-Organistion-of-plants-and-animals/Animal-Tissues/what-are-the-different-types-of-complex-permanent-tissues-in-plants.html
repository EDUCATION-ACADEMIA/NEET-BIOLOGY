What are the different types of complex permanent tissues in plants?

(A) ** Xylem and Phloem

(B) Parenchyma

(C) Phloem only

(D) Collenchyma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Xylem and phloem are the two different types of complex permanent tissues.

Concept note-2: -The complex tissue present in xylem and phloem is vascular tissue. It is a conducting tissue. Its main function is to conduct or transport fluid and nutrients from one part to another part in plant. In xylem, vascular tissue transports water and minerals from the roots to different parts of the plant.

Concept note-3: -Complex permanent Tissue It provides support to the plants. It is divided into-tracheids, vessels, xylem fiber, and xylem parenchyma.

Concept note-4: -Xylem and phloem are known as complex permanent tissues because both of them are made up of more than one type of cells.

Concept note-5: -Complex permanent tissue is defined as a collection of structurally dissimilar cells performing a common function or set of functions. They are made up of more than one type of cells. They are of two types xylem and phloem.
