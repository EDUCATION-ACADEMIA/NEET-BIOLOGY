ANIMAL TISSUES

[SOURCES]
Xylem and phloem are conductive tissue in plants that transport water and nutrients. Compare and name the tissue with similar function that is present in animals? Veins and nutritive tissues transport water and nutrients in plants. What is the name of the tissue in animals that has a similar function?

(A) A reolar tissue is an areolar tissue

(B) ** Blood

(C) Bone

(D) Cartilage is cartilage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: Xylem and phloem are the conducting tissues of the vascular strands. Their main role is in the transportation of food, minerals, and water. Xylem is the tissue which is responsible for the transport of water in plants while the phloem is responsible for the transfer of food and nutrients in the plant.

Concept note-2: -Xylem carries water and minerals from the roots to the leaves. Whereas, phloem carries the food prepared by the leaves to different parts of the plant.

Concept note-3: -4.1. Vascular tissue is comprised of the xylem and the phloem, the main transport systems of plants. They typically occur together in vascular bundles in all plant organs, traversing roots, stems, and leaves. Xylem is responsible for the transport of water and dissolved ions from the roots upwards through the plant.

Concept note-4: -Xylem is the complex tissue of plants, responsible for transporting water and other nutrients to the plants. Phloem is living tissue, responsible for transporting food and other organic materials.

Concept note-5: -Xylem and phloem are called vascular or conducting tissues because both xylem and phloem tissues transport materials in plants, they are together called vascular tissues.
